# Calorie Tracking Tactics

**Source:** The Circle Diet (2021) by Greg Doucette
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04

## Summary

Doucette provides practical methods for tracking calories and body weight to manage energy balance. His approach prioritizes simplicity over precision, recognizing that perfect accuracy is impossible but directional accuracy is sufficient for results.

## Weight Tracking Protocol

### Daily Weigh-In
1. Weigh yourself naked every morning
2. Use the bathroom first
3. Same scale, same spot
4. Record the number immediately

### Weekly Median Method
Rather than averaging (which can be skewed by outliers), use the **median**:

1. Collect 5-7 daily weights
2. Arrange from lowest to highest
3. Remove highest 3 and lowest 3 (if 7 days)
4. Middle number = weekly median

**Example:**
| Day | Weight |
|-----|--------|
| Sun | 153 |
| Mon | 149 |
| Tue | 147 |
| Wed | 150 |
| Thu | 149 |
| Fri | 147 |
| Sat | 151 |

Sorted: 147, 147, 149, **149**, 150, 151, 153
Median = **149 lbs**

### Why Median vs Average
> "By using the median instead of the average, we are getting rid of outliers in data, allowing you to paint the best picture of your true morning weight."

Weight fluctuates due to:
- Water retention
- Fiber intake
- Menstrual cycle
- Stress
- Sleep quality
- Sodium intake

## Food Tracking

### Required Tools
- Food scale (measure in grams)
- Tracking app (MyFitnessPal) or notebook
- Label reading habit

### Best Practices

> "Buy a food scale. Weigh your food in grams whenever possible."

1. **Weigh in grams** - cups/volume measurements are unreliable
2. **Weigh before plating** - or during meal prep
3. **Consider cooked vs raw** - water loss changes weight
4. **Track EVERYTHING** - sauces, oils, condiments add up fast

### Label Accuracy Warning
> "Labels LIE - they are allowed to be off by 20%!"

> "Most people underreport calorie intake by 30% and even as high as 50%."

Common tracking failures:
- Forgetting cooking oils/butter
- Estimating vs weighing
- "BLTs" (Bites, Licks, Tastes)
- Weekend alcohol not counted

## Starting Calorie Formula

For those who don't want to track existing intake:

### Formula
| Sex | Formula |
|-----|---------|
| Female | Body weight (lbs) × 10 |
| Male | Body weight (lbs) × 10 × 1.1 |

### Examples
| Weight | Sex | Starting Calories |
|--------|-----|-------------------|
| 150 lbs | Female | 1500 |
| 200 lbs | Female | 2000 |
| 200 lbs | Male | 2200 |
| 250 lbs | Male | 2750 |

## Adjustment Protocol

### When to Adjust
Wait **3 weeks** of consistent tracking before making changes.

### Decision Framework
| Weight Trend | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| Going down 0.5-1%/week | Continue (on track) |
| Staying same | Reduce by 250 cal (F) or 375 cal (M) |
| Going up | Reduce further or add cardio |

### Adjustment Magnitude
- Small adjustments (100-250 cal) work better long-term
- Can adjust calories OR cardio
- Or combination of both

## Tracking Graduation

### Long-Term Goal
> "You do not need to count calories for the rest of your life."

**Progression:**
1. **Phase 1:** Track everything meticulously
2. **Phase 2:** Track with known meals (memorize common foods)
3. **Phase 3:** Mental tracking only
4. **Phase 4:** Mirror/scale feedback + intuitive eating

### Doucette's Personal Approach
> "I personally no longer track my calories. But I can instinctively guess how many calories I've consumed in a meal or a day with high precision from having done so in the past."

## Weekly Check-In Routine

```
Monday:
[ ] Calculate last week's median weight
[ ] Compare to previous 2 weeks
[ ] Assess if changes needed
[ ] Plan any adjustments for coming week
```
