# Satiety Strategies (Minimize Suffering)

**Source:** The Circle Diet (2021) by Greg Doucette
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04

## Summary

Spoke #2 of the Circle Diet focuses on making dieting sustainable by minimizing hunger and suffering. Doucette emphasizes that the key to diet success is not willpower but building habits around foods that satisfy hunger while remaining within caloric limits. These tactics form the practical implementation of the "Minimize Suffering" spoke.

## Core Philosophy

> "Imagine liking your diet and imagine losing weight WITHOUT SUFFERING!"

> "You need just enough willpower to be willing to change your current set of habits."

## Protein Prioritization

### Why Protein Works
1. **Satiety** - Most filling macronutrient
2. **High TEF** - Burns more calories during digestion
3. **Muscle preservation** - Critical during deficit
4. **Doesn't trigger overeating** - Unlike fat+carb combinations

### Protein Guidelines
- Eat protein at **4-5 meals per day** (minimum 3)
- Target: **0.8-1g per pound body weight**
- Protein is "the nucleus of your meal"

### Best Protein Sources
- Egg whites
- Protein powder
- Chicken breast
- White fish
- Lean beef cuts

## Fiber Strategy

### Benefits of Fiber
1. Lowers cholesterol
2. Reduces heart disease risk
3. Maintains bowel health
4. Controls blood sugar
5. Reduces cancer risk
6. **Aids satiety**
7. **Adds volume to meals**
8. Keeps you regular

### Calorie Content
| Type | Calories/gram |
|------|---------------|
| Soluble fiber | ~2 cal |
| Insoluble fiber | 0 cal |

### Fiber Techniques
> "Guar and xanthan gum" - volume expansion agents

- Soluble fiber absorbs water and expands
- Creates bulk and slows nutrient absorption
- More stable blood glucose levels

## Low Calorie-Dense Foods

### The Principle
Eat foods with low calories per gram to maximize volume while staying in deficit.

### Food Density Comparison
| Food Type | Calorie Density |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Vegetables | Very low |
| Fruits | Low |
| Lean protein | Moderate |
| Whole grains | Moderate |
| Fats/oils | Very high |
| Processed snacks | Very high |

### Volume Eating
> "You can eat all the food you want, so long as it's timed correctly" - This is WRONG thinking

The RIGHT approach: Eat large volumes of low-calorie foods, not restricted portions of high-calorie foods.

## Foods to Avoid (High Fat + High Sugar)

> "Fats, especially when combined with carbs, make foods highly palatable while not being very filling, making a very dangerous combination and extremely difficult to put the fork down."

**Problem foods:**
- Ice cream
- Cookies
- Pastries
- Most desserts
- Fried foods
- Pizza

These trigger overeating because they're:
- Highly palatable
- Not filling relative to calories
- Designed to be moreish

## Cooking Fat Awareness

> "That 100-calorie veggie stir-fry can easily become 500 calories with the added oil. That 300-calorie salad can become 800 calories."

### Fat Math
| Addition | Calories Added |
|----------|----------------|
| 1 tbsp oil | ~120 cal |
| 1 tbsp butter | ~100 cal |
| 2 tbsp salad dressing | ~150-200 cal |
| 1/4 cup cheese | ~110 cal |

## Alcohol Considerations

> "Don't forget to include the calories from your night out at the bar! You may have had 2000 calories on Friday, but also 1000 extra calories from alcohol."

- Alcohol: **7 calories/gram**
- Beer/wine have carbs too
- Mixers add more calories
- "Skinny" vodka is marketing (just less alcohol)
- Low-carb beer actually does have fewer calories

## Practical Meal Building

### Meal Template
1. **Base:** Large portion of vegetables
2. **Protein:** Lean protein source
3. **Carbs:** Moderate portion of whole grains/fruit
4. **Fat:** Minimal added fats

### Example Meal
- 2 cups vegetables (50 cal)
- 6 oz chicken breast (200 cal)
- 1 cup rice (200 cal)
- Minimal oil spray (20 cal)
= **470 calories** for a large, filling meal

## The Anabolic Kitchen Philosophy

Doucette's cookbooks apply these principles:
- High-volume recipes
- Protein-forward meals
- Low calorie-dense substitutions
- Sugar-free sweeteners
- Air frying over oil frying
