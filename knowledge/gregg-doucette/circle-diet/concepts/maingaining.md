# Maingaining

**Source:** The Circle Diet (2021) by Greg Doucette
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** maingain, maintaining-gaining, body recomposition

## Summary

Maingaining is Greg Doucette's term for maintaining body fat percentage while slowly building muscle over time. It represents the "end-game for dieting" - the sustainable state where you no longer need to diet but continue improving body composition. Unlike traditional bulk/cut cycles, maingaining avoids the extremes and psychological toll of alternating surplus and deficit phases.

## Definition

> "If after a body fat self-assessment you've determined that you are in a healthy body fat percentage range, you should 'maingain.' I am a huge fan of the 'maingaining' approach as the end-game for dieting."

**Core principle:** Maintain body fat percentage while body weight slowly increases from muscle gain.

## Prerequisites

To begin maingaining, you must first reach a healthy body fat percentage:
- **Males:** 10-20% body fat (15% optimal)
- **Females:** 20-30% body fat (25% optimal)

If above these ranges → diet first, then maingain
If below these ranges → small surplus to reach healthy range, then maingain

## How It Works

### Calorie Balance
- Eat at or slightly above maintenance (~30 calories surplus)
- Body weight will slowly increase (muscle)
- Body fat percentage stays the same

### Target Rate
- **~0.5 lbs per month** body weight increase
- If gaining faster → likely adding fat, reduce calories
- If gaining slower → may need slightly more food

### Adjustments
> "You can always make adjustments to your calories or cardio for a week or two as needed, to scale back slightly to a ~250-calorie deficit if you start to notice you're gaining a little too much body fat."

## The Case Against Bulking

Doucette argues strongly against traditional bulking:

> "I almost never recommend that someone put themselves in a significant caloric surplus, or what most people refer to as a 'bulk.'"

**Why bulking is problematic:**
1. Muscle doesn't grow fast enough to justify large surplus
2. Only need ~30 calories/day surplus to build muscle
3. Excess calories stored as fat
4. Creates endless bulk/cut cycle
5. Psychological toll of constant extremes

> "If you eat big to get big, it will work, but you will get big and fat (and not the kind of big you were likely hoping for)."

## When Bulking IS Appropriate

The only times Doucette recommends a caloric surplus:
- Underweight individuals
- Not enough body fat for health
- Lack energy due to caloric restriction
- Just finished a bodybuilding show and need to recover

## Muscle Building While Maingaining

> "You can also build muscle in a caloric deficit. (Not as much as in a caloric surplus, but it can be done)."

**Requirements for muscle building:**
1. Protein (adequate intake)
2. Water (urinating every 2-3 hours = adequate)
3. Energy (from food OR fat stores)
4. Progressive overload training

> "A caloric surplus is NOT needed to build muscle."

## Expected Muscle Gains (Natural)

| Year | Good Genetics | Average/Women |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| 1 | 8-12 lbs | 4-6 lbs |
| 2 | 6-8 lbs | 3-4 lbs |
| 3 | 4-6 lbs | 2-3 lbs |
| 4+ | Progressively less | Progressively less |

This slow rate is why aggressive bulking doesn't make sense - you can't force muscle growth faster.

## Key Quotes

> "This is why I consider maingaining to be the end-game for dieting. You will get to very slowly increase calories over time, while gaining muscle and not gaining body fat."

> "Once you are maingaining, the goal isn't to remain the same body weight. The goal is to gain muscle without putting on fat. Which means your body weight will go UP over time."

> "Maingaining is for everyone!!!"
