# Calorie Balance (CICO)

**Source:** The Circle Diet (2021) by Greg Doucette
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** CICO, Calories In Calories Out, Energy Balance

## Summary

Calorie balance is the foundational principle of the Circle Diet, derived directly from the First Law of Thermodynamics. Doucette is emphatic that no diet can circumvent this law - weight change is determined entirely by the relationship between calories consumed and calories expended. This principle underlies every other aspect of the Circle Diet.

## The First Law of Thermodynamics

> "The First Law of Thermodynamics states that while energy can be converted from one form to another, energy itself CANNOT be created or destroyed."

> "No one can break the first law of thermodynamics. I repeat - NO ONE. (Not even those moronic YouTubers or TikTokers who try to tell you otherwise)."

## The Equation

**Calories IN** (food and drinks) vs **Calories OUT** (BMR + TEF + NEAT + Exercise)

| State | Condition | Result |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Surplus** | Calories IN > Calories OUT | Weight gain |
| **Deficit** | Calories OUT > Calories IN | Weight loss |
| **Maintenance** | Calories IN = Calories OUT | No change |

## Calories OUT Components

### 1. BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)
> "The total amount of calories your body burns per day just by being alive, as if you simply laid in bed for 24 hours."

- Supports brain function, heartbeat, organ function
- Changes with body weight and muscle mass
- BMR calculators are estimates only

### 2. TEF (Thermic Effect of Food)
> "It takes less energy to convert fat you eat into stored body fat than it takes to convert protein or fiber you eat into stored body fat."

- Protein has highest TEF
- Fiber has high TEF
- Fat has lowest TEF

### 3. NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis)
> "All the calories you burn in a day that are NOT from existing or exercising."

- Typing, fidgeting, walking around house
- Can vary by 500+ calories between individuals
- Body may reduce NEAT when in prolonged deficit

### 4. Exercise
- Cardio burns calories during activity
- Resistance training builds muscle (increases BMR)
- Improvements in fitness = more calories burned

## Average Calorie Balance

> "Your body weight reflects average calorie balance over time."

Key insight: Single days don't matter - weekly/monthly average determines weight change.

**Example:**
- 6 days at 1500-cal deficit + 1 day at 5500-cal surplus = maintenance, not deficit
- This is why weekend binges can erase weekday deficits

### The Math
- 3500 calories = 1 pound of body fat
- 500 cal/day deficit = ~1 lb/week loss
- 250 cal/day deficit = ~0.5 lb/week loss

## Dynamic Nature

> "The amount of calories you need to consume to lose weight can CHANGE over time."

Factors that change caloric needs:
- Changes in body weight
- Changes in muscle mass
- Changes in activity level
- Changes in NEAT (can decrease with prolonged deficit)
- Changes in exercise efficiency

**Recommendation:** Monitor weight for 3 weeks before making calorie adjustments.

## Tracking Recommendations

### Weight Tracking
- Weigh naked every morning after bathroom
- Track 5-7 days per week
- Use **median** (not average) to remove outliers
- Compare weekly medians over 3+ weeks

### Calorie Tracking
- Use food scale (measure in grams)
- Track everything (including sauces, cooking oil)
- Labels can be off by 20%
- Most people underreport by 30-50%

> "Most people underreport calorie intake by 30% and even as high as 50%."

### Starting Calorie Formula
- **Female:** Body weight (lbs) × 10 = starting calories
- **Male:** Body weight (lbs) × 10 × 1.1 = starting calories

Example:
- 150 lb female → 1500 calories
- 200 lb male → 2200 calories

## Long-Term Tracking

> "You do not need to count calories for the rest of your life. But, doing so at the beginning will help you learn about the calories in various foods."

Progression:
1. **Beginner:** Track everything in app/notebook
2. **Intermediate:** Mental tracking with periodic verification
3. **Advanced:** Intuitive eating with mirror feedback

- Surplus → weight gain
