# The Circle Diet - Index

**Author:** Greg Doucette
**Publisher:** Coach Greg Inc.
**Year:** 2021
**Focus:** Sustainable forever-diet for lifelong weight management

## Overview

The Circle Diet is a lifestyle-based approach to nutrition centered on the concept of inclusion rather than exclusion. The name references a bicycle wheel with spokes - each spoke representing a key principle that supports sustainable weight management. Doucette emphasizes this is the "last diet you'll ever start" - a forever diet, not a temporary fix.

## The 4 Spokes Framework

| Spoke | Name | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| 1 | **Calories In, Calories Out** | Foundation - First Law of Thermodynamics, calorie balance |
| 2 | **Minimize Suffering** | Sustainability - habits over willpower, satiety strategies |
| 3 | **Move Your Ass** | Cardio - 150 min/week minimum, improve over time |
| 4 | **Maximize Muscle** | Resistance training - builds metabolically active tissue |

## Key Principles

### 1. Forever Diet Philosophy
> "Dieting is not about temporary fixes that have a start and finish. The diet you follow should be one you can maintain for the rest of your life."

> "95% of diets fail! Why? Because they are not sustainable."

### 2. CICO is Non-Negotiable
> "No one can break the first law of thermodynamics. I repeat - NO ONE."

### 3. Habits Over Willpower
> "You need just enough willpower to be willing to change your current set of habits."

### 4. Maingaining as End-Game
> "I am a huge fan of the 'maingaining' approach as the end-game for dieting."

## 5 Prerequisites for a Forever Diet

1. **Honesty** - Being truthful with yourself about goals
2. **Habits** - Daily actions with diet and exercise
3. **Tools** - Physical things needed to carry out habits
4. **Organization** - Systems that maintain habits in any situation
5. **Motivation** - "HARDER THAN LAST TIME" mentality

## Calorie Guidelines

### Starting Calorie Formula
- **Female**: Add a zero to body weight (lbs) = starting calories
- **Male**: Add a zero to body weight + 10% = starting calories

Example:
- 150 lb female → 1500 calories
- 200 lb male → 2200 calories

### Weight Loss Rate
- Target: 0.5-1% body weight per week
- 3500 calories = 1 pound of body fat
- 500 cal/day deficit = ~1 lb/week loss

## Macronutrient Position

Unlike RP's detailed macro tracking, Doucette takes a simpler approach:
> "NO!!!! You don't need to precisely count your macronutrient intake to change your body weight."

**Rough Guidelines:**
- Track total calories (not macros)
- Protein at most meals (preferably 5x/day)
- Healthy fats 3x/week minimum
- Fiber at most meals
- Avoid high fat + high sugar combinations

### Macros Quick Reference
| Macro | Calories/gram | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| Protein | 4 cal | High thermic effect, most satiating |
| Carbs | 4 cal | Fuel for training, not evil |
| Fat | 9 cal | Essential but calorie-dense |
| Fiber (soluble) | 2 cal | Volume, satiety |
| Fiber (insoluble) | 0 cal | Passes through |
| Alcohol | 7 cal | Include in tracking! |
