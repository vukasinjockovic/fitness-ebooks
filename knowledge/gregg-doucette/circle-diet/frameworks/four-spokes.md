# The Four Spokes Framework

**Source:** The Circle Diet (2021) by Greg Doucette
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04

## Summary

The Four Spokes Framework is the core organizational structure of The Circle Diet. Like spokes on a bicycle wheel that "add strength and help transfer power," these four principles work together to support sustainable weight management. The framework prioritizes simplicity over the detailed hierarchies found in academic nutrition texts.

## The Four Spokes

### Spoke #1: Calories In, Calories Out (CICO)
**Foundation of the diet**

> "This is the foundation of the Circle Diet."

- First Law of Thermodynamics - energy cannot be created or destroyed
- Calorie surplus → weight gain
- Calorie deficit → weight loss
- Average calorie balance over time matters, not single days

**Key equation:**
- Calories IN (food) vs Calories OUT (TEF + Exercise + NEAT + BMR)

### Spoke #2: Minimize Suffering
**Sustainability mechanism**

> "Notice I said 'minimize' rather than 'eliminate' or 'erase.' But you won't need to die trying to reach your weight loss goals."

- Habits over willpower
- Satiety strategies (protein, fiber, volume)
- Low calorie-dense foods
- Making diet enjoyable enough to sustain

### Spoke #3: Move Your Ass
**Cardiovascular exercise**

> "Adding movement into the equation can allow for more flexibility in caloric intake."

- Minimum 150 minutes/week moderate-intensity cardio
- Improves "calories out" side of equation
- Get better over time → burn more calories
- "HARDER THAN LAST TIME" principle

### Spoke #4: Maximize Muscle
**Resistance training**

> "The more muscle you have, the more calories you will burn, and the faster your metabolism will be."

- Weight training builds metabolically active tissue
- More muscle = higher BMR
- Progressive overload
- Supports long-term metabolic health

## Visual Metaphor

The bicycle wheel analogy:
- **Circle** = diet has no beginning or end (lifestyle)
- **Spokes** = support, stability, carry weight
- All spokes needed for wheel to function

> "Spokes add strength and help transfer power to the wheel. Spokes provide support and stability. Spokes allow wheels to simultaneously carry weight AND move that weight."

## Unique Aspects

### Integration of Exercise
Unlike RP and Helms who treat training separately, Doucette integrates cardio and resistance training as core "spokes" of the diet framework itself.

### Simplicity Over Detail
> "You don't need to precisely count your macronutrient intake to change your body weight."

This contrasts sharply with RP's detailed macro percentages and Helms' multi-tiered tracking systems.

### Equal Weighting (Implicit)
While CICO is called the "foundation," the spoke metaphor implies all four are necessary for the wheel to work. RP explicitly assigns percentages (60% calories, 20% composition, etc.).

## Key Quotes

> "My goal is for you to transform what you learn in this book into a lifestyle that works for you that you will never forget and be able to use for the rest of your life."
