# Muscle Memory (Myonuclei Persistence)

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** myonuclear domain theory, detrainee advantage, muscle regain, myonuclei persistence

## Summary

Muscle memory refers to the phenomenon where previously trained individuals can regain lost muscle much faster than it took to build originally. This is explained by the persistence of myonuclei—the "control centers" within muscle fibers that regulate protein synthesis. Once created through training, these nuclei remain even after muscle atrophy, allowing rapid regrowth when training resumes.

## The Mechanism

1. **Training builds myonuclei:** Weight training causes muscle fibers to grow and increases the number of nuclei (myonuclei) in the muscle
2. **Myonuclei persist during detraining:** When you stop training and lose muscle size, the myonuclei remain
3. **Rapid regrowth:** When training resumes, the preserved myonuclei can immediately start producing muscle proteins, allowing much faster growth than starting from scratch

> "You can think of myonuclei as the control centers inside the muscle fiber that command the production of new muscle. When we get back to training again, myonuclei that were built months or years ago can now start cranking out commands to ramp up the production of new muscle much faster."

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.5

## Practical Example

The book uses a "polar bear" analogy:
- A 900 lb polar bear loses significant weight during hibernation (inactivity + starvation)
- Once eating/moving normally, it rapidly regains mass
- Years to reach full size originally, but months to regain after loss

**Human parallel:**
- 10 years to build near-genetic-potential muscle
- Abandon training for 1-2 years → lose most muscle
- Resume training → regain in just a few months (not 10 years!)

## Implications for Recomposition

**The detrainee is uniquely positioned for body recomposition:**
- Treated more like a beginner in terms of potential rapid gains
- Can often achieve impressive recomposition in short timeframes
- Starting recommendation: maintenance calories (unless body comp suggests deficit/surplus)

## Calorie Recommendations for Detrained Individuals

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Weight lower than trained state | +20% surplus |
| Weight similar, composition regressed | Maintenance |
| Weight higher than trained state | -20% deficit |
