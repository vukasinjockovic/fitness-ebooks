# Thermic Effect of Food (TEF)

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** dietary thermogenesis, TEF, thermic effect, diet-induced thermogenesis

## Summary

The Thermic Effect of Food (TEF) is the energy expended by the body to digest, absorb, transport, and store food. Different macronutrients require different amounts of energy to process, with protein having the highest TEF—one reason high-protein diets support better body composition even when calories are equated.

## TEF by Macronutrient

| Macronutrient | Calories/gram | TEF Ranking | Approx TEF % |
|---------------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Protein | ~4 | **Highest** | 20-30% |
| Carbohydrates | ~4 | Medium | 5-10% |
| Fat | ~9 | **Lowest** | 0-3% |

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.4

## Practical Implications

### 1. "A calorie is NOT just a calorie"
> "This fact serves as another example of why the expression 'a calorie is a calorie' fails to capture the more complex and nuanced ways in which each of the different macronutrients are handled by our bodies."

### 2. High-protein diets burn more calories
Eating more protein means more calories spent on digestion itself, contributing to better fat loss results even at equal total calorie intake.

### 3. Food form matters within macros

**Example:** Complex vs Simple Carbohydrates
- 200g sweet potato and 43g Gatorade both deliver ~40g carbs
- Sweet potato has higher TEF (more processing required)
- Neither is "good" or "bad"—context determines suitability

## TEF as Part of TDEE

TEF typically accounts for ~10% of total daily energy expenditure, but this can shift based on:
- Macronutrient composition
- Food processing level
- Meal size and frequency

## Key Quotes

> "Protein is the most thermogenic macronutrient. This means eating a higher protein diet will result in more calories burned because it requires more energy to digest and absorb that protein."

> "Your body will expend more energy digesting and absorbing the carbohydrates from the sweet potato than it will the Gatorade."
