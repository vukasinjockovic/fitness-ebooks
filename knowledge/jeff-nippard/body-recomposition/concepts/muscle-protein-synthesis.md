# Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS)

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** MPS, protein synthesis, building muscle protein, anabolism

## Summary

Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS) is the process of building new muscle protein—the fundamental mechanism of muscle growth. All nine essential amino acids must be present for MPS to occur, making protein quality and distribution critical factors for optimizing body recomposition.

## The Building Blocks

MPS requires all **nine essential amino acids** (EAAs):

| Essential (Must Consume) | Non-Essential (Body Can Make) |
|-------------------------|------------------------------|
| Histidine | Arginine |
| Isoleucine* | Alanine |
| **Leucine*** | Asparagine |
| Lysine | Aspartic Acid |
| Methionine | Cysteine |
| Phenylalanine | Glutamic Acid |
| Threonine | Glutamine |
| Tryptophan | Glycine |
| Valine* | Proline |
| | Serine |
| | Tyrosine |

*Branched-chain amino acids (BCAAs)
**Leucine is particularly important as the "trigger" for MPS

> "The same way we cannot build a brick house without bricks, we also cannot build muscle without essential amino acids."

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.9

## Protein Quality

Not all proteins are created equal. **Complete proteins** provide all nine EAAs.

Quality factors:
1. **Amino acid profile** — Does it have all EAAs?
2. **Leucine content** — Higher = stronger MPS trigger
3. **Digestibility** — How much is actually absorbed?

### Ranking (Leucine Content & Quality)
1. **Whey protein** — Highest leucine, fastest absorption
2. **Egg protein** — Complete, highly bioavailable
3. **Casein protein** — Slow-release, good before bed
4. **Soy protein** — Best plant-based option
5. **Pea/rice protein** — Combine for complete profile

## Practical Implications

### Hitting Daily Targets is Priority #1
> "While we would agree that hitting your daily protein target is the most important factor, it may not be enough to optimize progress on its own."

### Optimization Beyond Totals
1. **Protein quality** — Prioritize complete proteins
2. **Protein timing** — Distribute throughout day
3. **Protein distribution** — ~20-40g per meal to maximize MPS per feeding

## Key Quotes
