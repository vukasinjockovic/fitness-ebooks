# NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis)

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** non-exercise activity, daily movement, fidgeting, spontaneous physical activity

## Summary

NEAT represents all calories burned through daily activities that aren't formal exercise—typing, walking, fidgeting, carrying groceries, etc. It's a highly variable component of energy expenditure that can explain massive differences in metabolic rate between similar individuals and is largely responsible for why some people can eat significantly more without gaining fat.

## What NEAT Includes

- Walking to the car
- Typing at your desk
- Bringing groceries inside
- Taking stairs
- Fidgeting
- Standing vs sitting
- Dog walking
- All non-exercise movement

**Excluded:** Formal exercise (lifting, cardio sessions)

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.4

## Why NEAT Matters for Recomp

### High individual variance
> "Did you ever wonder why your friend who is exactly the same age, weight and bodyfat as you can eat 1,000 calories more than you and still stay just as lean? It's probably largely because he or she has higher levels of NEAT."

### Beyond calorie burning
NEAT also improves:
- Insulin sensitivity
- Carbohydrate tolerance

### Job-related differences
Massive TDEE gap between:
- Construction worker (high NEAT)
- Desk job worker (low NEAT)

## NEAT and Dieting

**Critical warning:** When in caloric deficit, the body automatically downregulates NEAT.

Effects include:
- Less fidgeting
- Less spontaneous movement
- Severe fatigue (if dieting too hard/long)

> "This is a key driver of the slowed metabolism that you often see with chronic dieters and is one of the reasons why throughout the book, we recommend more moderate caloric deficits and surpluses."

## Practical Recommendations

For those with sedentary lifestyles and fat loss goals:
- Take daily walks (10,000+ steps)
- Use stairs instead of elevators
- Park farther away
- Standing desk
- Walk during phone calls

**Key insight:** NEAT interventions can be more sustainable than formal cardio for increasing energy expenditure.

## Key Quotes

> "High levels of NEAT is advantageous from a fat loss perspective and is believed to be largely responsible for the massive differences in total metabolic rate we see."

> "When in a caloric deficit for an extended period of time, your body senses the decline in food intake and automatically decreases energy expenditure by downregulating NEAT. This can result in something as simple as fidgeting less, but can also present as severe fatigue."
