# Body Recomposition Framework

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** recomp, simultaneous fat loss and muscle gain, body recomp

## Summary

Body recomposition is the process of building muscle while simultaneously losing fat. Unlike traditional bulking/cutting cycles, recomposition requires optimizing multiple variables together: calorie intake, macronutrient distribution, training stimulus, and recovery. The framework provides a decision matrix based on primary goal, current body composition, and training experience.

## Key Principles

1. **Weight training is the engine, nutrition is the fuel**
   - You cannot build appreciable muscle without training stimulus
   - Optimal nutrition is "permissive" to training, not a replacement
   — Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.15

2. **Calorie intake depends on three factors**
   - Primary goal (fat loss vs muscle gain priority)
   - Current body composition (body fat percentage)
   - Training experience (beginner → intermediate → advanced)
   — Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.5

3. **Not all weight change is created equal**
   - Weight loss ≠ fat loss
   - Sleep-restricted subjects lost 80% weight from lean mass vs 17% for normal sleepers
   — Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.14

## The Decision Matrix

| Primary Goal | Experience | Body Composition | Calorie Adjustment |
|-------------|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Build Muscle | Beginner | Low BF (8-12% M / 18-22% F) | +25% surplus |
| Build Muscle | Intermediate | Low BF | +15-20% surplus |
| Build Muscle | Advanced | Low BF | +10-15% surplus |
| Lose Fat | Any | High BF (18%+ M / 28%+ F) | -20% deficit |
| Both Equal | Any | Moderate BF (12-18% M / 22-28% F) | Maintenance |
| Both Equal | Advanced | Moderate BF | ±5-10% based on lean/gain priority |

## Key Quotes

> "When we say optimize, we mean that we are valuing the most effective approach over the most time-efficient approach."

> "Measuring body recomposition is a bit more tricky... the body weight scale can be difficult to interpret and isn't going to be a sufficient tool on its own."

> "It is easy to lose fat by using diet alone, but nearly impossible to build muscle without weight training."
