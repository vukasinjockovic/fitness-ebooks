# Supplement Tier System

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** supplement hierarchy, evidence-based supplements, supplement recommendations

## Summary

A four-tier classification system for supplements based on safety, efficacy (quality/quantity of research + practical benefit), and cost-effectiveness. Higher tiers represent supplements with stronger evidence, better safety profiles, and greater practical impact on body recomposition.

## Three Evaluation Criteria

1. **Safety** - Risk of negative side effects at appropriate dosages (all listed supplements pass this)
2. **Efficacy** - Does it work? Quality/quantity of research + magnitude of effect
3. **Cost** - Is the benefit worth the price?

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.13

## Tier 1: Foundational (Recommended for Most)

| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Protein Powder** | As needed to hit targets | Whey, casein, or vegan (soy/pea/rice) |
| **Creatine Monohydrate** | 5g/day (~1 tsp) | Most studied supplement; strength, power, muscle hydration |
| **Caffeine** | 1-2 mg/kg (alertness/cardio) or 3-6 mg/kg (max strength, 1-2x/week) | Build tolerance; avoid late day use |

### Creatine Details
- Loading phase (20g/day for 1 week) optional—reaches same levels after ~1 month either way
- Timing less important than consistency
- Slightly enhanced uptake post-workout with carbs/protein
- Neuroprotective properties noted

### Caffeine Details
- Higher doses for strength should be reserved for heaviest sessions
- Tolerance builds with frequent high-dose use
- Impacts sleep quality—use responsibly

## Tier 2: Conditional (Diet-Dependent)

| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Multivitamin** | As directed | Insurance for micronutrient gaps; quality varies |
| **Fish Oil (EPA + DHA)** | 2g combined EPA/DHA daily | Unnecessary if eating fatty fish 1-2x/week |

**Key insight:** RDI (Recommended Daily Intake) prevents disease but doesn't optimize health.

## Tier 3: Potentially Beneficial (Smaller Effects)

| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Green Tea / EGCG** | 125-250mg EGCG + 50mg caffeine | ~4% increased resting energy expenditure |
| **Ashwagandha (KSM-66)** | 500-1000mg/day | Reduces cortisol ~28%; take post-workout or before bed |
| **L-Citrulline** | 4-10g pre-workout | Increases blood flow, muscular endurance; cycle on/off |

### Ashwagandha Details
- Herbal adaptogen for stress/anxiety
- May improve strength, glucose uptake, testosterone
- KSM-66 strand most studied

### L-Citrulline Details
- More effective than L-Arginine at raising blood arginine
- Bypasses liver metabolism
- Body may upregulate arginase over time—cycle during deloads

## Tier 4: Context-Specific (Use with Caution)

| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Yohimbine HCL** | Start 2.5mg/50lbs, max 2.5mg/25lbs | For stubborn fat; take fasted or 2hr post low-GI meal |

### Yohimbine Details
- Blocks alpha receptors → more beta receptor binding → enhanced fat breakdown
- Targets stubborn fat (men: abs, women: hips)
- **Side effects:** Anxiety, increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure
- Stack suggestion: 1mg/kg caffeine + 250mg EGCG + 2.5mg/lb yohimbine

## Cheat Sheet

```
Tier 1 (Most recommended):
- Protein powder: As needed
- Creatine: 5g/day
- Caffeine: 1-6 mg/kg depending on goal

Tier 2 (Diet insurance):
- Multivitamin: As needed
- Fish oil: 2g EPA+DHA daily

Tier 3 (Optional extras):
- EGCG: 125-250mg + 50mg caffeine
- Ashwagandha: 500-1000mg
- L-Citrulline: 4-10g pre-workout

Tier 4 (Advanced/stubborn fat):
- Yohimbine: 2.5mg/25-50lbs (assess tolerance)
```
