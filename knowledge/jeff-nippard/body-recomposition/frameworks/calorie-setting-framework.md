# Calorie Setting Framework

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** calorie targets, how many calories, diet setup

## Summary

A systematic approach to determining calorie intake for body recomposition based on three primary factors: your goal priority, current body composition, and training experience. Rejects one-size-fits-all recommendations in favor of individualized targets expressed as percentages above/below maintenance.

## The Three Determining Factors

### 1. Primary Goal
Even when pursuing recomposition (both goals), decide which matters more:
- **Fat loss priority** → Favor deficit
- **Muscle gain priority** → Favor surplus
- **Both equal** → Start at maintenance

### 2. Current Body Composition
| Category | Men | Women |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Low BF | 8-12% | 18-22% |
| Moderate BF | 12-18% | 22-28% |
| High BF | 18-20%+ | 28-30%+ |

### 3. Training Experience
| Level | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| Beginner | Progress weekly, visual changes monthly (0-2 years) |
| Intermediate | Progress monthly, visual changes every few months (2-5 years) |
| Advanced | Multiple months/years to see progress (5+ years) |

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.5

## Calorie Target Matrix

| Goal | Experience | Body Comp | Calorie Adjustment |
|------|------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Build Muscle | Beginner | Low BF | **+25%** surplus |
| Build Muscle | Intermediate | Low BF | **+15-20%** surplus |
| Build Muscle | Advanced | Low BF | **+10-15%** surplus |
| Lose Fat | Any level | High BF | **-20%** deficit |
| Both Equal | Beginner-Int | Moderate BF | **Maintenance** |
| Both Equal | Advanced | Moderate BF | **±5-10%** based on lean preference |

**Why experience affects surplus but not deficit:**
Training age impacts the rate of muscle gain (diminishing returns) but does not diminish fat loss ability.

## Special Cases

### The Detrainee (Muscle Memory)
Previously trained individuals who lost muscle during time off:
- If weight is lower than trained state → +20% surplus
- If weight is similar but composition regressed → Maintenance
- If weight is higher than trained state → -20% deficit

### Genetic Outliers (Exception 1)
Signs you may need larger surplus (+5-10% more):
- Large frame (broad shoulders, thick wrists/ankles)
- Muscular parents/relatives
- Generally gifted at sports
- Notice quick muscle gains
- Men: High 2D:4D ratio (ring finger > index finger)

### Obese Individuals (Exception 2)
BF >25% (men) or >35% (women): Consider -20-30% deficit

## Two Methods to Estimate Maintenance

### Method 1: Mifflin St. Jeor Formula
See [Mifflin St. Jeor](../tactics/mifflin-st-jeor.md)

### Method 2: Guess-and-Check
1. Track bodyweight and calories for 2 weeks
2. Calculate averages for week 1 and week 2
3. If weight stable → current intake = maintenance
4. If lost 1 lb/week → add 500 cal to get maintenance
5. If gained 1 lb/week → subtract 500 cal to get maintenance

## Key Quotes

> "It's commonly suggested that if your goal is to lose one pound of fat per week, you should eat 500 calories below your maintenance... research indicates that this figure often overestimates predicted weight loss and undermines the adaptive nature of our metabolism."

> "The more advanced you are, the more likely it is that large caloric surpluses will lead to fat gain rather than muscle gain."
