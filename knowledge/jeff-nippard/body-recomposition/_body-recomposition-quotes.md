# Quotable Quotes from The Ultimate Guide to Body Recomposition

**Authors:** Jeff Nippard & Chris Barakat
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03

## On Optimization

> "There are no shortcuts to any place worth going."

> "When we say optimize, we mean that we are valuing the most effective approach over the most time-efficient approach."

> "A truly optimized nutritional approach cannot be simplified into a single soundbite."

## On Body Recomposition

> "Measuring body recomposition is a bit more tricky... the body weight scale can be difficult to interpret and isn't going to be a sufficient tool on its own."

> "It is easy to lose fat by using diet alone, but nearly impossible to build muscle without weight training."

> "Weight training can be considered the driving force of body recomposition."

> "Nutrition is permissive to weight training."

## On Energy Balance

> "The energy balance equation does a great job of predicting changes in weight loss, [but] it doesn't paint the full picture of what is possible in terms of body recomposition on its own."

> "If your goal is to simply lose weight, then you simply need to focus on eating fewer calories and/or burning more calories... But since you're pursuing body recomposition..."

## On Metabolism

> "Did you ever wonder why your friend who is exactly the same age, weight and bodyfat as you can eat 1,000 calories more than you and still stay just as lean? It's probably largely because he or she has higher levels of NEAT."

> "When in a caloric deficit for an extended period of time, your body senses the decline in food intake and automatically decreases energy expenditure by downregulating NEAT."

> "This is a key driver of the slowed metabolism that you often see with chronic dieters."

## On Protein

> "The same way we cannot build a brick house without bricks, we also cannot build muscle without essential amino acids."

> "Protein is the most thermogenic macronutrient."

## On Training Experience

> "The more training experience you have, the closer you will be to your natural genetic limitation for muscle mass and consequently, it will be more difficult for you to gain lean mass."

> "The more advanced you are, the more likely it is that large caloric surpluses will lead to fat gain rather than muscle gain."

## On Muscle Memory

> "Long before scientific research was able to explain it, bodybuilders talked about the phenomenon of 'muscle memory.' Basically, it's much easier to build muscle back than it was to build that muscle from scratch."

> "Thanks to those persistent myonuclei, they'd be able to regain that lost muscle mass in just a few short months, compared to the 10 years it took them to build it from ground zero!"

## On Progressive Overload

> "In its most basic form, progressive overload simply means doing more, over time."

> "Adding load to the bar will only lead to progressive tension overload if form and execution are kept consistent."

## On Supplements

> "Supplements are not required to improve body composition. They are, by definition, supplemental."

> "Investing more time, energy and money in improving your training and nutrition will positively impact your physique far more than any natural supplement ever could."

> "The international society of sports nutrition deemed creatine to be the safest and most effective ergogenic aid."

## On Sleep

> "Sleep is probably the most underappreciated and often neglected component of body recomposition."

> "Sleeping less will not only negatively impact your body recomposition goals in its own right, it will also make hitting your calorie and macro targets even more difficult by making you feel hungrier than usual."

> "Although there were no significant differences in total weight lost, there was an enormous difference in where that weight was lost from."

> "We challenge you to no longer think of your training as what happens in the gym. Consider sleep and the reduction of stress as part of the training regimen."

## On Health vs Optimization

> "There is a big difference between not being sick and being optimally healthy."

> "RDIs are values suggested to prevent disease but there is a big difference between not being sick and being optimally healthy."

## On Carb Cycling

> "The idea that you can somehow 'trick your body' or 'keep your metabolism guessing' by arbitrarily increasing and decreasing carb intakes throughout the week will not provide any extra metabolic benefits."

## On Individual Variation

> "Since there are a plethora of factors that can impact your individual calorie requirements, it's important to recognize that your calorie intake may not be comparable to someone else's, even if you are the same weight and have the same goals."

> "What is most optimal for you may not necessarily be most optimal for your gym buddy."
