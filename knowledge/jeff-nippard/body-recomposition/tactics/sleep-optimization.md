# Sleep Optimization for Body Recomposition

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** sleep hygiene, sleep for gains, recovery sleep, 8 hours sleep

## Summary

Sleep is the "dark horse" of body recomposition—critically important but often neglected. Research shows that even one hour less sleep per night can shift weight loss from 83% fat to 80% lean mass. The recommended target is 8-9 hours per night for optimal recomposition, with elite athletes often targeting 10+ hours.

## The Shocking Research

### Study 1: Wang et al. 2018
- 8-week fat loss diet
- Sleep-restricted group: 1 hour less sleep, 5 nights/week (weekend catch-up allowed)
- **Normal sleep:** 83% of weight lost was fat
- **Restricted sleep:** 80% of weight lost was lean mass

> "Although there were no significant differences in total weight lost, there was an enormous difference in where that weight was lost from."

### Study 2: 8.5 vs 5.5 Hours
- Both groups lost ~6.5 lbs
- 8.5 hour group: Most weight from fat
- 5.5 hour group: Only 1.3 lbs from fat (80% was lean mass!)
- Sleep-restricted group also had spiked ghrelin (hunger hormone)

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.14

## Why Sleep Affects Composition

Sleep restriction creates a catabolic hormonal environment:
- Higher cortisol
- Lower testosterone
- Lower IGF-1
- Lower myostatin regulation
- Higher ghrelin (hunger)

> "Collectively, these hormonal changes are positioned to rob you of muscle."

## Recommended Sleep Duration

| Population | Target |
|------------|--------|
| General adult | 7-9 hours |
| **Body recomp focus** | **8-9 hours** |
| Elite athletes (LeBron James) | 10-11 hours |

> "Based on our coaching experience and the body of scientific research, we recommend a slightly higher target of eight to nine hours of sleep per night to optimize progress."

## Sleep and Training Performance

### Sleep Deprivation Effects
- 3 hours/night for 3 days:
  - Significant strength loss on bench, deadlift, leg press
  - Increased perception of effort (same weight feels heavier)
  - Larger/complex movements more affected

### Sleep Extension Benefits
- Stanford swimmers with 10 hours sleep:
  - Faster sprint times
  - Improved reaction times
  - Quicker turns
- Stanford basketball with 10+ hours:
  - 9% improvement on free throws
  - 9.2% improvement on three-pointers

## Sleep Debt

> "One study found that just one hour of sleep debt can take four days of optimal sleep time to fully recover from."

**Implications:**
- Can't fully "catch up" over a weekend
- But some catch-up is better than none
- Naps can help

## Sleep Optimization Tips

### Environment
- Remove bright lights (computers, phones)
- Minimize irregular sounds
- Optimize room temperature
- Comfortable mattress and pillow
- No TV in bedroom (if it keeps you awake)

### Timing
- Consistent sleep/wake schedule
- Avoid caffeine after 5pm
- No alcohol before bed
- Exercise earlier, not too close to bed
- Relaxing pre-bed routine (bath, reading)

### Mental
- If worrying, write it down to address tomorrow
- Don't tackle stressful topics before sleep
- Meditation/mindfulness practice

## Stress Management

Stress compounds sleep issues. Recommendations:
- Ashwagandha supplementation (see [Supplement Tier System](../frameworks/supplement-tier-system.md))
- Meditation
- Flow-state activities
- Time with positive people
- Gratitude practice
- Exercise (but not too late)
