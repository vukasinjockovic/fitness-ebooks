# Logical Carb Cycling

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** carb cycling, non-linear carb intake, training day carbs, refeeds

## Summary

Logical carb cycling is a strategic approach to varying carbohydrate intake based on training demands rather than arbitrary schedules. Unlike traditional carb cycling which attempts to "trick metabolism," logical cycling aligns higher carbs with higher training demands for performance optimization while keeping weekly averages consistent with targets.

## Two Approaches

### 1. Linear Approach (No Cycling)
Same calories and macros every day.

**Best for:**
- Those in a caloric surplus (plenty of nutrients already)
- Those who prefer simplicity and consistency
- Those who find daily consistency easier to adhere to

> "When in a caloric surplus, it is of relatively less importance to vary your carbohydrate intake throughout the week."

### 2. Logical Carb Cycling (Non-Linear)
Vary carbs based on training demands.

**Best for:**
- Those in a deficit or at maintenance
- Those seeking to optimize performance on hard training days
- Those who benefit from psychological variety

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.5

## Why "Logical" Cycling?

Traditional carb cycling myths debunked:
> "The idea that you can somehow 'trick your body' or 'keep your metabolism guessing' by arbitrarily increasing and decreasing carb intakes throughout the week will not provide any extra metabolic benefits."

**Example:** High Monday, Moderate Tuesday, Low Wednesday has NO metabolic advantage over Moderate all three days (same weekly average).

**BUT:** Aligning high carbs with heavy training days HAS performance benefits.

## Implementation

### Higher Carb Days
- Heavy training days
- Competition/performance days
- Days with higher volume

### Moderate Carb Days
- Regular training days
- Active recovery days

### Lower Carb Days
- Rest days
- Light training days

**Key:** Weekly totals remain the same—just redistribute based on demand.

## Refeeds

A **refeed** is a 24-hour period of increased calories (usually via carbs).

**Purposes:**
1. Acutely improve training performance
2. Provide mental break from diet monotony
3. Acutely reverse negative hormonal adaptations (leptin)
4. Improve diet adherence

**When needed:**
- Very lean individuals
- Very low calorie intakes
- Extended dieting phases

> "Since our recommendations in this book are set toward achieving body recomposition, we are neither recommending very large caloric deficits nor very low end-goal body fat percentages. As such, scheduled weekly refeeds are not required."

## Practical Guidelines

**If in surplus:** Linear approach fine
**If at maintenance/deficit:**
- Consider logical cycling
- Higher carbs on hardest training days
- Lower carbs on rest/easy days
- Keep weekly averages on target

**Occasional free meals:**
> "Sometimes, the best approach from a psychological perspective is to simply enjoy the occasional 'free meal' or 'cheat night' and get back on track the next day."

## Key Quotes

> "We believe that, on the whole, carb-cycling has gotten more public attention than it deserves."

> "However, there may still be merit in cycling carb intake in a more logical fashion from a body recomposition perspective."
