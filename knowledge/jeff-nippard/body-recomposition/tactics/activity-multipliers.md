# Activity Multipliers

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** TDEE multiplier, activity factor, PAL (Physical Activity Level)

## Summary

Activity multipliers are scaling factors applied to BMR to estimate Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). They account for all physical activity including formal exercise and NEAT. Combined with the Mifflin St. Jeor formula, they provide maintenance calorie estimates for setting up recomposition diets.

## Activity Multiplier Table

| Lifestyle + Training | Example | Multiplier |
|---------------------|---------|------------|
| **Sedentary** + Training 3-6x/wk | Desk job, very little activity outside lifting | 1.2 - 1.5 |
| **Lightly Active** + Training 3-6x/wk | Desk job, walks dog most days + lifting | 1.5 - 1.8 |
| **Moderately Active** + Training 3-6x/wk | Full-time waitress, occasional tennis + lifting | 1.8 - 2.0 |
| **Highly Active** + Training 3-6x/wk | Construction worker, regular hiking + lifting | 2.0 - 2.2 |

— Source: Body Recomposition, Ch.5

## The Formula

```
Maintenance Calories = BMR × Activity Multiplier
```

## Example Calculation

**5'7" male, 150 lbs, desk job but walks dog most days:**

1. **Calculate BMR** (Mifflin St. Jeor):
   - BMR ≈ 1,639 calories

2. **Select multiplier:**
   - "Lightly active" → 1.6 (middle of 1.5-1.8 range)

3. **Calculate maintenance:**
   - 1,639 × 1.6 = **2,622 calories**

## How to Choose Your Multiplier

### Sedentary (1.2 - 1.5)
- Desk job
- Drive everywhere
- Little to no walking besides gym
- Low NEAT (fidgeting, standing, etc.)

### Lightly Active (1.5 - 1.8)
- Desk job BUT
- Regular walking (dog, commute, etc.)
- Some standing throughout day
- Moderate NEAT

### Moderately Active (1.8 - 2.0)
- On your feet at work (retail, server, teacher)
- Regular recreational activity
- Higher NEAT
- Active hobbies

### Highly Active (2.0 - 2.2)
- Physical labor job
- Very active lifestyle
- Multiple activities beyond gym
- Very high NEAT

## Tips for Selection

1. **When in doubt, go lower** — It's easier to add calories than lose unwanted fat
2. **Be honest** — Most desk workers overestimate activity
3. **Account for NEAT** — Fidgeters can be higher than expected
4. **Consider the range** — Start middle, adjust based on results
5. **Reassess periodically** — Activity levels change with seasons, job changes, etc.
