# Nutrition Fundamentals

**Type:** Concept (Layer 1 - Mechanism/Layer 2 - Pattern)
**Source:** The Muscle Ladder, Chapter 14
**Author:** Jeff Nippard (2024)

## The Hierarchy

Training drives muscle growth. Nutrition supports it.

> "Training is the number one driver of muscle growth. Focus first and foremost on making progress in the gym."

## Energy Balance (CICO)

**Calories In vs Calories Out** determines body weight changes:

| Goal | Energy Balance | Typical Deficit/Surplus |
|------|---------------|------------------------|
| Fat loss (cutting) | Deficit | -10% to -25% of maintenance |
| Muscle gain (bulking) | Surplus | +5% to +15% of maintenance |
| Body recomposition | Near maintenance | ±5% of maintenance |

## Calculating Maintenance Calories

**Formula:** Bodyweight (lb) × Activity Multiplier

| Activity Level | Multiplier |
|----------------|------------|
| Sedentary | 12-13 |
| Lightly active | 13-14 |
| Moderately active | 14-15 |
| Very active | 15-17 |

**Example:** 180 lb × 14 = 2,520 kcal maintenance

## Macronutrients

### Protein

| Goal | Protein Target | Example (190 lb) |
|------|---------------|------------------|
| Bulking | 0.7-1.0 g/lb (1.6-2.2 g/kg) | 133-190 g/day |
| Cutting | 0.8-1.2 g/lb (1.8-2.7 g/kg) | 152-228 g/day |
| Recomposition | 0.7-1.0 g/lb (1.6-2.2 g/kg) | 133-190 g/day |

**Notes:**
- Higher protein when cutting (preserves muscle)
- Leaner individuals → higher end of range
- High body fat → use goal weight, not current weight

### Fat

**Target:** 20-35% of total calories

**Importance:**
- Hormone function
- Energy source
- Essential fatty acids

**Calculation:**
```
2,200 kcal × 30% = 660 kcal from fat
660 ÷ 9 kcal/g = 73 g fat/day
```

### Carbohydrates

**Target:** Remaining calories after protein and fat

**Role:**
- Primary training fuel
- Glycogen replenishment
- Satiety and enjoyment

**Calculation:**
```
Total: 2,200 kcal
Protein: 180g × 4 = 720 kcal
Fat: 73g × 9 = 660 kcal
Carbs: 2,200 - 720 - 660 = 820 kcal ÷ 4 = 205g
```

### Fiber

| Daily Carb Intake | Recommended Fiber |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| <100g | ~20g |
| 100-200g | 20-30g |
| 200-300g | 30-40g |
| 300-400g | 40-50g |
| 400-500g | 50-60g |
| >500g | 60-70g |

## Flexibility in Tracking

> "The key is to find a meal pattern that allows you to hit your calorie and protein targets while minimizing hunger."

**Practical tolerances:**
- Calories: ±50-100 kcal of target
- Protein: ±10-20g of target
- Carbs/Fats: More flexible if calories and protein hit

## Refeeds (During Cuts)

**Definition:** Higher-calorie days during a diet (1-2 days/week)

**Benefits:**
- Psychological relief
- Glycogen replenishment
- Training energy

**Example (2,000 kcal avg target = 20% deficit):**
| Schedule | Deficit Days | Refeed Days |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| No refeed | 2,000 × 7 | — |
| 1 refeed | 1,875 × 6 | 2,750 × 1 |
| 2 refeeds | 1,750 × 5 | 2,750 × 2 |

## Hydration

**Guideline:** Drink when thirsty (body regulates well)

**Check:** Urine color should be light (lemonade, not apple juice)

**Note:** 3% dehydration significantly reduces strength and reps

## Alcohol

**Impact:**
- 7 kcal/g with no nutritive value
- Impairs sleep → impairs training
- Reduces muscle protein synthesis
- Loosens dietary inhibitions

**Recommendation:** Limit to 1-2 drinks/day max (on drinking days)

## Meal Frequency

### For Fat Loss
- Number of meals doesn't matter for results
- Choose pattern that controls hunger best
- 1-2 meals (IF) works for some
- 4-6 meals works for others

### For Muscle Gain
- Sweet spot: 3-6 meals/day
- Each meal: ~30g protein
- 1-2 meals can work if protein target hit
- Training matters more than meal timing

## Peri-Workout Nutrition

### Pre-Workout (Most Important)
**Goals:**
1. Prevent hunger during training
2. Avoid bloating
3. Fit your routine

**Recommendations:**
- 1-3 hours before training
- 0.5-1g carbs/kg bodyweight (40-80g for 80kg person)
- 0.3g protein/kg bodyweight (~24g for 80kg person)
- Limit fat to ~20-25g (can cause sluggishness)

### Intra-Workout
Generally unnecessary unless:
- Session >60-90 minutes
- Extremely lean (end of cut)
- Training fasted and energy crashes

**If needed:** 15-30g simple carbs

### Post-Workout
- Anabolic window is overblown
- 3-6 hours between pre and post meals is fine
- 0.3-0.4g protein/kg bodyweight (25-35g for 80kg)
- Carbs: Not urgent (24 hours to replenish glycogen)
- Fat: Personal preference

## Supplements

**Useful:**
- Creatine monohydrate (5g/day)
- Protein powder (convenience)
- Fish/algae oil (if not eating fatty fish 2x/week)
- Vitamin D (if deficient/low sun exposure)
- Caffeine (performance, optional)

**Not essential but potentially useful:**
- Beta-alanine
- Citrulline

**Overhyped:**
- Most "muscle builders"
- Fat burners
- Testosterone boosters
