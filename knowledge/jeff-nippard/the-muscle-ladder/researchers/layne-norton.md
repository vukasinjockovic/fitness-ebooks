## Summary

Dr. Layne Norton is a physique coach, natural bodybuilder, and powerlifter who popularized the PHAT (Power Hypertrophy Adaptive Training) split. He holds a PhD in nutritional sciences and is known for bridging academic research with practical bodybuilding and powerlifting applications.

## Key Contributions

1. **PHAT Training Split** - Combined upper/lower strength days with push/pull/legs hypertrophy days
   — Source: The Muscle Ladder, Ch.9

2. **Evidence-Based Coaching** - PhD-level researcher who competes and coaches

## The PHAT Split

Norton's split combines:
- **Early week:** Upper/lower days targeting strength (heavy weights, low-moderate reps)
- **Late week:** Push/pull/legs days targeting hypertrophy (moderate weights, medium-high reps)

> "I can personally endorse this split, which Dr. Layne Norton popularized. It's the one I used to train for the 2012 Canadian Nationals in natural bodybuilding, where I won the lightweight class."
— Source: The Muscle Ladder, Ch.9
