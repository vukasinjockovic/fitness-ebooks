# Training Splits

**Type:** Framework (Layer 2 - Pattern)
**Source:** The Muscle Ladder, Chapter 9
**Author:** Jeff Nippard (2024)

## Overview

A training split organizes which muscles are trained on which days. The key variable isn't the specific day but **training frequency** (how often each muscle is worked).

## Split Types

### 1. Body-Part Split (Bro Split)

One muscle group per day.

```
Monday:    Chest
Tuesday:   Back
Wednesday: Legs
Thursday:  Shoulders
Friday:    Arms
```

**Pros:** High volume per muscle, satisfying pump, popular among bodybuilders
**Cons:** Each muscle trained only 1x/week, long recovery between sessions
**Best for:** Advanced lifters with high volume needs, those who enjoy it

### 2. Upper/Lower Split

Alternate upper and lower body days.

**4-Day Version:**
```
Monday:    Upper 1
Tuesday:   Lower 1
Wednesday: Rest
Thursday:  Upper 2
Friday:    Lower 2
Weekend:   Rest
```

**6-Day Version (Advanced):**
```
Mon/Wed/Fri: Upper 1/2/3
Tue/Thu/Sat: Lower 1/2/3
```

**Pros:** 2x/week frequency, good volume distribution, flexible
**Cons:** Upper days can be long with many muscle groups
**Best for:** Intermediates, most lifters

**Variation - DUP Style:**
- Upper 1 + Lower 1: Strength-focused (low-moderate reps, heavier weights)
- Upper 2 + Lower 2: Endurance-focused (moderate-high reps, lighter weights)

### 3. Full-Body Split

Train all major muscles each workout.

**Frequency Options:**
| Workouts/Week | Best For |
|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Time-constrained (not recommended) |
| 2 | Beginners, minimal time |
| 3 | Beginners, older lifters |
| 4-5 | Intermediates/advanced (high-frequency) |

**Pros:** High frequency, fresh for each muscle, time-efficient
**Cons:** Can't hit high volume per muscle per session
**Best for:** Beginners (3x/week), advanced seeking variety (4-5x/week)

### 4. Push/Pull/Legs (PPL)

Group muscles by movement pattern.

**Push:** Chest, shoulders, triceps (pressing movements)
**Pull:** Back, biceps, rear delts (pulling movements)
**Legs:** Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves

**6-Day PPL:**
```
Monday:    Pull 1
Tuesday:   Push 1
Wednesday: Legs 1
Thursday:  Pull 2
Friday:    Push 2
Saturday:  Legs 2
Sunday:    Rest
```

**Rotating PPL (with rest days):**
```
Day 1: Pull → Day 2: Push → Day 3: Legs → Day 4: Rest → Repeat
```

**Emphasis Options:**
- Push 1: Chest emphasis → Push 2: Shoulder emphasis
- Pull 1: Lat emphasis → Pull 2: Mid-trap emphasis
- Legs 1: Quad emphasis → Legs 2: Glute/hamstring emphasis

**Pros:** 2x/week frequency, fun workouts, combines best of body-part and upper/lower
**Cons:** Slightly upper-body biased (2 upper sessions per 1 leg session)
**Best for:** Intermediate to advanced, those who enjoy dedicated workout themes

### 5. Hybrid Splits

Combine elements of multiple splits.

**PHAT Split (Layne Norton):**
```
Monday:    Upper (Power - heavy)
Tuesday:   Lower (Power - heavy)
Wednesday: Rest
Thursday:  Back & Shoulders (Hypertrophy)
Friday:    Legs (Hypertrophy)
Saturday:  Chest & Arms (Hypertrophy)
Sunday:    Rest
```

**Key Features:**
- 2 power days (strength focus)
- 3 hypertrophy days (higher reps)
- Each muscle hit 2x/week with different stimuli

> "I can personally endorse this split... It's the one I used to train for the 2012 Canadian Nationals in natural bodybuilding, where I won the lightweight class."

## High-Frequency Training

Training each muscle 3+ times per week.

**Guidelines:**
1. Keep per-session volume low (3-5 sets/muscle/workout)
2. Start with RPE 5-7 for first 1-2 weeks (avoid soreness)
3. After "repeated bout effect" kicks in, increase to RPE 7-10
4. Strategically separate heaviest exercises across days
5. Don't need to hit every muscle every workout

**Example - 5-Day Full-Body Chest Distribution:**
```
Day 1: Bench press (3 sets)
Day 2: Incline DB press (3 sets)
Day 3: Cable crossover (3 sets)
Day 4: Weighted dip (3 sets)
Day 5: Pec deck (3 sets)
Total: 15 sets/week spread across 5 sessions
```

## Split Selection Guide

| Situation | Recommended Split |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Beginner | Full-body 3x/week |
| Intermediate, 4 days available | Upper/Lower |
| Intermediate, 5-6 days available | PPL or PHAT |
| Advanced seeking variety | High-frequency full-body |
| Advanced with high volume needs | 6-day PPL or body-part |
| Time-constrained | Full-body 2-3x/week |

## Key Research

**2019 Meta-analysis (25 studies):** No difference in hypertrophy between high and low frequency when volume is equated.

> "The research shows that you can achieve about the same results with any split as long as you hit your volume target."

## Practical Considerations

- **Recovery:** Upper/lower gives 48-72 hours between similar workouts
- **Workout length:** Body-part splits = shorter sessions; full-body = longer
- **Enjoyment:** Choose a split you'll stick with
- **Schedule:** Match split to available training days
