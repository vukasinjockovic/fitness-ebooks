# The Big Six Movement Patterns

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Big Six, Fundamental Movement Patterns

## Summary

The Big Six are the most fundamental movement patterns that form the foundation of effective training programs. Any complete bodybuilding program should include at least one exercise from each pattern to ensure balanced muscular development.

## The Six Patterns

### 1. Squat-Type Movement
**Primary muscles:** Quadriceps, glutes, adductors
**Joint actions:** Knee extension, hip extension

Examples:
- Barbell back squat
- Front squat
- Leg press
- Lunges
- Bulgarian split squat

### 2. Hip Hinge
**Primary muscles:** Glutes, hamstrings, spinal erectors
**Joint actions:** Hip flexion/extension with minimal knee action

Examples:
- Conventional deadlift
- Romanian deadlift (RDL)
- Good morning
- 45-degree back extension

### 3. Vertical Push
**Primary muscles:** Anterior deltoids, lateral deltoids, triceps, upper chest
**Movement:** Pressing overhead

Examples:
- Barbell overhead press
- Dumbbell shoulder press
- Machine shoulder press

### 4. Horizontal Push
**Primary muscles:** Pectorals, triceps, anterior deltoids
**Movement:** Pressing away from torso

Examples:
- Bench press (flat, incline, decline)
- Dumbbell press
- Push-ups
- Parallel-bar dips

### 5. Vertical Pull
**Primary muscles:** Latissimus dorsi, traps, teres major, rear delts, biceps
**Movement:** Pulling down toward torso

Examples:
- Pull-ups
- Chin-ups
- Lat pulldown

### 6. Horizontal Pull
**Primary muscles:** Middle back, lats, rear delts, biceps
**Movement:** Pulling toward torso

Examples:
- Barbell row
- Dumbbell row
- Cable row
- Face pull

## Key Insights

1. **Foundation for all programs** - Learn these patterns in first few months of training
   — Source: The Muscle Ladder, Ch.5

2. **Compound vs. isolation balance** - Big Six are compounds; isolation exercises fill gaps
   — Source: The Muscle Ladder, Ch.5

3. **Order in workouts** - Do compound exercises (Big Six) early when fresh
   — Source: The Muscle Ladder, Ch.5
