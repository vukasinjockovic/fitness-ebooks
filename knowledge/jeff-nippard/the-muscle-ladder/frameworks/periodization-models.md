# Periodization Models

**Type:** Framework (Layer 3 - Strategy)
**Source:** The Muscle Ladder, Chapter 13
**Author:** Jeff Nippard (2024)

## Definition

Periodization is the systematic organization of training over time into distinct phases (macrocycles, mesocycles, microcycles) to optimize progress and prevent stagnation.

## Training Cycle Hierarchy

```
Macrocycle (months to year)
  └── Mesocycle (4-12 weeks)
        └── Microcycle (1 week)
```

## Core Periodization Models

### 1. Linear Periodization

**Pattern:** Intensity (load) increases while volume decreases over time.

**Example - 7-week squat mesocycle:**
| Week | Sets × Reps | RPE |
|------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | 4 × 8 | 7 |
| 2 | 4 × 6 | 8 |
| 3 | 4 × 5 | 8 |
| 4 | 5 × 4 | 8.5 |
| 5 | 5 × 3 | 9 |
| 6 | 3 × 2 | 9.5 |
| 7 | Deload | 6 |

**Best for:** Strength peaking, powerlifting prep
**Note:** Linear periodization ≠ linear progression (load/reps both going up)

### 2. Reverse Linear Periodization

**Pattern:** Intensity decreases while volume increases over time.

**Example - 6-week squat mesocycle:**
| Week | Reps | RPE |
|------|------|-----|
| 1 | 2 | 9 |
| 2 | 4 | 8.5 |
| 3 | 6 | 8 |
| 4 | 8 | 8 |
| 5 | 10 | 8 |
| 6 | Deload | 6 |

**Best for:**
- Bodybuilders building volume tolerance
- Powerlifters in offseason (muscle building while maintaining lift specificity)
- Increasing work capacity

### 3. Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP)

**Pattern:** Sets, reps, and/or loads vary within each week (daily undulation).

**Example - Biceps 3x/week:**
| Monday | Wednesday | Friday |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| 3×10 @ 60lb | 3×6 @ 80lb | 3×15 @ 40lb |
| (moderate) | (heavy) | (light) |

**Example - Powerlifter bench press:**
| Monday | Wednesday | Friday |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Moderate reps | Low reps (strength) | Light/explosive (power) |
| 3×8 @ 65% | 5×3 @ 85% | 5×5 @ 55% |

**Best for:** Most lifters, prevents boredom, targets multiple adaptations
**Mechanism:** Likely psychological (variety) rather than physiological

### 4. Weekly Undulating Periodization (WUP)

**Pattern:** Sets, reps, and loads vary week-to-week in waves.

**Example - 5-week bench mesocycle:**
| Week | Reps | Load Pattern |
|------|------|--------------|
| 1 | 8 | Moderate |
| 2 | 6 | Up |
| 3 | 10 | Down |
| 4 | 4 | Up |
| 5 | Deload | Recovery |

Then repeat with slightly increased loads for another 5 weeks.

**Visual:** Waves progressively getting bigger over multiple mesocycles.

**Best for:**
- Advanced lifters stagnated on other models
- Those who thrive on novelty/variety
- Breaking through strength plateaus

## Mesocycle Progression Strategies

### Adding Reps + Load
| Week | Reps | Load |
|------|------|------|
| 1 | 4 | 100 |
| 2 | 5 | 100 |
| 3 | 6 | 100 |
| 4 | 4 | 105 |
| 5 | 5 | 105 |

### Increasing RPE
| Week | Sets × Reps | RPE |
|------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | 3×10 | 7 |
| 2 | 3×10 | 8 |
| 3 | 3×10 | 9 |
| 4 | 3×10 + drop set | 10 |
| 5 | Deload | 6-7 |

### Adding Sets (Advanced)
| Week | Sets | Reps | RPE |
|------|------|------|-----|
| 1 | 2 | 12-15 | 8 |
| 2 | 3 | 12-15 | 8 |
| 3 | 4 | 12-15 | 8 |
| 4 | 5 | 12-15 | 8 |
| 5 | Deload | - | 6 |

> "Adding sets increases volume very quickly... should be reserved for low-load exercises and movements where other forms of progression are more difficult."

## Deloads

**Definition:** A period (usually 1 week) where load, volume, or both are decreased to reduce fatigue and promote recovery.

### Frequency
| Training Level | Deload Frequency |
|----------------|------------------|
| Beginner | Rarely needed |
| Intermediate | Every 8-12 weeks |
| Advanced | Every 4-8 weeks |

### Calculated Deload Protocol
1. Drop volume 30-50% (cut 1-2 sets/exercise)
2. Reduce RPE by 1-3 points (e.g., RPE 9 → RPE 6-7)
3. If using %1RM, reduce by 5-10%

### Instinctive Deload
- Still aim for "light week"
- Flexible with exact sets/reps/loads
- May swap exercises or equipment
- Focus on technique refinement

### Signs You Need a Deload
- Weights feeling unusually heavy
- Poor sleep quality
- Declining motivation
- Chronic fatigue or joint aches

> "A deload works best with moderate decreases in volume and intensity... You're not using your deload week as an excuse to sandbag your workouts."

## Complete Training Break

- Research: No significant size/strength loss until 2-3 weeks of complete detraining
- Risk: Loss of momentum, weights feel heavier on return
- Best used: Vacations, mental health breaks, injuries

## Microcycle Organization

### Prioritization
Place most important work:
1. Early in the week (after rest day)
2. First in the workout

**Example - Weak chest on upper/lower:**
- Monday: Upper (chest first) - after Sunday rest
- Friday: Upper (chest first) - after Thursday rest

### Variation (within microcycle)
- Use different exercises for same muscle on different days
- Apply DUP (vary reps/loads across week)
