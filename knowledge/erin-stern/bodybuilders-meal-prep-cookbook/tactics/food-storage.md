# Food Storage
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** meal prep storage, food preservation, container storage

## Summary

Proper food storage techniques that maintain freshness, safety, and flavor of meal-prepped foods throughout the week. Includes container selection, refrigeration, freezing, and reheating guidelines.

## Container Selection

### Glass Containers (Recommended)
- **Pros:** Durable, nontoxic, no odor absorption, microwave/oven safe
- **Cons:** Heavier, more expensive, breakable

### Plastic Containers
- **Requirements:** BPA-free, microwave-safe, secure lids
- **Pros:** Lightweight, affordable, durable
- **Cons:** Can absorb odors, may warp over time

### Specialized Options
- **Sectioned containers** - Keep components separate (prevents sogginess)
- **Freezer-safe bags** - For items going to freezer
- **Ramekins** - For individual portions like egg bakes

## Refrigerator Storage

**Temperature:** Below 40°F (4°C)

**Placement:**
- Store on shelves (not door—temperature fluctuates)
- Keep raw ingredients separate from cooked meals
- Place oldest meals at front (FIFO—first in, first out)

**Duration:** Most prepped meals last 5 days refrigerated

**Cooling Protocol:**
> "Allow them to cool down to room temperature. Placing hot food directly into the refrigerator can create condensation, leading to moisture buildup and potential bacterial growth. It's best to let your meals cool for about 30 to 60 minutes before refrigerating."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## Freezer Storage

**Duration:** 2-3 months before quality degradation

**Best Practices:**
- Divide large batches into individual portions before freezing
- Use freezer-safe containers or wrap tightly in foil/freezer bags
- Label with name and date
- Remove excess air from bags

**What Freezes Well:**
- Cooked proteins
- Grains (rice, quinoa)
- Casseroles and bakes
- Soups and stews
- Egg muffins

**What Doesn't Freeze Well:**
- Lettuce/leafy greens (become mushy)
- Cucumber
- Milk and eggs (texture changes)
- High-water-content vegetables

## Reheating Guidelines

**Microwave:**
- Use microwave-safe containers
- Cover with lid or damp paper towel (retains moisture)
- Reheat in short intervals
- Stir/flip halfway through

**Oven/Stovetop:**
- Transfer to oven-safe dish
- Cover with foil or lid (prevents drying)
- Lower heat, longer time = better texture

## Key Quotes
