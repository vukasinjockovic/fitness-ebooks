# Batch Cooking
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** meal prep cooking, bulk cooking, prep day cooking

## Summary

A time-efficient cooking method where large quantities of food are prepared simultaneously during dedicated "prep sessions," then portioned and stored for the week. This transforms healthy eating from a daily chore into a weekly investment.

## Core Principles

1. **Parallel processing** - Multiple items cook simultaneously (oven, stovetop, slow cooker)
2. **Single ingredient, multiple uses** - Cook 6 cups quinoa for use in 3 different recipes
3. **Efficient sequencing** - Start long-cook items first, fill gaps with quick tasks
4. **Batch similar tasks** - Chop all vegetables at once, measure all spices together

## Implementation

### Prep Session Structure (from Stern's plans)

**Phase 1: Prep Work**
- Preheat oven
- Wash and chop all vegetables
- Measure and combine dry ingredients
- Mix sauces and dressings

**Phase 2: Long-Cook Items**
- Start slow-cooker items
- Begin oven-baked proteins
- Start grains (quinoa, rice)

**Phase 3: Stovetop Items**
- Cook proteins that need attention
- Prepare quick-cook vegetables
- Make sauces that need heating

**Phase 4: Assembly**
- Portion into containers
- Label with date
- Store appropriately

### Recommended Schedule

| Option | Frequency | Duration |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Single session | 1x/week (Sunday) | 3-4 hours |
| Split sessions | 2x/week (Sun + Wed) | 1.5-2 hours each |
| Hybrid | Cook proteins Sun, assemble daily | Variable |

## Batch Cooking Candidates

**Best for batch cooking:**
- Proteins (chicken, turkey, beef, tofu)
- Grains (rice, quinoa, oatmeal)
- Roasted vegetables
- Sauces and dressings
- Egg muffins/bakes

**Less suitable:**
- Salads (prep ingredients separately)
- Foods that don't reheat well
- Items better fresh (certain vegetables)

## Time-Saving Tips

1. **Use timers** - Track multiple cooking items simultaneously
2. **Clean as you go** - Minimize end-of-prep cleanup
3. **Prep ingredients first** - "Mise en place" before cooking starts
4. **Double recipes** - Freeze half for future weeks
