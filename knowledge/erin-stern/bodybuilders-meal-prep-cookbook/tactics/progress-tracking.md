# Progress Tracking
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** monitoring progress, physique tracking, measurement protocols

## Summary

A systematic approach to monitoring physique changes using multiple data points—scale weight, body measurements, and progress photos—with appropriate frequency and methodology to enable data-driven adjustments.

## Tracking Methods

### 1. Scale Weight
**Frequency:** Weekly (not more often)
**Protocol:**
- Same day each week
- Same time (morning recommended)
- Same conditions (after bathroom, before food)

**Targets:**
- Fat loss: ~1% body weight lost per week
- Muscle gain: ~0.5% body weight gained per week

> "You should not be weighing yourself more often than once per week, as this can lead to too many changes too quickly."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

### 2. Body Measurements
**Frequency:** Bi-weekly or monthly
**Key Sites:**
- Waist (at navel)
- Target muscle groups (biceps, thighs, chest)
- Hips (for overall composition)

**Application:**
- If waist grows faster than muscles → reduce calories
- If muscles grow faster than waist → surplus is appropriate

### 3. Progress Photos
**Frequency:** Bi-weekly or monthly
**Protocol:**
- Same lighting
- Same poses (front, side, back)
- Same time of day
- Consistent clothing

**Value:** Captures visual changes that scale/measurements miss

### 4. Body Fat Percentage
**Methods:**
- Calipers (affordable, moderate accuracy)
- DEXA scan (expensive, highest accuracy)

**Frequency:** Monthly or less
**Use:** Baseline assessment and periodic check-ins

## Assessment Timing

| Check Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| Weigh-in | Weekly | Trend tracking |
| Measurements | Bi-weekly | Composition changes |
| Photos | Bi-weekly | Visual documentation |
| Body fat | Monthly+ | Detailed composition |
| Plan adjustment | After 2 weeks no change | Course correction |

## Key Quotes

> "If you don't see progress, or if it's going in the wrong direction, adjustments will need to be made."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

> "Keep in mind that progress is never linear; you may have one week where you'll see great progress and another week where nothing seems to happen. Wait until you've gone two weeks with no changes before adjusting your diet."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## When to Adjust

**Trigger adjustment after:**
- 2+ weeks of no measurable progress
- Progress going in wrong direction
- Significant life changes affecting routine

**Do NOT adjust when:**
- Less than 2 weeks since last change
- Single "bad" weigh-in (wait for trend)
- External factors explain temporary stall

## Choosing Metrics

> "It's important to only choose 1 to 2 metrics for simplicity purposes."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

**Recommended combinations:**
- Fat loss: Scale + waist measurement
- Muscle gain: Scale + target muscle measurements + waist
- Recomp: Photos + measurements (scale may not move)
