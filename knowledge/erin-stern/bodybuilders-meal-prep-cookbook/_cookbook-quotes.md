# Quotable Quotes from The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook
**Author:** Erin Stern
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04

## On Balance and Sustainability

> "It's important to note that the 90/10 rule of fitness also applies to eating, as life is about balance. Eat on plan for about 90% of your meals, but allow some room for social time, celebrations, holidays, etc."

> "Enjoy the 10% and don't stress about an off-plan meal here and there. You will still make progress, and you'll do it without feeling deprived."

## On Nutrition Fundamentals

> "It has been said that abs are made in the kitchen. While training provides the stimulus for muscle gain, nutrition provides the body with the necessary fuel to make physique changes happen."

> "Think about it like building a house—in order to build a house, you need materials for the foundation, walls, roof, and so on. You can have the plans, but without the necessary materials, the plans won't be realized."

> "It can be helpful to think about protein as a fixed macro, meaning that your intake should not vary much from day to day."

## On Caloric Surplus and Deficit

> "Make sure your caloric surplus is in the form of carbs and protein, not fats; in a caloric surplus, an excess of dietary fats is easily converted to body fat."

> "Carbs assist in building muscle and in recovery, and can be protein-sparing. This means that a higher carb intake will help prevent the body from using protein for fuel."

## On Meal Timing

> "The two most important meals of the day are your pre- and post-workout meals."

> "If you train early in the morning, try to eat at least 15 to 20 grams of protein right before training. This goes a long way toward preventing muscle loss and boosting energy levels."

## On Goal Setting

> "A vague goal like 'getting in shape' is not specific and is based on self-judgment. You're essentially beating yourself up before you start and have no way of gauging what's 'good enough.'"

> "Your main goal may seem daunting and far away when you start out, but the mini goals will keep you feeling motivated and help you feel that your bigger goals are attainable."

## On Adjustments

> "A great rule of thumb is to adjust caloric intake either upward or downward by approximately 10% or between 100 to 300 average daily calories for each adjustment."

> "Making too many adjustments too soon may result in you not being able to pinpoint which changes worked and which did not, leading to the need for extra troubleshooting."

> "Understand that, in general, your caloric needs will increase as you gain muscle and will decrease as you lean down."

## On Habits and Consistency

> "As with any lifestyle change, slow adjustments over time usually stick, whereas immediate, complete overhauls usually don't."

> "Give yourself a few weeks for meal prep to become a habit, and it will be much easier to stay on track."

> "Make an appointment to meal prep as you would for any other task. Stick with it for a couple of weeks, as it likely will feel like work when you first get started, but after a short while it will become a habit, and eventually it will become a lifestyle!"

## On Progress Monitoring

> "You should not be weighing yourself more often than once per week, as this can lead to too many changes too quickly."

> "For fat loss, you can safely drop about 1% of your body weight each week. This will ensure that you're losing fat and not muscle."

> "A good rule of thumb for muscle gain is a 0.5% of body weight gained each week."

## On Carb Cycling

> "Lower-carb diets can help improve insulin sensitivity and can promote feelings of satiety, while higher-carb diets can fuel muscle recovery and aid in muscle retention. Get the best of both worlds with this carb cycling plan."
