# Goal-Based Meal Planning System
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** prep plan system, physique goal meal plans

## Summary

A systematic approach to bodybuilding nutrition that organizes meal plans by physique goals rather than generic "healthy eating." Each goal (bulk, lean bulk, maintenance, leaning down) has its own calorie targets, macro ratios, and curated recipes designed to support that specific outcome.

## Key Principles

1. **Goal determines calories** - Bulk requires surplus, fat loss requires deficit, maintenance matches expenditure
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

2. **Macro ratios vary by goal** - Bulking emphasizes carbs (45%), fat loss emphasizes protein (40-45%)
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

3. **Recipes are goal-specific** - Each plan includes recipes optimized for its macro targets
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.2-9

4. **5-day meal cycle** - Weekly plans cover 5 days with complete shopping lists and prep instructions
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.2-9

## The 8 Prep Plans

| Plan | Goal | Calories | Macro Split |
|------|------|----------|-------------|
| Bulk | Maximum muscle gain | 2,100-2,200 | 35P/45C/20F |
| Lean Bulk | Muscle with minimal fat | 1,700-1,800 | 40P/40C/20F |
| Calorie Cycling | Maintenance | 1,500-1,700 | Variable |
| Reduced Carb | Maintenance | 1,400-1,500 | 40P/30C/30F |
| Balanced | Maintenance | 1,400-1,500 | 35P/35C/30F |
| Paleo-Style | Maintenance | 1,400-1,500 | 40P/25C/35F |
| Higher Volume | Fat loss | 1,200-1,400 | 45P/35C/20F |
| Carb Cycling | Fat loss | 1,200-1,400 | Variable |

## Implementation Steps

1. **Assess starting point** - Body composition, body fat percentage, current caloric intake
2. **Select goal** - Bulk, lean bulk, maintain, or lean down
3. **Calculate maintenance** - Weight × activity factor (12-14)
4. **Choose appropriate plan** - Match plan to goal and preferences
5. **Execute meal prep** - Follow shopping list, equipment list, and step-by-step prep
6. **Monitor progress** - Weekly check-ins using scale, measurements, or photos
7. **Adjust as needed** - Use 10% rule to modify when progress stalls

## Key Quotes
