# Carb Cycling Framework
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** carbohydrate cycling, high/low carb days

## Summary

A dietary strategy that alternates between higher-carb and lower-carb days to achieve benefits of both approaches: improved insulin sensitivity and satiety from lower-carb periods, plus enhanced muscle recovery and retention from higher-carb periods. The approach strategically aligns higher-carb days with heavier training sessions.

## Key Principles

1. **Strategic carb placement** - Higher carbs on training days, lower carbs on rest/lighter days
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.9

2. **Improved insulin sensitivity** - Lower-carb days help improve how the body responds to carbohydrates
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.9

3. **Satiety benefits** - Lower-carb days with higher fat/protein improve fullness
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.9

4. **Muscle preservation** - Higher-carb days fuel recovery and aid muscle retention during fat loss
   — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.9

## Implementation (from Stern's Carb Cycling Plan)

### Higher-Carb Days (3 per week)
- **Calories:** ~1,400-1,500
- **Macro Split:** 40P/40C/20F
- **Example:** 140g protein, 160g carbs, 42g fat
- **When:** Heavy training days

### Lower-Carb Days (2 per week)
- **Calories:** ~1,200
- **Macro Split:** 40P/20C/40F
- **Example:** 114g protein, 76g carbs, 47g fat
- **When:** Rest days or lighter training

## Practical Application

- Each recipe provides both lower-carb and higher-carb serving options
- Portion sizes and additions (bread, fruit, rice) create the carb difference
- Same meal prep, different assembly for each day type
