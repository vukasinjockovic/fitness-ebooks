# Salmon Buddha Bowls

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings (1 lower carb and 3 higher carb) |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 20 minutes |

> Flavorful bites of omega-3-rich salmon are so easy to prepare and are the star of these simple rice bowls. A finishing drizzle of spicy Greek yogurt sauce adds some protein while also adding some spicy flavor.

## Ingredients

- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tbsp honey
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 lb (454g) wild-caught salmon fillet, skin removed and diced
- 3/4 cup plus ٢ tbsp finely chopped carrots
- 4 cups peeled and chopped cucumber
- 1 3/4 cups cooked Arborio or other short-grain rice

**For the sauce**
- 1 3/4 cups plain nonfat Greek yogurt
- Juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1–2 tbsp sriracha sauce, to taste

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Combine the paprika, honey, Dijon mustard, and salt in a large bowl. Stir to combine and then add the salmon. Toss to coat.
3. Place the salmon on the prepared sheet pan. Bake until golden, about 12 minutes, then remove from oven and set aside to cool.
4. While the salmon is cooling, make the sauce by whisking the yogurt, lemon juice, and sriracha sauce in a small bowl until smooth and easily pourable, adding a little water if needed to thin out as desired.
5. Place the salmon in 4 meal prep containers along with the rice, carrots, and cucumber. Store the sauce in a separate container until ready to serve. When ready to serve, combine the bowl ingredients and then drizzle the sauce over the top. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 342 | Total fat 16g | Total carbohydrate 19g | Protein 31g
