# Hash Brown Stacks

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings (1 lower carb and 3 higher carb) |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 10 minutes |

> Make this quick and easy, balanced meal for a breakfast swap or an easy weeknight dinner alternative. Look for prepared hash browns in the freezer section of the grocery store.

## Ingredients

- 7 hash brown patties
- 1 tbsp avocado or olive oil (omit oil for high-carb servings)
- 4 cups egg whites, divided
- 1 cup diced bell peppers, divided
- 4 slices Canadian bacon, divided
- 4oz (113g) shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
- Fresh basil, for garnish (optional)
- 3 medium apples

## Directions

1. Prepare the hash browns according to package directions.
2. Add the oil to a large nonstick skillet or spray the skillet with cooking spray. Preheat the skillet over medium heat. Add 1 cup egg whites and 1/4 cup peppers. Scramble until the eggs begin to set and then add 1 ounce cheese. Continue scrambling until the cheese is melted, then remove the eggs from the pan.
3. Wipe the skillet clean with a paper towel and then add the remaining 3 cups egg whites and 3/4 cup peppers. Scramble until the eggs are set and then add the remaining 3 ounces cheese. Continue scrambling until the cheese is melted, then remove the eggs from the pan.
4. Place the Canadian bacon in the skillet and heat until warmed.
5. To each of 4 meal prep containers, add 1 hash brown patty for the low-carb serving and 2 hash brown patties for the high-carb servings. Top the hash brown patties with one slice Canadian bacon. Place the eggs on the opposite sides of the containers and top the eggs with the basil (if using).
6. When ready to serve, stack the eggs on top of the Canadian bacon and hash brown patties and reheat. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- If you prefer the hash browns to be extra crispy, cook them in your air fryer.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 359 | Total fat 14g | Total carbohydrate 17g | Protein 42g
