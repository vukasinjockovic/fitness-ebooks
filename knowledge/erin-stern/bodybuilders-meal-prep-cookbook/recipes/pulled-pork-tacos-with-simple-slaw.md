# Pulled Pork Tacos With Simple Slaw

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 8 tacos |
| **Serving Size** | 2 tacos with about 1 cup slaw |
| **Prep Time** | 15 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 30 minutes on the stovetop (or 4 hours in the slow cooker) |

> Instead of high-fat cuts of pork, this recipe uses lean pork tenderloin that is simmered low and slow on the stovetop or in a slow cooker. You can use this pork for rice bowls and burritos, as well as tacos.

## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 lb (680g) pork tenderloin, trimmed and cut into 4 pieces
- 1 large yellow onion, sliced
- 1/4 cup no-sugar-added BBQ sauce (such as Noble Made)
- 1/2 tsp garlic salt
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 8 medium corn tortillas, for serving

**For the slaw**
- 1 (14oz/397g) bag pre-made coleslaw mix
- Juice of 1 medium lime
- 1/2 tsp celery salt
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp plain low-fat Greek yogurt

## Directions

1. Preheat a Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the pork, onion, barbecue sauce, garlic salt, kosher salt, and chili powder. Sauté, uncovered, for 5 minutes, then cover and cook for an additional 25 minutes or until the pork reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (65°C).
2. While the pork is cooking, make the slaw by combining the coleslaw mix, lime juice, celery salt, olive oil, and Greek yogurt in a large bowl. Toss well.
3. Once the pork is cooked, transfer to a cutting board and use two forks to shred. Boil the sauce until it reduces by half, then place the meat back in the Dutch oven and toss to coat with the sauce.
4. Store the pork and slaw in separate airtight storage containers. To serve, top each warmed tortilla with 2 ounces (57g) of the pork and 1/2 cup of the slaw. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 433 | Total fat 14g | Total carbohydrate 35g | Protein 41g
