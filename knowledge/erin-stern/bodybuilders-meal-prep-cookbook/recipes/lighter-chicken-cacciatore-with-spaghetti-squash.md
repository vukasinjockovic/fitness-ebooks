# Lighter Chicken Cacciatore With Spaghetti Squash

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | Approximately 7 ounces (198g) cooked chicken and 1/4 of the sauce and spaghetti squash |
| **Prep Time** | 15 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 1 hour 10 minutes |

> This version of an Italian comfort food favorite is more macro-friendly and easier for meal prep. Chicken thighs are inexpensive, and spaghetti squash stands in for pasta and adds nutrients, fiber, and flavor without adding too many carbs. The leftovers taste great, as the flavors have time to meld in the refrigerator, making this an excellent meal prep choice.

## Ingredients

- 1 medium spaghetti squash
- 2lb (907g) boneless skinless chicken thighs
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 1 medium green bell pepper, diced
- 3/4 cup chicken broth
- 1 (28oz/794g) can crushed tomatoes
- 1/2 pint button mushrooms
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C). Carefully cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out seeds. Lightly spray the flesh with cooking spray and then place cut side down on a sheet pan. Poke a few holes in the outside of the spaghetti squash with a fork. Place the squash in the oven and set a timer for 30 minutes.
2. While the spaghetti squash is baking, spray a large nonstick pan with cooking spray and place over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and Italian seasoning and then add the thighs to the pan, making sure not to crowd the pan. Leave the chicken thighs in the pan to brown for 3 to 4 minutes before flipping them. Once the chicken is browned on both sides, add the onion and bell pepper and reduce the heat to medium. Cook until the onion becomes translucent, about 3 to 4 minutes.
3. Add the chicken broth, tomatoes, mushrooms, and garlic powder. Stir until the ingredients are well combined and then reduce the heat to low. Simmer for 10 minutes, then cover and simmer for an additional 10 to 15 minutes.
4. Once the spaghetti squash looks browned on the outside (30 to 40 minutes), remove it from the oven and allow it to cool for 10 to 15 minutes. Shred the squash with a fork to create “spaghetti.”
5. Spoon the squash into 4 meal prep containers and then top with the chicken and sauce. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- For a lower-fat option, substitute chicken breasts for thighs. Note that since chicken breasts are leaner, they can become dry if overcooked, so aim for a 160°F (70°C) internal temperature.
- Change it up: If you want to add a salty kick, toss in a few tablespoons of green olives or 1 to 2 tablespoons of capers. For a variation on this low-carb meal, use shirataki noodles instead of spaghetti squash—just toss them into the cacciatore during the last few minutes of simmering.
