# Spaghetti Squash With Lentil Bolognese

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Prep Time** | 15 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 35 minutes |

> Lentils pack in the plant-based protein, boasting nearly 20 grams of protein per cup. Mozzarella cheese is another unexpected protein source with an impressive 6 grams of protein per ounce.

## Ingredients

- 1 large spaghetti squash
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 cup diced onion
- 1/2 cup chopped carrot
- 1/2 cup chopped celery
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 1 (15oz/425g) can diced tomatoes
- 5 cups cooked lentils
- 5oz (141g) shredded mozzarella cheese

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C). Carefully cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds. Lightly spray the cut sides with cooking spray and then place them cut sides down on a foil-lined baking sheet. Poke a few holes in the outside of the squash with a fork. Place in the oven and set a timer for 30 minutes.
2. While the spaghetti squash is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large nonstick pan over medium heat. Add the onion, carrot, and celery, season with the salt and Italian seasoning, and sauté for 5 minutes.
3. Add the garlic, tomatoes, and lentils to the pan. Mix well and simmer for 20 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
4. When the squash is cooked, remove it from the oven and preheat the broiler. Using tongs, carefully flip each half over, fill each with the lentil mixture, and then sprinkle the cheese over the top.
5. Place under the broiler for 2 to 3 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Once cooled, cut each piece in half and transfer to 4 meal prep containers. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 455 | Total fat 14g | Total carbohydrate 38g | Protein 38g
