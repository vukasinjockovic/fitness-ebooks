# Thai Turkey Lettuce Cups

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 16 cups |
| **Serving Size** | 4 cups |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 15 minutes |

> This handheld meal is just as fun to eat as it is healthy. But if scooping up lettuce cups isn’t your idea of fun, just chop everything up, place it right into the meal prep containers, and eat these with a fork instead. Lean turkey takes on a ton of flavor with just a few simple ingredients. (And don’t forget to add a sprinkle of salty peanuts for some crunch.)

## Ingredients

- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 cup chopped white onion
- 1lb (454g) lean ground turkey
- 1/2 tsp ground ginger
- 3/4 tsp kosher salt
- 4 tbsp Thai chili sauce
- 1 cup shredded carrots
- 2 cups cooked Arborio or other short-grain rice
- 1/3 cup chopped raw peanuts
- 1/4 cup chopped scallions
- Iceberg lettuce leaves, for serving

## Directions

1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and sauté until translucent, about 3 minutes.
2. Add the turkey, ginger, and salt to the skillet. Cook for an additional 5 to 6 minutes or until the turkey is browned. Add the chili sauce and carrots and simmer for 5 minutes more. Mix in the rice.
3. Transfer the filling to a large storage container and store the lettuce cups, peanuts, and scallions in separate medium storage containers. When ready to serve, scoop the filling into the lettuce cups and then top with chopped peanuts and scallions.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 419 | Total fat 14g | Total carbohydrate 40g | Protein 37g
