# Roasted Mustard Salmon With Rosemary Potato Wedges

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 40 minutes |

> A flavorful dose of inflammation-fighting omega-3 fats from salmon, along with savory and crispy roasted potatoes, checks all the boxes and macro targets. This is an ideal post-workout meal, especially after a strenuous workout.

## Ingredients

- 4 medium Yukon Gold potatoes, washed, dried, and cut into wedges
- 2 tsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp chopped fresh rosemary
- 1/2 tsp black pepper, divided
- 1 tsp kosher salt, divided
- 1lb (454g) salmon fillet (skin on)
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- Juice of 1 medium lemon, divided
- 4 cups steamed spinach, to serve

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line 2 sheet pans with parchment paper.
2. Add the potatoes to one sheet pan, drizzle with olive oil, and then season with the rosemary, 1/4 teaspoon black pepper, and 1/2 teaspoon salt.
3. Place the salmon fillet on the second sheet pan. Spread the mustard over the salmon, followed by the juice of half a lemon, the remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt, and the remaining 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
4. Place the potatoes in the oven and set the timer for 20 minutes. At the timer expiring, place the salmon in the oven with the potatoes and set the timer for 20 more minutes. After 40 minutes, remove the potatoes and salmon from the oven, squeeze the remaining lemon juice over the salmon, and allow to cool slightly.
5. To each of 4 meal prep containers, add one quarter of the salmon, 1 cup potatoes, and 1 cup spinach. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- Cut the potatoes ahead and soak them in cold water—this will prevent them from browning and will help them get extra crispy in the oven. Pat dry before roasting.
- Change it up: You can make this recipe with artic char, swordfish, or any other type of meaty fish.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 461 | Total fat 18g | Total carbohydrate 44g | Protein 33g
