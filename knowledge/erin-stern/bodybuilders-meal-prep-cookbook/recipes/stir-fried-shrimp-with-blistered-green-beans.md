# Stir-Fried Shrimp With Blistered Green Beans

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 6 ounces (170g) cooked shrimp, 1 cup green beans, and |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 15 minutes |

> Lean shrimp and lightly charred green beans make for a quick-cooking lunch or dinner. Green beans offer up plentiful hunger-fighting fiber, which helps make this low-fat meal extra satisfying.

## Ingredients

- 2 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 2 tsp honey
- 1 tbsp reduced-sodium soy sauce or tamari
- 2lb (907g) large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 4 cups roughly chopped fresh green beans
- 3 cups cooked Arborio or other short-grain rice

## Directions

1. Combine the sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, and soy sauce or tamari in a small bowl. Whisk and then set aside.
2. Preheat a large nonstick skillet over high heat. Add the shrimp and green beans. Cook until the shrimp turn pink and the green beans begin to char, about 3 to 4 minutes.
3. Reduce the heat to medium-high and add the sauce. Stir-fry, tossing constantly, until the shrimp are opaque and the green beans are slightly tender.
4. Turn off the heat and allow to cool slightly. Transfer 6 ounces (170g) shrimp, 1 cup green beans, and 3/4 cup rice to each of 4 meal prep containers. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- Frozen shrimp are a healthy and affordable option, just run them under cool water to defrost. To save time, you can substitute a bottled teriyaki—such as Soyaki from Trader Joe’s.
- Change it up: For added flavor, add freshly grated ginger and minced garlic to the sauce.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 387 | Total fat 7g | Total carbohydrate 43g | Protein 37g
