# Sheet Pan Smoky Chicken With Roasted Veggies

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 5 ounces (142g) cooked chicken and 2 cups veggies |
| **Prep Time** | 15 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 20 to 25 minutes |

> Spice up your meal prep with a variety of flavors all contained within one sheet pan. Roasting the meal at a higher temperature yields tender and juicy chicken along with crispy, caramelized veggies. Be sure to dice your vegetables evenly so they’ll roast evenly.

## Ingredients


**For the chicken**
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp brown sugar
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 1/2 lb (680g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs (about 4 thighs)

**For the veggies**
- 1 1/2 lb (680g) sweet potatoes (about 6 cups), peeled and diced
- 1 1/2 lb (680g) Brussels sprouts (about 4 cups), trimmed and halved
- 1 1/2 tsp salt
- 2 garlic cloves, diced

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C). Line a sheet pan with foil and then spray with cooking spray. (If needed, you can use two sheet pans and add a few minutes to the cooking time)
2. In a large bowl, combine the paprika, brown sugar, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, and black pepper. Mix until well incorporated.
3. Add the chicken thighs to the bowl and toss, coating each piece thoroughly with the seasoning. Place the seasoned chicken on the sheet pan, making sure to space the chicken evenly.
4. Combine the sweet potatoes, Brussels sprouts, and garlic in a large bowl. Sprinkle with salt and then hand toss to distribute evenly.
5. Place the veggies on the sheet pan with the chicken. Spread them out evenly and then lightly spray everything with cooking spray. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C), tossing the veggies halfway through cooking. Transfer to 4 meal prep containers and keep tightly covered in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- This chicken stores and reheats well. Depending on your caloric intake or for multiple people, this recipe can be doubled.
- Change it up: This recipe also works well for turkey breasts. You can use russet potatoes instead of sweet potatoes.

Calories 598 | Total fat 13g | Total carbohydrate 64g | Protein 55g
