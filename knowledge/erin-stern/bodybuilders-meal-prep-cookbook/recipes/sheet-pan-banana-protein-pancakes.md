# Sheet Pan Banana Protein Pancakes

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 12 pancakes |
| **Serving Size** | 3 pancakes, |
| **Prep Time** | 20 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 15 minutes |

> The best thing about these pancakes is how easy they are to make in bulk. They’re naturally sweetened with just a hint of banana and served with a high-protein topping instead of sugary maple syrup.

## Ingredients

- 2 cups high-protein pancake mix (such as Kodiak)
- 3 large eggs, lightly beaten
- 1 cup water
- 1 medium banana (ripe), chopped
- 1 tbsp melted coconut oil
- 2 cups plain nonfat Greek yogurt
- 1 cup fresh berries (such as blueberries, strawberries, or raspberries)

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Spray two 9×13-inch (23×33cm) sheet pans with cooking spray. Line the pans with parchment paper and then spray again with cooking spray. (This will help ensure the pancakes won’t stick to the pan.)
2. Combine the pancake mix, eggs, water, banana, and coconut oil in a blender. Blend until smooth, adding additional water in small amounts, if needed, to create a pourable consistency.
3. Pour half the batter into each prepared sheet pan and then place the pans in the oven. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes or until the pancakes are golden and a cake tester or butter knife inserted into the center comes out clean.
4. Allow to cool completely. Once cooled, cut each pancake into 6 equal-size pieces. To each of 4 meal prep containers, add 3 pancakes, 1/2 cup yogurt, and 1/4 cup berries, keeping the yogurt separate from the other ingredients until ready to serve. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- Use a sheet pan with a high enough edge so that when the batter cooks, the pancakes won’t expand over the sides of the pan.
