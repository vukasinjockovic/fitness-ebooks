# Lean Protein Rice Pudding

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 1 cup rice pudding, |
| **Prep Time** | 5 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 30 minutes |

> Rice pudding is usually thick, decadent, and absolutely not on a meal prep plan for leaning down—until now. This reduced-calorie version still tastes indulgent, but it fits well into your plan. And it’s easy to whip up, making it a great breakfast or snack after a workout. If you haven’t tried rice pudding but love overnight oats, give this recipe a go.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup Arborio or other short-grain rice
- 1 cup water
- 3 cups unsweetened almond milk
- 1/4 cup sugar-free granulated sweetener (Swerve, Truvia, or Sweet Leaf)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 tsp ground cinnamon
- Pinch of salt
- 5 scoops (25–30 grams each) vanilla protein powder (see Prep Tip )
- 1 cup fresh blueberries (or other fresh berries)
- 1/2 cup sliced almonds

## Directions

1. In a large pot over high heat, combine the rice, water, almond milk, sweetener, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil, stirring continuously. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and set a timer for 15 minutes, stirring the mixture frequently to prevent it from sticking or burning at the bottom of the pot.
2. After 15 minutes, check the consistency of the rice, as it should be soft and look like porridge. If the rice is ready, remove it from the heat and allow it to cool for about 10 minutes before stirring in the protein powder.
3. Transfer to 4 meal prep containers. Top each serving with 1/4 cup berries and 2 tablespoons almonds. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- Instead of using an isolate for this recipe, consider using a protein blend that contains either whey protein concentrate or casein. Plant-based proteins work well, too. An isolate tends to become watery, and this will affect the consistency of your pudding.
- Change it up: Coconut milk works well in this recipe, too. (Be sure to adjust the macros when substituting ingredients.) Add 1 teaspoon orange zest to give the pudding a citrus kick that pairs well with cinnamon. You can also add a chocolate protein powder for a richer-tasting pudding.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 525 | Total fat 10g | Total carbohydrate 61g | Protein 44g
