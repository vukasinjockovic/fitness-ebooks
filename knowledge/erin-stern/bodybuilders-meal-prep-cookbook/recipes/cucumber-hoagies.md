# Cucumber "Hoagies"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 hoagies |
| **Serving Size** | 1 hoagie and 1 cup mixed berries |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | none |

> These make-ahead sandwiches won’t get soggy! Fresh mozzarella cheese is high in protein and helps this recipe hit a high-protein macro target. Cucumbers help with hydration and satiety and are high in antioxidants. This recipe is convenient, as there’s no cooking involved!

## Ingredients

- 4 large seedless cucumbers, each cut in half, lengthwise
- 12oz (340g) thinly sliced roasted turkey breast
- 8oz (227g) fresh mozzarella cheese
- 2–3 roasted red peppers, cut into strips
- 4 tbsp honey mustard sauce
- 4 cups mixed berries

## Directions

1. Place 3 ounces (85g) of turkey on one cucumber half, then add 2 ounces (57g) of mozzarella, followed by a few red pepper strips.
2. Spread 1 tablespoon of the honey mustard sauce over the other cucumber half and then place on top of the other ingredients. Repeat with the remaining ingredients.
3. Tightly wrap the hoagies in parchment paper to store and prevent the fillings from slipping out. Cut the wrapped hoagies in half and store in a large resealable zipper-lock storage bag in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. When ready to serve, serve with 1 cup of mixed berries to hit your carb target for the meal.

## Tips

- Change it up: If desired, you can use your favorite combination of lean cold cuts and low-fat cheeses and then pile the hoagies high with any veggies you like.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 448 | Total fat 13g | Total carbohydrate 38g | Protein 39g
