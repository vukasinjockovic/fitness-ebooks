# Dry-Rubbed Flank Steak With Sweet Potato Fries

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 5 ounces (142g) cooked steak, |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 40 minutes |

> No time to marinate your protein? No problem! Dry rubs impart big flavor in a short amount of time. This meal prep includes broccoli and sweet potatoes with the skin left intact to take advantage of all their nutrients.

## Ingredients

- 4 large sweet potatoes, cut into fries
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional)
- 1 lb (454g) flank steak
- 8 cups steamed broccoli (about 2 large heads)

**For the dry rub**
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp dried oregano

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper and place the sweet potato fries on the sheet pan in an even layer. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with salt and chili powder (if using), and then toss to coat. Roast for 35 to 40 minutes or until tender and slightly golden.
2. While the fries are roasting, in a small bowl, make the dry rub by combining the cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and oregano. Apply the rub to both sides of the steak.
3. Heat a grill or grill pan to medium high. Place the steak on the grill and cook until the desired doneness is achieved.
4. Allow the steak to cool before slicing and transferring to 4 meal prep containers with the fries and broccoli. Store in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

## Tips

- Scale up the dry rub recipe and make a big batch to keep on your spice rack.
- Change it up: You can also make this recipe with Yukon Gold potatoes and bone-in, skin-on, split chicken breasts.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 549 | Total fat 14g | Total carbohydrate 68g | Protein 42g
