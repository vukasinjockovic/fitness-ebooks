# “Neat” Joe Casserole

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Prep Time** | 20 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 35 to 40 minutes |

> This classic comfort food gets some minor tweaks to make it more bodybuilding friendly. The ingredients of this dish can be found easily and purchased on a budget. You can also easily amp up the spice levels by adding ground red pepper or hot sauce. The addition of a cornbread base means this dish will stand up to storing and reheating, unlike a traditional sloppy Joe, which gets soggy quickly after constructing.

## Ingredients


**For the cornbread**
- 1 cup cornmeal
- 1 cup quick oats
- 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
- 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
- 3 tbsp granulated sugar-free sweetener (Truvia, Swerve, or Sweet Leaf)
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp salt

**For the filling**
- 1 lb (454g) lean ground beef
- 1/2 cup diced medium onion
- 1/2 cup diced green pepper
- 1/2 cup no-sugar-added or natural ketchup
- 1 tbsp yellow mustard
- 1/2 tsp granulated garlic
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Spray a 9×9-inch (22×22cm) baking dish with cooking spray.
2. In a large bowl, combine the cornmeal, oats, Greek yogurt, almond milk, sweetener, baking powder, and salt. Mix until well incorporated. Pour into the baking dish and bake for 20 minutes.
3. While the cornbread is baking, preheat a large skillet over medium heat. When the pan is hot, add the beef and cook for 3 to 4 minutes or until well browned, while using a spatula to break up the beef. Drain off the fat and then add the onion and green pepper. Let the beef and veggies cook for another 3 to 4 minutes and then reduce the heat to low and add the ketchup, mustard, garlic, salt, black pepper, and Worcestershire sauce. Allow the beef to cook until the timer sounds for the cornbread.
4. Remove the cornbread from the oven and carefully pour the beef mixture over the top of the cornbread. Place the casserole back in the oven for an additional 15 to 20 minutes.
5. Allow to cool for 5 to 10 minutes before portioning into 4 storage containers. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or freeze for up to 3 months.

## Tips

- The prep and cook times will overlap, so this dish can be made in under 45 minutes total.
- Change it up: Depending on your macros, you can add shredded cheese to the top of the beef before placing it in the oven. For a veggie boost, add peas or carrots to the beef when adding the peppers and onions. You can also experiment with different spices: 1/2 teaspoon chili powder or cumin will lend a different flavor profile to the casserole.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 400 | Total fat 8g | Total carbohydrate 42g | Protein 40g
