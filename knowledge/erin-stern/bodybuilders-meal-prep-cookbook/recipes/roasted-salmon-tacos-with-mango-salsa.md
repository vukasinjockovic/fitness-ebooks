# Roasted Salmon Tacos With Mango Salsa

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 8 tacos |
| **Serving Size** | 2 tacos plus |
| **Prep Time** | 15 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 15 minutes |

> No need to be intimidated by cooking fish, especially when all it requires is a hot oven. Wild-caught salmon is one of the easiest types of fish to prepare, plus it's low in mercury and bursting with inflammation-fighting omega-3 fats. For a higher carb meal, serve the salmon and salsa in a rice bowl.

## Ingredients

- 1lb (454g) wild-caught salmon fillet
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- Juice of 1 lime
- 1 cup nonfat Greek yogurt, for serving
- 8 small corn tortillas, for serving

**For the salsa**
- 1 medium mango
- 1/4 cup diced red onion
- 1/2 cup diced cherry tomatoes
- 1/2 cup chopped f resh cilantro
- Juice of 1/2 lime
- 1/4 tsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp hot sauce, plus more for serving

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Place the salmon on the prepared sheet pan. Season with the salt, black pepper, and lime juice. Roast for 12 to 14 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (65°C).
3. While the salmon is cooking, make the salsa by combining the mango, onion, tomatoes, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and hot sauce in a medium bowl. Mix to combine.
4. Transfer the salmon, salsa, and Greek yogurt to 3 medium storage containers, and pack the tortillas separately. When ready to serve, add the salmon to a warmed tortilla and then top with a scoop of salsa, a dollop of Greek yogurt, and an additional dash of hot sauce (if using). Store in the refrigerator for 3 to 4 days.

## Tips

- Change it up: Make this with tilapia or cod for a slightly lower calorie meal.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 431 | Total fat 17g | Total carbohydrate 39g | Protein 32g
