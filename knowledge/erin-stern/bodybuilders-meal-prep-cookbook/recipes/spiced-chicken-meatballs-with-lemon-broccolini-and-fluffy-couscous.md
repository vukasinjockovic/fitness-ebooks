# Spiced Chicken Meatballs With Lemon Broccolini And Fluffy Couscous

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 4 meatballs, |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 25 to 30 minutes |

> Lean ground chicken is a blank canvas for all kinds of amazing flavors. Here is another oh-so-easy sheet pan meal where you can let your oven do all of the heavy lifting.

## Ingredients

- 1 1/3 cups small-grain dry couscous
- 1 tsp kosher salt, divided
- 1 3/4 cups water
- 3/4 cup cooked Arborio or other short-grain rice
- 1 large egg
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tbsp ground sumac
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/2 tsp dried dill
- 1lb (454g) ground chicken breast
- 1lb (454g) broccolini
- 1 medium lemon

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the dry couscous in an ovenproof dish, add 1/4 teaspoon salt, and then pour the water over the couscous. Cover with foil and let it sit for 5 minutes.
2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, egg, garlic powder, ground sumac, remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt, black pepper, onion powder, and dried dill. Mix well and then add the ground chicken. Continue mixing until everything is well incorporated.
3. Slice the lemon in half and then slice one of the lemon halves into thin slices. Place the broccolini in a separate large bowl and squeeze the uncut lemon half over the top of the broccolini. Lightly spray the broccolini with cooking spray and then sprinkle with the remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt.
4. Lightly spray a large sheet pan with cooking spray. Use a large spoon to scoop out even amounts of the chicken mixture. Roll the individual scoops into balls and then place them onto the prepared sheet pan, leaving room for the broccolini. (You should have around 16 evenly sized meatballs.)
5. Once you have formed the meatballs, carefully place the broccolini onto the pan around the meatballs. Place the sliced lemons over some of the broccolini bunches. Place the meatballs and couscous in the oven. Bake for 25 to 30 minutes or until the interior temperature of the meatballs reaches 165°F (75°C).
6. Remove the couscous from the oven and fluff with a fork. Transfer equal portions of the couscous and meatballs to 4 meal prep containers. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- Change it up: For a slightly different flavor profile, swap out the ground sumac for lemon pepper or coriander. You can also roast carrots or beets instead of the broccolini.
