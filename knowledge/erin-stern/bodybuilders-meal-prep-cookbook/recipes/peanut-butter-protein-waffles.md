# Peanut Butter Protein Waffles

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 1 waffle or |
| **Prep Time** | 5 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 10 minutes |

> Easy to whip up, easy to digest, and easy to pack, these waffles may just become your go-to snack. Cassava flour can be used in place of wheat flour, as it has a milder flavor, cooks well and is also high in resistant starch, which can aid in better gut health and digestion. All the ingredients are pantry items, so this recipe can be used for a meal in a pinch. Double the recipe for a week of snacks and keep the leftovers in the freezer for the following week.

## Ingredients

- 1 1/4 cup cassava flour
- 4 scoops (25–30 grams each) flavored protein powder
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1/2 cup powdered peanut butter
- 1 cup unsweetened vanilla almond milk
- 4 tbsp creamy or crunchy peanut butter, for serving

## Directions

1. Spray a waffle iron with cooking spray preheat the waffle iron.
2. Combine all the ingredients in a large bowl and mix well.
3. Pour the batter into the waffle maker until the batter just reaches all sides, then close the lid. Cook according to the waffle maker prescribed time, about 1 to 2 minutes.
4. Top each serving with 1 tablespoon of peanut butter. These waffles will store well for a few days in an airtight container at room temperature. For storage up to 1 week, place in the refrigerator. To freeze, place in a resealable freezer-safe bag and store in the freezer for up to 3 months.

## Tips

- To keep the waffles from sticking to each other, layer them in between wax paper or parchment paper. If you don’t have cassava flour, feel free to use rice flour or oat flour.
- Change it up: You can use vanilla, chocolate, or even fruit-flavored protein powder with this recipe.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 433 | Total fat 12g | Total carbohydrate 49g | Protein 37g
