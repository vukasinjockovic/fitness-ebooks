# Korean Chicken With Baby Potatoes

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 5 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 3 |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 20 minutes |

> If you’re craving unique flavors and an easy one-pan recipe, you’ll love this dish. Korean BBQ sauce adds zesty sweetness, while the cornstarch thickens the dish and makes it taste rich and decadent. Slicing the chicken thinly allows it to cook quickly and absorb more flavor. If you can’t find Korean BBQ sauce, you can use teriyaki sauce in a pinch.

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp avocado oil
- 3/4 lb (340g) baby potatoes, sliced
- 1 medium white onion, diced
- 4 tbsp Korean BBQ sauce
- 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 tbsp water
- 1 1/2 tsp cornstarch
- 1lb (454g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into 1-inch (2.5cm) strips
- 2 cups steamed broccoli

## Directions

1. Heat the oil in large pan set over medium heat. Place the potatoes in the pan, cover, and cook for 15 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes. Add the onions during the last 5 minutes of cooking. After 15 minutes, stick a knife into one of the potatoes to check for doneness. The potatoes should be soft and the onion should be translucent. When the potatoes and onions are cooked, transfer them to a large bowl and set aside.
2. Prepare the sauce by combining the Korean BBQ sauce, soy sauce, water, and cornstarch in a large bowl. Mix thoroughly to dissolve the cornstarch. Set aside.
3. Place the pan back over the heat. Add the chicken strips, cooking for 1 to 2 minutes on each side. Add the potato and onion mixture back to the pan and then pour the sauce over the mixture. Continue to cook over medium heat for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens.
4. Transfer to 5 meal prep containers with equal-size servings of the broccoli. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- This meal stores and reheats well. Reheat in the microwave or in the oven for 10 to 15 minutes.
- Change it up: Add 1/4 teaspoon cayenne or red pepper or 1 teaspoon gochugaru (Korean pepper flakes) to give this dish some fire. You can also add sliced carrots, peppers, or mushrooms for more veggie variety.
