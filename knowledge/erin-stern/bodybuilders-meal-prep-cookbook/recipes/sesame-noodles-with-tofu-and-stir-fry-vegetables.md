# Sesame Noodles With Tofu And Stir-Fry Vegetables

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | 4 servings |
| **Serving Size** | 3 |
| **Prep Time** | 15 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 25 minutes |

> There’s no need to call for takeout noodles when you can make this healthier version at home. Protein-boosted noodles and lots of tofu and veggies makes this meal extra satisfying. And it’s even better the next day, making this recipe ideal for meal prep.

## Ingredients

- 12oz (340g) high-protein pasta noodles (such as Banza or Barilla Plus)
- 2 (14oz/397g) packages extra firm tofu, drained and diced into large cubes
- 2 tsp avocado oil
- 1 (20oz/567g) package frozen mixed stir-fry vegetables (you’ll need about 4 cups)

**For the sauce**
- 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce or tamari
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)

## Directions

1. Cook the pasta according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
2. Make the sauce by whisking the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey and red pepper flakes (if using) together in a small bowl or measuring cup. Set aside.
3. In a large skillet or wok, heat the avocado oil over medium-high heat. Add the tofu and cook for 5 to 7 minutes, tossing gently until the edges begin to crisp. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.
4. Add the vegetables to the skillet and cook until they begin to soften slightly, about 10 minutes.
5. Add the noodles and sauce to the skillet with the vegetables and toss to combine. Add the tofu and cook for an additional 5 minutes, being careful not to break the pieces of tofu.
6. Transfer to 4 meal prep containers. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Tips

- To make the tofu extra crispy, drain and then press in a clean dish towel for 15 to 20 minutes before cooking.

## Nutrition (per serving)

Calories 559 | Total fat 13g | Total carbohydrate 69g | Protein 40g
