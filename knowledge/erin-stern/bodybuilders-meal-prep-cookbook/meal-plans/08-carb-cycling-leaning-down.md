# Meal Plan 8: Carb Cycling (Leaning Down)

## Summary

**Average Macros:** lower-carb days: 40P/20C/40F; higher-carb days: 40P/40C/20F
**Approximate Calories:** lower-carb days: 1,200; higher-carb days: 1,400

> Lower-carb diets can help improve insulin sensitivity and can promote feelings of satiety, while higher-carb diets can fuel muscle recovery and aid in muscle retention. Get the best of both worlds with this carb cycling plan. Feel free to move the days around to align your higher-carb days with your heavier training sessions.

## Step-by-Step Prep Plan

1. Combine the ingredients for the Chocolate Protein Truffles in a large bowl. Roll the mixture into balls and then roll the balls in the coconut as instructed. Place the truffles in a large storage container and transfer to the refrigerator. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
2. Prep the ingredients for the Greek Style Turkey Sliders . Preheat a large nonstick skillet over medium heat and spray with cooking spray. Cook the burgers as instructed and then transfer to meal prep containers to cool.
3. Prep and steam the broccoli for the sliders. (You will need a total of 4 cups for the week.) Once the broccoli has cooled, transfer it to the meal prep containers with the sliders. Add a lettuce leaf to one serving; store the buns separately until ready to serve. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
4. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Prep the ingredients for the Taco Casserole . Preheat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the filling ingredients as instructed and then layer the filling and additional ingredients into the baking dish. Cover the dish with foil and place it in the oven. Set a timer for 10 minutes.
5. While the casserole is baking, prepare the ramekins and sheet pan for the Sausage and Egg Bake . Whisk the egg bake ingredients in a large bowl and then transfer the mixture to the ramekins. Set aside.
6. When the timer for the taco casserole expires, remove the foil from the baking dish, reset the timer for an additional 20 minutes, and bake the casserole for an additional 20 minutes.
7. While the casserole is baking, prepare the sheet pan and prep the salmon for the Salmon Buddha Bowls . Set aside.
8. Prepare the rice for the Buddha bowls in a medium saucepan. (You will need a total of 1 3 / 4 cups for the week.) Set aside to cool.
9. When the timer for the casserole expires, remove the casserole from the oven and set it aside to cool. Once cooled, cut the casserole into four equal-size servings and transfer to meal prep containers. Top with the Greek yogurt and salsa. Store the apples separately until ready to serve. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
10. Place the egg bake in the oven at the same temperature, 375°F (190°C). Set a timer for 20 minutes.
11. While the egg bake is cooking, whisk together the sauce ingredients for the Buddha bowls. Place the sauce in a small storage container. Prepare the carrots and cucumber for the bowls. Set aside.
12. When the timer for the egg bake expires, remove the egg bake from the oven and increase the oven temperature to 425°F (220°C). When the egg bake has cooled, cover the ramekins with plastic wrap. Store the bread and apples separately until ready to serve. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
13. Place the salmon in the oven and set a timer for 12 minutes.
14. While the salmon is baking, whisk together the ingredients for the Peaches and Cream Greek Yogurt Bowls . Place the yogurt into sectioned meal prep containers along with the peaches and almonds. Assemble in meal prep containers when ready to serve. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
15. When the salmon is done baking, set it aside to cool. Once cooled, transfer the salmon to meal prep containers along with the rice, cucumber, and carrots. Store the sauce separately until ready to serve. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
