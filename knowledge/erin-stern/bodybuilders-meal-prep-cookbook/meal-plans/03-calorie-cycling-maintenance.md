# Meal Plan 3: Calorie Cycling (Maintenance)

## Summary

**Average Macros:** 40P/35C/25F
**Approximate Calories:** higher-calorie days: 1,850; lower-calorie days: 1,450

> Calorie cycling can be an effective strategy for maintaining weight in the long term. With higher- and lower-calorie days, you’ll average out at maintenance. But you have the ability to eat more on training days or on days where you’re more active, thus refueling your body and recovering more quickly. It can also be a good option for those coming out of a caloric deficit, as the higher-calorie days can provide a break from the diet “fatigue” that can often set in when you’ve been in a deficit for a while. Feel free to shift the days to match the higher-calorie intake days with your more active days.

## Step-by-Step Prep Plan

1. Use a rice cooker or medium saucepan to prepare the rice for the week. (You will need a total of 4 cups.)
2. Prepare the chicken for the Slow Cooker Chicken Chili by sautéing the ground chicken breast in a slow cooker or large skillet. Prepare the remaining ingredients and add to the slow cooker along with the chicken. Set the slow cooker on high for 4 hours or on low for 8 hours.
3. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Prep the ingredients for the Baked Oregano Chicken with Carrot Fries . Place the chicken and carrots on separate sheet pans, place the sheet pans in the oven, and set a timer for 25 minutes. While the chicken and carrots are baking, prepare the dipping sauce. Place the dipping sauce in a small container until ready to use.
4. Prepare the filling ingredients for the Pulled Pork Tacos . Preheat a Dutch oven over medium heat and then add the filling ingredients. Sauté the pork for 5 minutes, then cover the Dutch oven and set a second timer for 25 minutes. While the filling is simmering, prepare the slaw for the tacos in a separate large bowl and then transfer the slaw to a large storage container.
5. When the timer for the the oregano chicken expires, remove the chicken and carrot fries from the oven and set aside to cool. Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C).
6. Prepare the muffin pan and batter for the Double Protein Blueberry Muffins . Pour the batter into the prepared muffin pan, place the pan in the oven, and set a timer for 22 minutes.
7. Once the oregano chicken and carrot fries have cooled, transfer them to 4 meal prep containers. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
8. When the timer for the pulled pork tacos expires, carefully remove the pork to a cutting board, but do not turn off the heat. Use two forks to shred the pork. Once the sauce has reduced by about half, place the shredded pork back into the Dutch oven and toss the meat to coat in the sauce. Transfer the pork and sauce toa large storage container. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
9. Prepare the ingredients for the Hi-Pro Overnight Oats in a large bowl. Transfer the oats to 4 small jars with lids and then transfer to the refrigerator to set overnight. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
10. When the timer for the muffins expires, remove the muffins from the oven and transfer them to a cooling rack. Once cooled, transfer the muffins to a large storage container. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
11. Prepare the ingredients for the Savory Cottage Cheese Breakfast Bowls . Make the tomato mixture and then place the cottage cheese into 4 meal prep containers. Top with the tomato mixture or place the mixture on the side. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
12. Once the chicken chili is done cooking, transfer the chili to 4 meal prep containers. (Prep for this recipe is complete.)
