# Macronutrient Balance
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** macro balance, protein/carbs/fats ratio, macro distribution

## Summary

The strategic distribution of protein, carbohydrates, and fats to support specific physique goals. Stern's key insight: treat protein as a "fixed macro" (constant daily intake), then adjust carbs and fats in an inverse relationship based on goals and preference.

## The Three Macronutrients

### Protein (The Fixed Macro)
- **Role:** Muscle repair, growth, and maintenance
- **Target:** 1-1.5g per pound body weight
- **Sources:** Lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu
- **Key insight:** Keep protein constant; it's the foundation

### Carbohydrates (Primary Energy)
- **Role:** Fuel workouts, replenish glycogen, spare protein
- **When high:** Building muscle, heavy training days
- **When low:** Fat loss phases, rest days
- **Sources:** Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes

### Fats (Supporting Role)
- **Role:** Hormone production, vitamin absorption, energy
- **Target:** 0.2-0.5g per pound body weight
- **Sources:** Avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, fatty fish
- **Key insight:** Fats move inversely to carbs

## The Inverse Relationship

> "Picturing an inverse relationship between carbs and fats may make calculations easier. So, as you increase carb intake, fat intake should decrease, and vice versa."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

| Goal | Carbs | Fats | Protein |
|------|-------|------|---------|
| Bulk | High (45%) | Low (20%) | Fixed (35%) |
| Lean Bulk | Moderate (40%) | Low (20%) | Fixed (40%) |
| Maintenance | Variable | Variable | Fixed (35-40%) |
| Fat Loss | Lower (35%) | Moderate (25%) | High (40-45%) |

## Goal-Specific Guidelines

### Muscle Building (Surplus)
> "Make sure your caloric surplus is in the form of carbs and protein, not fats; in a caloric surplus, an excess of dietary fats is easily converted to body fat."

### Fat Loss (Deficit)
> "As long as you're in a caloric deficit, you can adjust your macros or meal plan to your liking. If you prefer higher fats, or if you prefer higher carbs, either will work."

### Recomposition (Maintenance)
- Lean toward higher carbs and protein for muscle gain stimulus
- Time carbs around training windows

## Key Quotes

> "It can be helpful to think about protein as a fixed macro, meaning that your intake should not vary much from day to day. Once you determine your ideal protein intake, it becomes easier to calculate carb and fat intake."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

> "Carbs assist in building muscle and in recovery, and can be protein-sparing. This means that a higher carb intake will help prevent the body from using protein for fuel."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1
