# The 10% Adjustment Rule
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** 10 percent rule, caloric adjustment rule, 100-300 calorie adjustment

## Summary

A practical guideline for modifying caloric intake when progress stalls: adjust by approximately 10% of total daily calories (typically 100-300 calories). This prevents overly aggressive changes that could trigger metabolic adaptation or muscle loss while ensuring meaningful progress.

## Key Principle

Small, gradual adjustments allow time to assess impact and prevent overshooting in either direction. Wait 1-2 weeks between adjustments to see effects.

— Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## Implementation

### For Fat Loss (plateau breaking)
1. Calculate 10% of current calories
2. Reduce by that amount (or 100-300 cal)
3. Wait 2 weeks before reassessing
4. Repeat if needed

**Example:** 1,500 cal → reduce by 150 cal → 1,350 cal

### For Muscle Building (if weight gain stalls)
1. Calculate 10% of current calories
2. Increase by that amount (or 100-300 cal)
3. Monitor for 2 weeks
4. Adjust based on scale and measurements

**Example:** 2,200 cal → increase by 220 cal → 2,420 cal

## Key Quotes

> "A great rule of thumb is to adjust caloric intake either upward or downward by approximately 10% or between 100 to 300 average daily calories for each adjustment."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

> "Making too many adjustments too soon may result in you not being able to pinpoint which changes worked and which did not, leading to the need for extra troubleshooting."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## When to Apply

**Trigger adjustment when:**
- No weight change for 2+ weeks (fat loss)
- No weight/measurement change for 2+ weeks (muscle gain)
- Performance declining despite recovery

**Do NOT adjust when:**
- Less than 1 week since last change
- Progress is happening, even slowly
- External factors explain plateau (stress, sleep, etc.)

## Progress Rate Guidelines

| Goal | Target Rate | Monitor |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Fat Loss | ~1% body weight/week | Scale, photos, measurements |
| Muscle Gain | ~0.5% body weight/week | Scale, measurements, waist |
| Lean Bulk | 0.5-1% body weight/week | Scale, waist vs muscle measurements |
