# Maintenance Calories
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** TDEE, total daily energy expenditure, maintenance intake

## Summary

The number of calories required to maintain current weight and physique—the baseline from which all adjustments are made. Stern provides a simple formula using body weight and activity multipliers, defaulting to a 150-lb moderately active person for recipe portions.

## Calculation Method

### Activity Multiplier Method
| Activity Level | Multiplier | Description |
|----------------|------------|-------------|
| Lightly Active | Weight × 12 | Office job, light exercise 1-2x/week |
| Moderately Active | Weight × 13 | Regular exercise 3-4x/week |
| Very Active | Weight × 14 | Intense training 5+ x/week or physical job |

**Example:** 150 lb × 13 (moderate) = 1,950 calories maintenance

— Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

### Alternative Method
Track current food intake and weight for 2 weeks:
- If weight stable → current intake = maintenance
- If gaining → current intake > maintenance
- If losing → current intake < maintenance

## Key Points

1. **BMR is the floor** - Never eat below BMR (basal metabolic rate) for extended periods
2. **Maintenance is the anchor** - All adjustments (bulk, cut) are relative to this number
3. **It changes** - Maintenance increases with muscle gain, decreases with fat loss
4. **Individual variation** - Formulas are starting points; actual needs vary

## Application to Goals

| Goal | Adjustment from Maintenance |
|------|----------------------------|
| Bulk | +250 to 500 calories |
| Lean Bulk | +100 to 300 calories |
| Maintenance/Recomp | ±0 calories |
| Fat Loss | -100 to 300 calories (10% rule) |

## Key Quotes

> "Maintenance calories are the number of calories required to maintain your current weight and physique. This is also the number you'll use for body recomposition."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

> "Don't set calories below BMR, as this can cause muscle loss and slow down your metabolism over time."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## Recipe Baseline

> "The default portion sizes in the recipes are designed for a moderately active person who weighs 150 pounds."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

This means:
- If lighter/less active: reduce portions
- If heavier/more active: increase portions
