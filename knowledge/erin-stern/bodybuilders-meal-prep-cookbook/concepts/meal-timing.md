# Meal Timing
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** nutrient timing, peri-workout nutrition, meal frequency

## Summary

The strategic scheduling of meals around training to optimize performance and recovery. Stern's key insight: pre- and post-workout meals are the two most important meals of the day, with other meal timing being largely personal preference.

## Hierarchy of Importance

1. **Post-workout meal** - Most important; highest carb meal of the day
2. **Pre-workout meal** - Second most important; fuels the session
3. **Other meals** - Distribute remaining macros based on preference

## Pre-Workout Guidelines

**Timing:** 1-2 hours before training

**Composition:**
- Balanced carbs and protein
- Easy-to-digest carbs (rice, oatmeal, sweet potatoes)
- Lean protein (chicken, egg whites, tofu)
- Low fat and fiber (slow digestion = sluggishness)

**Early Morning Training Special Case:**
> "If you train early in the morning, try to eat at least 15 to 20 grams of protein right before training. This goes a long way toward preventing muscle loss and boosting energy levels, and it can boost metabolism for the rest of the day."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## Post-Workout Guidelines

**Timing:** As soon as practical after training

**Composition:**
- High carbs (often highest carb meal of day)
- High protein
- Low fat and fiber (faster absorption)
- Easy-to-digest sources

**Purpose:**
- Replenish muscle glycogen
- Initiate protein synthesis
- Begin recovery process

## Key Quotes

> "The two most important meals of the day are your pre- and post-workout meals."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

> "Between pre- and post-workout nutrition, post workout is the most important. Ideally, it will be your highest carb meal, and often your most calorie-rich meal."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## Meal Frequency

> "The concept of meal frequency is individualized. In general, you'll want to focus on meeting your daily macronutrient and caloric goals, while spreading your meals out evenly throughout the day based on your personal preference."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

**Recommended average:** 4 meals (or 3 meals + 1 snack)

**Benefits:**
- Consistent energy throughout day
- No need to "graze" constantly
- Practical for most schedules

## Recomposition Optimization

> "For body recomposition, or recomping, you may want to lean more toward higher carbs and protein, as this can be beneficial for gaining muscle. Try to time your carb intake to be highest both before and after training to maximize results."
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1
