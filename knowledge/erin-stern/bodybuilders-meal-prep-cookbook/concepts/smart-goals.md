# SMART Goals
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** SMART criteria, goal-setting framework

## Summary

A goal-setting methodology where each goal must be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Applied to bodybuilding, this transforms vague aspirations ("get in shape") into actionable targets ("reduce body fat to 15% in 12 weeks").

## The SMART Criteria

| Letter | Criterion | Example (Bodybuilding) |
|--------|-----------|------------------------|
| **S** | Specific | "Reduce body fat" not "get in shape" |
| **M** | Measurable | "To 15%" not "look leaner" |
| **A** | Achievable | "12 weeks" (realistic) not "2 weeks" |
| **R** | Relevant | Aligns with overall physique goals |
| **T** | Time-bound | "By June 1st" not "eventually" |

— Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## Why Vague Goals Fail

> "A vague goal like 'getting in shape' is not specific and is based on self-judgment. You're essentially beating yourself up before you start and have no way of gauging what's 'good enough.'"
> — Source: The Bodybuilder's Meal Prep Cookbook, Ch.1

## Implementation for Bodybuilding

### Step 1: Assess Starting Point
- Current body weight
- Body fat percentage (DEXA, calipers, or estimation)
- Strength benchmarks
- Progress photos

### Step 2: Set Long-Term Goal
- Specific body composition target
- Timeframe (3+ months for meaningful change)

### Step 3: Break Into Mini-Goals
- Monthly or bi-weekly checkpoints
- Process goals (follow meal plan, hit training sessions)
- Outcome goals (weight, measurements)

### Step 4: Track Progress
- Weekly weigh-ins (same time, same conditions)
- Bi-weekly measurements
- Monthly progress photos

## Process vs Outcome Goals

**Process Goals** (within your control):
- Follow meal plan 90% of the time
- Hit 4 training sessions per week
- Sleep 7+ hours nightly

**Outcome Goals** (results-based):
- Reach 15% body fat
- Gain 5 lbs of muscle
- Reduce waist by 2 inches

Emphasize process goals—they lead to outcomes.
