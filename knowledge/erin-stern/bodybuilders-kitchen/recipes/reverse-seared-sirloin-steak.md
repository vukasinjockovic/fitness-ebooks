# REVERSE-SEARED SIRLOIN STEAK

**Category:** Entrées
**Source:** The Bodybuilder's Kitchen by Erin Stern (2018)

> This simple, yet flavorful steak is protein-packed and contains zero carbs! The steak is cooked in the oven at low temperature to ensure even cooking, and a quick sear on the stovetop seals in the juices. The end result is a beautifully crusted steak that is moist, tender, and delicious.

## Quick Info

- **Servings:** 4
- **Serving Size:** 4oz (120g)
- **Prep Time:** 30 minutes
- **Cook Time:** 45–60 minutes

## Ingredients

- 1lb (450g) sirloin steak
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp ground black pepper

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 275°F (135°C). Place an oven-safe cooling rack atop a large, foil-lined baking sheet.

2. Season both sides of the steak with salt and pepper, and allow to rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.

3. Place the steak on the cooling rack and place in the oven. Bake for 30 minutes if the steak is 1 to 2 inches thick (2.5 to 5cm), or 45 minutes if the steak is 3 to 4 inches thick (7.5 to 10cm).

4. Remove the steak from the oven and allow to rest for 15 minutes.

5. Spray a large cast iron skillet with coconut oil cooking spray and preheat over high heat. Place the steak in the skillet and sear for approximately 1 minute per side. The steak is done when it’s pink in the middle and the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C). Serve hot.

## Prep Tips

- Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

## Variations

For a Southwestern twist, season the steak with ½ tsp garlic powder, ½ tsp cumin, and ½ tsp paprika.
