# Quotable Quotes from Fat Loss Forever
**Authors:** Layne Norton, PhD & Peter Baker
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03

## On Diet Failure

> "While people are good at losing weight, they are not good at keeping the weight off. It's not because of the weight loss. It's because they gain it all back."
— Foreword

> "Within three years of weight loss, 95% of people will have gained it all back. Thus, the success rate of diets is ~5%, and most are equally terrible."
— Foreword

> "Out of the people who regain weight, one-third to two-thirds will add back more weight than they originally lost. Not only do they fail to maintain their weight loss, but they end up worse than before they ever started dieting."
— Foreword

## On Sustainability

> "If you can't see yourself sticking to your diet one year from now, you need to re-think your strategy. If you don't like the way you eat, you won't stick to it for long, and it'll only be a matter of time before you jump ship and end up regaining all the weight."
— Sohee Lee, Chapter 1 epigraph

> "Sustainability and dietary adherence are by far the most important factors in losing weight and keeping it off."
— Ch. 4

> "People don't fail diets because they didn't have the magic ratio of protein, carbohydrates, and fats. They fail because they can't sustain them."
— Ch. 8

## On Energy Balance

> "Calories actually refer to the energy that can be extracted from the nutrients (food) you consume."
— Ch. 2

> "If you don't lose weight, then you weren't in a caloric deficit."
— Ch. 2 Summary

> "Many people believe CICO doesn't work because they ate at what 'should have been' a caloric deficit based on calculations, but they didn't lose weight. This doesn't invalidate CICO but rather means that their energy expenditure is lower than predicted, or they are underestimating their intake or overestimating their expenditure, or both."
— Ch. 2 Summary

## On Metabolism

> "When we refer to someone who has a 'fast metabolism,' we mean that their body is inefficient at production and usage of ATP on a cellular level, and that they end up wasting a lot of potential energy. These people are the Ferraris of metabolism."
— Ch. 3

> "Someone with an efficient (slow) metabolism is able to waste less ATP and produce more ATP from less energy intake, meaning fat storage is easier for this person. Think of these people as the Prius of metabolisms."
— Ch. 3

## On Flexible Dieting

> "Your diet is neverending. The Skittles you eat off the floor of Denny's, the beers you pounded before you made that terrible choice to eat off the floor, the hot skillet they bring you, all the way to Thanksgiving dinner—all of these comprise the totality of your diet."
— Ch. 5

> "Think of your macros as a budget. If you're someone with a fast metabolism who can lose weight on a substantial amount of calories, you have a bigger budget."
— Ch. 5

## On Cognitive Restraint

> "The most common trait of people who lose weight and keep it off is that they practice some form of cognitive restraint."
— Ch. 4

> "While it's important to employ a strategy that requires minimal willpower and sacrifice to be effective, every diet will require some level of willpower and sacrifice. You'll need to decide what you're willing to sacrifice."
— Ch. 4

## On Supplements

> "The people who spend the most money on supplements usually get the least results. Not because all supplements are bunk (most are), but because the people who spend the most cash on supplements are also those people who tend to focus on quick fixes rather than long-term, real solutions."
— Ch. 15

> "You don't need any supplements. The very definition of 'supplement' implies that it's not needed. However, some of them can be helpful."
— Ch. 15

## On the Self Defense System

> "The body's metabolic rate drops significantly more than you would expect based on how much weight you lose in an overreaction to the imposed energy restriction."
— Ch. 9

> "Do not crash diet. The more slowly you lose the weight, the more lean body mass you will retain."
— Ch. 9

## On Post-Diet

> "If you go immediately back to your pre-diet maintenance calories (which will be higher than your actual maintenance calories at this point), then you'll gain a good amount of weight back."
— Ch. 11

## On Truth in Nutrition

> "Hopefully, after learning more about fat loss and regain physiology, you understand how ridiculous it is to expect a simple solution to such a complex problem."
— Ch. 16

> "Be tenacious. Be persistent. Be patient. Be consistent. Be committed. NEVER QUIT."
— Ch. 16
