# Flexible Dieting / IIFYM
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** If It Fits Your Macros, IIFYM, The Macro Diet, Macro Counting

## Summary

A dietary approach where no foods are inherently off-limits; instead, daily intake is managed by tracking macronutrient targets (protein, carbs, fat). The philosophy emphasizes that the source of calories matters less than the total intake and macro distribution. This approach prioritizes sustainability through flexibility and psychological freedom around food choices.

## Core Principles

### 1. No Food Restriction
- No foods labeled "good" or "bad"
- All foods can fit within macro targets
- Eliminates diet-induced food guilt
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.5

### 2. Macros as a Budget
> "Think of your macros as a budget. If you're someone with a fast metabolism who can lose weight on a substantial amount of calories, you have a bigger budget."
— Ch. 5

### 3. Total Diet Perspective
> "Your diet is neverending... all of these comprise the totality of your diet. Your diet is not something you engage in solely to lose weight."
— Ch. 5

### 4. Prioritize Sustainability
- Choose foods you enjoy
- Build habits, not temporary restrictions
- Social flexibility (restaurants, events)
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.5

## How It Works

1. **Calculate TDEE** - Total Daily Energy Expenditure
2. **Set calorie target** - Based on goal (deficit for fat loss)
3. **Determine protein** - First priority (1.6-2.4g/kg)
4. **Allocate remaining calories** - To carbs and fats based on preference
5. **Track intake** - Using app, scale, or estimation
6. **Adjust as needed** - Based on progress
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.6, Ch.8

## Why It Supports Adherence

| Factor | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| No forbidden foods | Reduces cravings and binge risk |
| Quantified feedback | Know exactly what to adjust |
| Social flexibility | Can eat out, attend events |
| Food choice autonomy | Respects preferences |
| Long-term viability | Sustainable lifestyle |
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.4, Ch.5

## Common Misconceptions

### "IIFYM means eating junk food"
**Reality:** While possible, most practitioners naturally gravitate toward nutrient-dense foods to hit protein/fiber targets while staying within calories.

### "All calories are created equal"
**Reality:** The book acknowledges different thermic effects, satiety, and nutrient density—but for weight loss specifically, the calorie total matters most.
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.14

## Practical Considerations

**Downsides:**
- Requires tracking (time investment)
- Learning curve for estimation
- May not suit those with disordered eating history
- Still requires portion awareness

**Best for:**
- People who dislike food rules
- Those who've failed restrictive diets
- Anyone wanting data-driven approach
- Long-term lifestyle change seekers
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.5

## Key Quotes

> "We love flexible dieting and find it to be the most sustainable nutrition strategy for many people, because no foods are restricted. You are only restricted by your macronutrient intake."
— Ch. 5

> "If you're following a program like 'clean eating'... what do you do if you stop losing weight? Eat cleaner? ...These dietary strategies don't offer an objective way to make adjustments."
