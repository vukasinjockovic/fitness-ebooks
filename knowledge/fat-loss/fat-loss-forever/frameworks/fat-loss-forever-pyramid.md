# Fat Loss Forever Pyramid
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** FLF Pyramid, Norton Pyramid, Fat Loss Pyramid

## Summary

A hierarchical framework for prioritizing fat loss factors, adapted from Eric Helms' Muscle and Strength Pyramid. It ranks seven factors from most to least important, with sustainability and adherence at the base (most important) and supplements at the top (least important). The key insight is that behavioral factors trump physiological optimization.

## The Seven Levels (Bottom to Top)

### Level 1: Sustainability and Adherence (MOST IMPORTANT)
- If you can't sustain it, nothing else matters
- The best diet is the one you can stick to
- Consider lifestyle fit, food preferences, social factors
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.4

### Level 2: Caloric Deficit
- Energy balance determines weight loss
- Must create negative energy balance
- Target 0.5-1% body weight loss per week
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2, Ch.6

### Level 3: Exercise
- Resistance training preserves lean mass
- Helps lower defended body weight set point
- Increases TDEE through NEAT and EAT
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.4

### Level 4: Self-Monitoring
- Weighing frequently (daily recommended)
- Tracking intake (macros or calories)
- Progress photos and measurements
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.4, Ch.10

### Level 5: Protein & Fiber Intake
- High protein (1.6-2.4g/kg) preserves muscle
- Higher satiety from protein and fiber
- Greater thermic effect of food
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.7, Ch.8

### Level 6: Carbs & Fat Distribution
- When protein is set, ratio matters less
- Personal preference drives adherence
- No metabolic advantage to either extreme
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.7, Ch.8

### Level 7: Supplements (LEAST IMPORTANT)
- Only after all other factors optimized
- Minimal impact on outcomes
- Most supplements ineffective
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.15

## Key Quotes

> "Ironically, not one specific diet has emerged as the clear 'winner' in people who have lost weight and kept it off. Instead, patterns of behavior have become indicators of sustainable weight loss."
— Ch. 4
