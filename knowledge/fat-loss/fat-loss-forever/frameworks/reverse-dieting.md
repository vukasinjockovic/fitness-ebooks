# Reverse Dieting
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Post-Diet Protocol, Metabolic Recovery, Calorie Restoration

## Summary

A systematic approach to increasing calories after a fat loss phase to restore metabolic rate while minimizing fat regain. Rather than immediately returning to pre-diet maintenance calories (which would cause rapid weight regain due to metabolic adaptation), calories are increased gradually over weeks to months. This allows the body's self-defense system to recalibrate.

## The Problem It Solves

After extended dieting:
- Metabolic rate is suppressed below predicted levels
- Leptin and thyroid hormones are reduced
- NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis) is diminished
- Pre-diet maintenance calories now cause surplus

> "If you go immediately back to your pre-diet maintenance calories (which will be higher than your actual maintenance calories at this point), then you'll gain a good amount of weight back."
— Ch. 11

## Protocol Overview

### Phase 1: Identify Current State
- Note current calorie intake at diet end
- Assess metabolic markers (energy, mood, performance)
- Accept that some weight regain is normal and expected
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

### Phase 2: Gradual Increase
**Typical approach:**
- Add 50-150 calories per week
- Primarily from carbohydrates (leptin response)
- Monitor weight and biofeedback
- Continue until reaching new maintenance

**Duration:**
- Varies based on diet length and severity
- Often takes as long as the diet itself
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

### Phase 3: Stabilization
- Find new sustainable maintenance level
- Weight will stabilize (may be slightly higher than diet end)
- Metabolic rate should recover significantly
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

## Expected Outcomes

| Outcome | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| Some weight regain | Normal; includes glycogen, water, food volume |
| NEAT increase | Body becomes less "efficient" |
| Mood/energy improvement | Hormone normalization |
| Hunger reduction | Leptin recovery |
| Performance recovery | Training capacity restored |
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

## Key Decision Points

### Should I Reverse Diet or Just Eat More?

**Reverse Diet If:**
- Extended diet (12+ weeks)
- Very low calories at diet end
- Significant metabolic adaptation signs
- Want to minimize fat regain

**Can Skip If:**
- Short diet phase
- Didn't diet very aggressively
- Accepting of faster regain
- Just want to return to normal eating
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

## Self-Assessment Questions

From the book, ask yourself:
1. "Am I happy with how I'm eating in the context of my lifestyle?"
2. "Can I keep living this way?"

If yes to both → can maintain current calories
If no → need to reverse diet or accept some regain
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11
