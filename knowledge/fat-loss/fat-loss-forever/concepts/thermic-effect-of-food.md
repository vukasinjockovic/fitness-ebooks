# Thermic Effect of Food (TEF)
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** TEF, Diet-Induced Thermogenesis, DIT, Specific Dynamic Action

## Summary

The energy expended to digest, absorb, and metabolize food. TEF accounts for approximately 8-15% of total daily energy expenditure and varies significantly by macronutrient. Protein has the highest TEF (20-35%), followed by carbohydrates (5-15%), then fats (0-5%). This is why high-protein diets have a slight thermogenic advantage—more calories are "burned" just processing the food.

## TEF by Macronutrient

| Macronutrient | TEF Range | Explanation |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| Protein | 20-35% | Complex digestion, amino acid metabolism |
| Carbohydrate | 5-15% | Moderate processing requirements |
| Fat | 0-5% | Efficient absorption, minimal processing |
| Alcohol | ~15% | Significant metabolic processing |
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2

## Practical Implications

### Why Protein Matters for Fat Loss

> "Not all sources of calories are created equal for the thermic effect of food since higher protein intakes have demonstrated better fat loss and LBM retention."
— Ch. 2 Summary

**Example calculation:**
- 100 calories from protein → ~25 calories used in TEF → ~75 net calories
- 100 calories from fat → ~3 calories used in TEF → ~97 net calories
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2

### The "Protein Advantage"

At equal calorie intake:
1. Higher protein = more calories "burned" via TEF
2. Higher protein = better satiety
3. Higher protein = better lean mass retention

This is why the book recommends setting protein first when planning macros.
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.7, Ch.8

## Components of TDEE

TEF is one of four components:

```
TDEE = BMR + NEAT + TEF + EAT

BMR:  60-70% (Basal Metabolic Rate)
NEAT: 15-30% (Non-Exercise Activity)
TEF:  8-15%  (Thermic Effect of Food)
EAT:  5-10%  (Exercise Activity)
```
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2

## TEF and Whole Foods

While not extensively covered, the book implies:
- Whole foods may have higher TEF than processed equivalents
- This is due to greater digestive processing required
- But the difference is relatively small in practical terms
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.14

## Why It's Not a Magic Bullet

Despite TEF differences:
- Total caloric balance still determines weight change
- TEF advantage from protein is meaningful but modest
- You can't "hack" your way to fat loss via TEF alone
- It's one factor among many (hence Level 5 on pyramid, not Level 1)
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.4
