# Energy Balance / CICO
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Calories In Calories Out, CICO, Energy Equation, Caloric Balance

## Summary

The fundamental principle governing weight change: body weight is determined by the relationship between energy consumed (calories in) and energy expended (calories out). A negative energy balance (deficit) causes weight loss; a positive balance (surplus) causes weight gain. While often oversimplified, this thermodynamic principle remains the foundation of all successful fat loss approaches regardless of dietary method.

## The Equation

```
Weight Change = Energy In - Energy Out

Deficit (negative balance) → Weight Loss
Surplus (positive balance) → Weight Gain
Maintenance (balance) → Weight Stable
```

## Components of "Calories In"

All energy consumed through food and drink:
- Protein: 4 kcal/gram
- Carbohydrates: 4 kcal/gram
- Fat: 9 kcal/gram
- Alcohol: 7 kcal/gram
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2, Ch.7

## Components of "Calories Out" (TDEE)

**Total Daily Energy Expenditure:**

| Component | % of TDEE | Description |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| BMR | 60-70% | Basal Metabolic Rate - energy at complete rest |
| NEAT | 15-30% | Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis |
| TEF | 8-15% | Thermic Effect of Food |
| EAT | 5-10% | Exercise Activity Thermogenesis |
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2, Ch.3

## Key Clarifications

### "CICO Doesn't Work for Me"
> "Many people believe CICO doesn't work because they ate at what 'should have been' a caloric deficit based on calculations, but they didn't lose weight. This doesn't invalidate CICO but rather means that their energy expenditure is lower than predicted, or they are underestimating their intake or overestimating their expenditure, or both."
— Ch. 2 Summary

### The Law Still Applies
> "If you don't lose weight, then you weren't in a caloric deficit."
— Ch. 2 Summary

### Not All Calories Are Equal (But Still Calories)
The book acknowledges differences in:
- Thermic effect (protein > carbs > fat)
- Satiety effects
- Hormonal responses
- Micronutrient content

But emphasizes: for weight loss specifically, total energy balance is deterministic.
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2, Ch.14

## What Is a Calorie?

> "Calories actually refer to the energy that can be extracted from the nutrients (food) you consume... One calorie is the amount of energy it takes to heat up one gram of water by one degree celsius."
— Ch. 2

Note: Food "calories" are actually kilocalories (1000 calories), but convention uses them interchangeably.

## Practical Implications

1. **Any diet that works** does so by creating energy deficit
2. **Method doesn't matter** for weight loss (low carb, low fat, keto, etc.)
3. **Tracking helps** but isn't required if deficit exists
4. **Calculations are estimates** - adjust based on results
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2, Ch.4

## Key Quotes

> "What ultimately determines weight loss is how much energy you consume versus how much energy you expend."
— Ch. 2

> "Energy balance is the most important factor for producing weight loss. To that end, you can accomplish weight loss through any number of different diet strategies."
