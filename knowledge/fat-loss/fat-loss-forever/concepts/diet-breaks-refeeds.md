# Diet Breaks and Refeeds
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Refeeding, Structured Overfeed, Diet Holiday, Maintenance Phase

## Summary

Strategic periods of higher calorie intake during a fat loss phase, designed to temporarily restore leptin levels, boost metabolic rate, and provide psychological relief. Refeeds are short (1-3 days) increases primarily in carbohydrates, while diet breaks are longer (1-2+ weeks) periods at maintenance calories. Both serve as counter-strategies to the body's Self Defense System.

## Refeeds

### What They Are
- Short-term (typically 1-3 days)
- Controlled caloric increase
- Primarily from carbohydrates
- Usually at or slightly above maintenance
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

### Mechanism
> "Carbohydrates—specifically glucose—have a much greater impact on leptin production than fats or protein."
— Ch. 9

Carb refeeds work by:
1. Temporarily raising leptin levels
2. Replenishing glycogen stores
3. Boosting thyroid hormones
4. Providing psychological relief
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

### Important Caveat

> "While refeeds have been shown to increase metabolic rate in the short term, there is no data showing that they actually improve fat loss during a diet."
— Ch. 9

**Translation:** Refeeds may not accelerate fat loss, but they may improve:
- Adherence (psychological benefit)
- Training performance (glycogen)
- Overall diet sustainability

### Implementation
- Frequency: 1-2x per week depending on body fat level
- Magnitude: Bring calories to maintenance or slightly above
- Composition: Primarily carbohydrate increase
- Duration: 1-3 days
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

## Diet Breaks

### What They Are
- Extended periods (1-2+ weeks)
- Eating at maintenance calories
- All macros may increase
- Full pause from deficit
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

### When to Use
- Longer diets (12+ weeks)
- Significant metabolic adaptation signs
- Declining performance/energy
- Psychological fatigue
- Hitting stubborn plateaus
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

### Benefits
1. **Metabolic:** More complete hormone recovery
2. **Psychological:** Mental break from restriction
3. **Performance:** Training quality restoration
4. **Adherence:** Prevents burnout
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

### Evidence Note
The book acknowledges mixed evidence but suggests practical benefits exist, particularly for adherence and long-term sustainability.

## Comparison

| Aspect | Refeed | Diet Break |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Duration | 1-3 days | 1-2+ weeks |
| Frequency | Weekly | Every 4-12 weeks |
| Calorie level | At/above maintenance | At maintenance |
| Primary macro | Carbohydrates | All macros |
| Main benefit | Leptin spike, psychological | Full recovery |
| Fat loss impact | Minimal/none | Pauses fat loss |
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

## Practical Considerations

### Who Benefits Most
- Leaner individuals (more aggressive defense)
- Longer dieters
- Those with high stress
- Performance-focused athletes
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.9

### Common Mistakes
1. Using as excuse for binge
2. Too frequent (preventing deficit)
3. Too infrequent (burnout)
4. Not returning to deficit properly

## Key Quotes

> "While there's no way to completely eliminate the self-defense system (that would require no energy gap, meaning you also wouldn't lose fat), we do have a few tools that we can use to mitigate the effects."
— Ch. 9
