## Summary

Dominic D'Agostino is a tenured Associate Professor at the University of South Florida Morsani College of Medicine, specializing in neuroscience, molecular pharmacology, and physiology. He is a leading researcher in ketogenic diet science, exogenous ketones, and metabolic therapies. He contributed Chapter 12 (The Ketogenic Diet) to Fat Loss Forever alongside Kristi Storoschuk.

## Background

- **Institution:** University of South Florida
- **Department:** Molecular Pharmacology and Physiology
- **Research focus:**
  - Ketogenic diets and metabolic therapies
  - Exogenous ketones
  - Nutritional approaches to neurological disorders
  - Metabolic health optimization
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.12

## Contribution to Fat Loss Forever

### Chapter 12: The Ketogenic Diet

Co-authored with Kristi Storoschuk, this chapter provides:
- Balanced, evidence-based view of ketogenic dieting
- Historical context of keto (from epilepsy treatment)
- Implementation guidelines
- Distinction from "fad diet" messaging
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.12

### Key Points from Chapter

> "We aren't trying to convince you that the ketogenic diet is a magic pill or that it's for everyone; we simply want to provide a different perspective to a strategy that many could benefit from."
— Ch. 12

**Core message:**
- Keto is a valid option, not magic
- Energy deficit still drives fat loss
- May work well for some people's adherence
- Has clinical applications beyond weight loss
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.12

## Key Findings Presented

### Historical Context
- 1920s: Ketogenic diet developed for epilepsy
- Based on fasting physiology
- Therapeutic ketosis without starvation
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.12

### Clinical Applications
- Epilepsy (original use)
- Neurodegenerative diseases
- Potential metabolic benefits
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.12

### Fat Loss Perspective
> "It doesn't appear that the ketogenic diet is superior for weight/fat loss when calories and protein are equated."
— Ch.12 (echoing main book theme)

But: "The way we see it, the ketogenic diet may be a practical option to create a desired calorie deficit."
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.12
