# Fat Loss Forever
**Authors:** Layne Norton, PhD & Peter Baker
**Year:** 2018
**Domain:** Fat Loss, Nutrition, Body Composition

## Overview

Fat Loss Forever addresses the 95% failure rate of diets by focusing on sustainability, adherence, and the body's physiological responses to weight loss. Rather than promoting a specific diet, it emphasizes behavioral patterns and evidence-based strategies for long-term weight maintenance.

## Core Thesis

The primary problem with fat loss isn't losing weight—most people can do that. The problem is keeping it off. The book addresses the body's "Self Defense System" (metabolic adaptation, leptin resistance, set point changes) and provides strategies to mitigate weight regain.

## Structure

The book uses the **Fat Loss Forever Pyramid** (adapted from Eric Helms) to prioritize factors:
1. Sustainability and Adherence (most important)
2. Caloric Deficit
3. Exercise
4. Self-Monitoring
5. Protein & Fiber Intake
6. Carbs & Fat Macros
7. Supplements (least important)

## Key Contributions

- **Yo-yo dieting analysis**: Explains why 95% of dieters regain weight within 3 years
- **Metabolic adaptation mechanics**: Details how the body fights weight loss
- **Flexible dieting approach**: IIFYM as sustainable strategy
- **Post-diet protocols**: Reverse dieting and maintenance strategies
- **Debunking nutrition myths**: Evidence-based critique of fad diets
