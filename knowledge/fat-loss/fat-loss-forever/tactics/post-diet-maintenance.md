# Post-Diet Maintenance
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Weight Maintenance, Diet Exit Strategy, Post-Diet Protocol

## Summary

The critical phase after active fat loss where the goal shifts from losing weight to maintaining the new weight. This is where 95% of dieters fail. The book emphasizes that post-diet maintenance requires ongoing attention, as the body's Self Defense System is actively working to restore lost weight. Success requires accepting that "maintenance" calories are now lower than pre-diet levels.

## The Core Problem

> "Your current maintenance calories at the end of a diet will not be the same as your starting maintenance calories."
— Ch. 11

**Why this happens:**
- Metabolic adaptation (reduced TDEE)
- Lower body mass (less to maintain)
- Hormonal changes (leptin, thyroid)
- NEAT reduction
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

## Decision Framework

### Two Self-Assessment Questions

1. "Am I happy with how I'm eating in the context of my lifestyle?"
2. "Can I keep living this way?"

**If YES to both:** Maintain current intake
**If NO:** Need to increase calories (accept some regain or reverse diet)
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

## Post-Diet Paths

### Path 1: Maintain Current Intake

**When appropriate:**
- You're comfortable with current eating
- Sustainable long-term
- Meeting all nutritional needs
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

**How:**
- Continue tracking (cognitive restraint)
- Keep weighing regularly
- Maintain training routine
- Make small adjustments as needed

### Path 2: Return to Pre-Diet Maintenance

**Warning:**
> "If you go immediately back to your pre-diet maintenance calories (which will be higher than your actual maintenance calories at this point), then you'll gain a good amount of weight back."
— Ch. 11

**Outcomes:**
- Rapid weight regain likely
- May regain more than lost (overshoot)
- Body stabilizes once LBM is restored
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

**When acceptable:**
- Lost significant weight (keeping most is still a win)
- Prioritizing quality of life over perfection
- Can't sustain lower intake

### Path 3: Reverse Diet

**When to use:**
- Long or aggressive diet
- Very low calories at end
- Want to minimize regain
- Symptoms of metabolic adaptation
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

**Protocol:**
- Increase calories gradually (50-150/week)
- Primarily from carbohydrates
- Monitor weight and biofeedback
- Accept some weight regain
- Continue until new maintenance found

See: [Reverse Dieting](../frameworks/reverse-dieting.md)

### Path 4: Continue Dieting

**When appropriate:**
- Didn't reach goal weight
- Still have energy/adherence
- No severe adaptation signs
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

**Consider:**
- Taking a diet break first
- Reassessing rate of loss
- Checking if goal is realistic

## Critical Success Factors

### 1. Continued Self-Monitoring
- Keep weighing regularly
- Track intake (at least loosely)
- Don't abandon all cognitive restraint
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.10, Ch.11

### 2. Maintain Exercise Routine
> "Training will lower the set point that your body defends."
— Ch. 9

### 3. Accept the New Reality
- Pre-diet "normal" eating won't work
- Some restriction is permanent
- This is lifestyle change, not temporary diet
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11

### 4. Plan for Challenges
- Holidays
- Vacations
- Stressful periods
- Social events
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.4

## Expected Weight Changes

**Normal post-diet increases:**
- Water retention (especially with carb increase)
- Glycogen replenishment
- Food volume in gut
- Small fat regain

**Not normal:**
- Rapid return to starting weight
- Gaining more than lost
- Complete loss of control
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.11
