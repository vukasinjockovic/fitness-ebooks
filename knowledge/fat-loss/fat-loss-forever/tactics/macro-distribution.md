# Macro Distribution
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Macro Setup, Macronutrient Partitioning, Setting Macros

## Summary

The process of distributing total daily calories across protein, carbohydrates, and fats. The book recommends setting protein first (based on lean body mass), then distributing remaining calories between carbs and fats according to personal preference. This approach prioritizes the macronutrient with the strongest evidence for fat loss benefits (protein) while maintaining flexibility for adherence.

## Priority Order

1. **Protein** - Set first (most important for body composition)
2. **Fiber** - Ensure adequate intake (satiety, health)
3. **Carbs & Fats** - Distribute remaining calories by preference
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.8

## Step 1: Set Protein

### Protein Recommendations

| Population | Protein Target |
|------------|---------------|
| General | 1.6-2.2 g/kg body weight |
| Dieting/Active | 2.0-2.4 g/kg body weight |
| Lean/Aggressive deficit | Up to 2.7-3.1 g/kg |
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.7, Ch.8

### Key Principle

> "Of the three macronutrients, protein is the most important, because it improves retention of LBM, satiety, and fat loss."
— Ch. 8

### Protein Benefits
- Preserves lean body mass during deficit
- Highest thermic effect (20-35%)
- Greatest satiety effect
- Supports recovery from training
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.7

## Step 2: Set Fiber

**Recommendation:** 10-15g fiber per 1000 calories
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.8

**Benefits:**
- Increases satiety
- Supports gut health
- Slows digestion
- Minimal caloric contribution

## Step 3: Distribute Remaining Calories

After protein calories are subtracted:

```
Remaining Calories = Total Calories - (Protein g × 4)
```

These can be divided between carbs and fats based on:
- Personal preference
- Food enjoyment
- Activity demands
- Medical considerations
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.8

### The Flexibility Principle

> "When calories and protein are equated, there does not appear to be a significant difference in fat loss between diets varying in carbohydrate and fat intake."
— Ch. 2 Summary

**This means:**
- Low carb + high fat = works
- High carb + low fat = works
- Moderate both = works

**What matters:** Sustainability and adherence
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.2, Ch.8

### Minimum Fats

The book doesn't specify a hard minimum but implies:
- Fat is essential for hormones
- Very low fat (<15-20% calories) may affect hormones
- Some dietary fat needed for nutrient absorption
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.7

### Carb Considerations

- Supports training performance
- Higher thermic effect than fat
- Important for glycogen replenishment
- May have slight advantage per Hall study
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.7

## Worked Example

**Target: 2000 calories, 80kg person, active**

1. **Protein:** 2.2 g/kg × 80kg = 176g = 704 calories
2. **Remaining:** 2000 - 704 = 1296 calories
3. **Distribution options:**
   - High carb: 250g carbs (1000 cal) + 33g fat (296 cal)
   - Moderate: 200g carbs (800 cal) + 55g fat (496 cal)
   - Low carb: 100g carbs (400 cal) + 100g fat (896 cal)
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.8

## Summary Algorithm

From Chapter 8:
1. Determine maintenance calories
2. Determine required deficit
3. Subtract deficit from maintenance
4. Determine protein intake
5. Subtract protein calories from total
6. Choose carb/fat distribution by preference
7. Consume 10-15g fiber per 1000 calories
— Source: Fat Loss Forever, Ch.8 Summary
