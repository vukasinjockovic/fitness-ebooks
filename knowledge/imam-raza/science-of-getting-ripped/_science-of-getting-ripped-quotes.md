# Quotable Quotes from The Science of Getting Ripped

**Author:** Raza Imam
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04

## On Training Philosophy

> "Fitness is intensity dependent, not time dependent."

> "It's not how long you're at the gym that counts, it's how you spend your time in the gym that matters."

> "It's not the years of kung fu, it's the kung fu in the years."

> "Train hard. But Safe. But hard." — Craig Ballantyne

## On Adaptation and Variation

> "Everything works" - All training methods have merit.

> "Nothing works forever" - Body adapts, requiring variation.

> "Your body is extremely smart and it adapts over time, so in order to get stronger, you have to always increase the weight or vary your exercise."

## On Progressive Overload

> "Every time you work out, you should be adding a little bit more weight to ensure that you are progressing properly and that your muscles are sufficiently stimulated to trigger more growth or to trigger greater fat loss."

> "If you find that you are reaching a plateau... that's perfectly natural. Your body will naturally plateau at a certain point. We can't continue to grow muscle and gain strength forever."

## On Strength vs Size

> "This is why huge guys with big muscles aren't as strong as smaller power lifters."

> "One of the biggest myths I held to be true was that if you want to build muscle, you should lift heavy weights with low repetitions. And that if you want to 'get cut' you should lift lighter weights with high repetitions... In fact, it's the total opposite, and the ONLY way to 'get cut' is to achieve low body-fat levels."

## On Getting Abs

> "Abs are made in the kitchen, not in the gym."

> "Contrary to popular belief, having six-pack abs does not mean that you have a strong core. It simply means that you have very little belly fat."

## On High Intensity Work

> "Performing any exercises or workout at a high intensity is extremely hard and requires a great level of focus which is why most people just don't do it."

> "When you're doing a circuit training type of workout with heavy weights, your time in the gym will be very efficient and very well-used. So you won't have time to chat up the ladies, to flip through fitness magazines, or to play with your iPhone. You're going to be there at the gym for one reason, and one reason only, and that's to work!"

## On the Afterburn Effect

> "The 'afterburn effect' is when your body makes up for this debt, which burns calories for up to 48 hours after the high intensity activity."

> "Short, high-intensity interval training has been proved to burn calories for up to 48 hours after working out; meaning you can burn calories doing nothing."

## On Circuit Training

> "Performing functional, full-body, weight-bearing exercises in a high-intensity method, like supersets or circuit training, is the single most effective way to develop a lean muscular build and to literally burn and melt fat from your body."

## On Intervals

> "The key to making intervals work is the 'interval' part where you switch between the two extremes of intense activity and rest."

> "If you don't rest between the intervals you won't be able to sustain the intensity and subsequently won't get the benefits."

## On Nutrition

> "Give me a one-page bullet-list of exactly what I should do. That's worth more to me than a stack of books that I have to dig through to get to the good stuff." — Alwyn Cosgrove

> "If you eat right 85% to 95% of the time, you'll see great results."

> "Professional models diet down, get airbrushed, get all kinds of implants... so don't feel like you have to follow a meal plan perfectly."
