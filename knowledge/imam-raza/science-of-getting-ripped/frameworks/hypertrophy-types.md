# Hypertrophy Types: Myofibrillar vs Sarcoplasmic

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Types of muscle growth, strength vs size training, functional vs cosmetic hypertrophy

## Summary

Distinguishes between two types of muscle growth: myofibrillar hypertrophy (strength-focused, increasing muscle fiber density) and sarcoplasmic hypertrophy (size-focused, increasing fluid volume in muscle cells). This explains why huge bodybuilders aren't always as strong as smaller powerlifters—they've trained for different adaptations.

## The Two Types

### Myofibrillar Hypertrophy (Strength)
**What it is:** Increasing the number and density of muscle fibers (myofibrils)

**Training characteristics:**
- Heavy weights
- Low repetitions (1-5 reps)
- Trains nervous system efficiency
- Recruits more muscle fibers

**Result:** Stronger muscles without proportional size increase

**Mechanism:**
> "When you lift a light weight, only a small amount of fibers are activated. The heavier the weight becomes, the more fibers get 'recruited' into action."

— Source: Science of Getting Ripped, Training Principle 1

### Sarcoplasmic Hypertrophy (Size)
**What it is:** Increasing the fluid (sarcoplasm) within muscle cells

**Training characteristics:**
- Moderate weights
- Higher repetitions (8-12+ reps)
- Metabolic stress focus
- Time under tension emphasis

**Result:** Larger muscles without proportional strength increase

## The Key Insight

> "This is why huge guys with big muscles aren't as strong as smaller power lifters."

**Common myth debunked:**
> "One of the biggest myths I held to be true was that if you want to build muscle, you should lift heavy weights with low repetitions. And that if you want to 'get cut' you should lift lighter weights with high repetitions... In fact, it's the total opposite, and the ONLY way to 'get cut' is to achieve low body-fat levels."

— Source: Science of Getting Ripped, Training Principle 1

## Training Implications

| Goal | Rep Range | Weight | Rest |
|------|-----------|--------|------|
| **Strength** (myofibrillar) | 1-5 | Heavy | 3-5 min |
| **Size** (sarcoplasmic) | 8-12+ | Moderate | 60-90 sec |
| **Getting cut** | Either | Either | Diet-dependent |

**Key point:** "Getting cut" is about body fat percentage, NOT rep ranges
