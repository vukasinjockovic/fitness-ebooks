# Supreme 7 Movements

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Seven essential exercises, compound movement framework

## Summary

A minimalist framework of seven fundamental compound movements (with variations) that form the basis of an effective training program. Designed for busy individuals who need simple, equipment-minimal workouts that can be done at any gym. Emphasizes that hundreds of complex exercises aren't necessary—these seven movements and their variations cover all major muscle groups.

## The Supreme 7

### 1. Squat
**Variations:**
- Front squat
- Overhead squat
- Box squat
- Dumbbell/Kettlebell squat
- Lunges (dumbbell or barbell)

### 2. Overhead Press
**Variations:**
- Barbell/Kettlebell
- Military press
- Behind/Front of body
- Push press
- One-arm
- Alternating

### 3. Deadlift
**Variations:**
- Conventional
- Snatch grip

### 4. Bench Press
**Variations:**
- Wide/Medium/Close grips
- Reverse grip
- Incline/Decline
- Dumbbell/Kettlebell
- Push-up variations
- Dips

### 5. Upper Body Pulling
**Variations:**
- Chin-up/Pull-up variations
- Cable rowing
- Barbell/Dumbbell/Kettlebell rows
- Renegade rows

### 6. Clean and Jerk
**Variations:**
- Power clean
- Dumbbell/Kettlebell clean
- Dumbbell/Kettlebell jerk
- Combinations of the two

### 7. Snatch
**Variations:**
- Hang snatch
- Dumbbell/Kettlebell snatch
- One-arm barbell snatch

— Source: Science of Getting Ripped, Training Principle 7

## Key Principles

1. **Simplicity** - Only need 1-2 pieces of equipment
2. **Compound focus** - Each exercise works multiple muscle groups
3. **Functional strength** - Builds athletic, usable strength
4. **Time efficiency** - 30-40 minutes, 3x/week is sufficient
5. **Variation within movements** - Same pattern, different equipment/angles
