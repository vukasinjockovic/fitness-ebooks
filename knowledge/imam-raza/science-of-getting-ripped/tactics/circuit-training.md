# Circuit Training (Strength Circuits)

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Supersets, tri-sets, strength circuits, metabolic resistance training

## Summary

Circuit training combines 3-6 weight-bearing exercises performed consecutively with minimal rest (30-90 seconds between circuits). It's the most effective method for busy people to burn fat and build muscle simultaneously by triggering growth hormone release while keeping heart rate elevated. Less optimal for pure strength or mass, but ideal for body recomposition.

## The Core Method

> "Performing functional, full-body, weight-bearing exercises in a high-intensity method, like supersets or circuit training, is the single most effective way to develop a lean muscular build and to literally burn and melt fat from your body."

— Source: Science of Getting Ripped, Training Principle 8

## Circuit vs Traditional Training

**Traditional (Station Training):**
- Complete all sets of exercise A
- Then all sets of exercise B
- Then all sets of exercise C
- Best for strength and mass

**Circuit Training:**
- Perform A → B → C → D → E → F consecutively
- Rest 30-90 seconds
- Repeat circuit 2-4 times
- Best for fat loss + muscle maintenance

## Sample Circuit Structure

> "You might do:
> - a set of push-ups for 30 seconds
> - a set of dips for 30 seconds
> - a set of squats for 30 seconds
> - a set of overhead presses for 30 seconds
> - followed by jumping jacks for 30 seconds"

## The Science: Why It Works

### Lactate and Fat-Burning

> "During your circuit, you're accumulating the chemical called 'lactate' in your bloodstream, and high lactate levels are typically associated with elevated levels of fat-burning hormones. But if you rest too long between sets, the oxygen that you breathe in will help your body clear the lactate from your bloodstream."

**Key:** Keep rest intervals short (≤90 seconds) to maintain high lactate levels.

### Research Support

> "Ball State University researchers discovered that fat-burning hormones increased with completing even just one set of a circuit."

## Supersets: Best of Both Worlds

Supersets combine cardiovascular benefits with strength/mass benefits:

**Example Superset:**
> "8 squats followed immediately by 8 bench presses followed immediately by 8 barbell rows. Rest for 1-2 minutes and repeat."

**Guidelines:**
- Use 85% of 1RM
- Combine exercises that work different muscle groups
- Prevents unnecessary fatigue in target muscles

## Training Trade-offs

| Goal | Circuit Training Effectiveness |
|------|-------------------------------|
| Fat loss | ★★★★★ Excellent |
| Conditioning | ★★★★★ Excellent |
| Muscle maintenance | ★★★★☆ Very Good |
| Muscle growth | ★★★☆☆ Moderate |
| Pure strength | ★★☆☆☆ 30-50% less gain |

> "The amount of muscular strength that you build with circuit training... will be 30 to 50 percent less, so you'll gain less strength in doing circuit training, but it's particularly helpful for cardiovascular endurance and conditioning."

## Mental Toughness Component

> "These workouts, when done properly, with good form and done one right after another... is extremely difficult and it will help you develop an element of mental toughness and confidence as you push through the discomfort and the intensity of these exercises."

> "When you're doing a circuit training type of workout with heavy weights, your time in the gym will be very efficient and very well-used. So you won't have time to chat up the ladies, to flip through fitness magazines, or to play with your iPhone. You're going to be there at the gym for one reason, and one reason only, and that's to work!"
