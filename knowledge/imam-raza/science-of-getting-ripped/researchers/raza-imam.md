# Raza Imam

**Type:** researcher
**Status:** partial
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Imam

## Summary

Author of "The Science of Getting Ripped" (2016). A busy professional and father who developed a practical approach to fitness for time-constrained individuals. His background includes martial arts training, and his methodology emphasizes efficiency—achieving results in 30-40 minutes, 3x/week through high-intensity training.

## Background

> "Like most 30-something guys with kids, I have a very busy life. Here's my typical day: An hour-long commute to and from work. Helping my 5 year-old with homework. Giving the kids baths. Putting them to bed. Doing dishes. Hanging out with the wife. And going to bed."

> "Once I hit 30, I was terrified that I would get the dreaded 'skinny fat' body type."

## Philosophy

> "I know for a fact that lots of guys can relate. I did a lot of research and came to realize that guys who want to get ripped need a specific type of workout."

**Core beliefs:**
- Fitness is intensity dependent, not time dependent
- Everything works, but nothing works forever
- Busy people need efficient, proven methods
- Mental toughness develops alongside physical fitness

## Key Contributions

1. **Supreme 7 Movements** - Minimalist exercise framework
2. **Intensity-Dependent Training** - Quality over quantity
3. **Circuit Training for Recomp** - Fat loss + muscle gain simultaneously
4. **18 Nutrition Hacks** - Practical diet guidelines for busy people

## Promise to Readers

> "My goal is to get you noticeable results in the next 15 days so that you're motivated to continue."

> "It takes 4 weeks to see a change in your body. It takes 8 weeks for others to see it. It takes 12 weeks for the world to see it."
