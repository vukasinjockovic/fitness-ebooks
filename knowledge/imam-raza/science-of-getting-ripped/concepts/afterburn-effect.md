# Afterburn Effect (EPOC)

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** EPOC, Excess Post-Exercise Oxygen Consumption, post-workout calorie burn

## Summary

The afterburn effect refers to the calories your body burns after your workout due to "oxygen debt" created during high-intensity exercise. At higher workout intensities, your body accumulates this debt because you're working so hard. EPOC (Excess Post-Exercise Oxygen Consumption) is your body making up for this debt, which burns calories for up to 48 hours after high-intensity activity.

## Two Key Factors

> "The 2 main key factors to burning calories during your workout are:
> 1. Calories Burned During Exercise (O2) - This is the number of calories you burn during your workout. This is measured because the amount of oxygen uptake is proportional to the number of calories burned
> 2. Calories Burned AFTER Exercise (EPOC) – At higher workout intensities, your body builds an 'oxygen debt' because you're working out so hard."

— Source: Science of Getting Ripped, Training Principle 9

## The Mechanism

> "So interval training (aka sprinting) gets you lean and in shape fast by improving oxygen utilization at the muscular level."

**How it works:**
1. High-intensity exercise depletes muscle glycogen
2. Anaerobic metabolism creates lactic acid
3. Lactic acid creates "oxygen debt"
4. During recovery, heart and lungs "pay back" this debt
5. Body becomes more efficient at oxygen delivery and utilization

## Practical Application

**To trigger the afterburn effect:**
- Perform high-intensity interval training (HIIT)
- Use circuit training with minimal rest
- Train at 9/10 intensity during work intervals
- Include rest periods for recovery

**Duration of effect:** Up to 48 hours of elevated calorie burn

## Key Quotes

> "The 'afterburn effect' is when your body makes up for this debt, which burns calories for up to 48 hours after the high intensity activity."

## Comparison: Steady State vs HIIT

| Aspect | Steady State Cardio | HIIT |
|--------|---------------------|------|
| Calorie burn | During activity only | During + 48 hours after |
| Oxygen use | Aerobic only | Aerobic + Anaerobic |
| Glycogen depletion | Slow | Fast |
| Fat burning signal | Moderate | Strong |
