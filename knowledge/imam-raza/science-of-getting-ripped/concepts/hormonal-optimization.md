# Hormonal Optimization

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Hormone hacking, metabolic hormones, fat loss hormones

## Summary

Getting ripped requires understanding and optimizing four key hormones: Insulin (glucose management), Growth Hormone (muscle building/fat burning), T3/Thyroid (metabolism master), and Leptin (hunger/satiety signaling). Circuit training and HIIT trigger favorable hormonal responses that burn fat while preserving muscle.

## The Four Key Hormones

### 1. Insulin
**Role:** Manages blood sugar, shuttles glucose to muscles or fat

> "Insulin is the hormone that is created when sugar is present in your blood. If your glycogen stores in your muscles are empty (after cardio or weight training), this is good because your body will send the sugar in your blood (glucose) to your glycogen stores in your muscles."

**Optimization:**
- Time carbs around workouts when glycogen is depleted
- Eat complex/fibrous carbs to avoid insulin spikes
- Avoid processed sugars that flood bloodstream

### 2. Growth Hormone (GH)
**Role:** Burns fat and builds muscle

> "Growth Hormone is the hormone that breaks down fat and builds muscle. You trigger growth hormone by lifting weights, doing intense cardio, and sleeping."

**Optimization:**
- Lift weights with intensity
- Perform HIIT/circuit training
- Prioritize sleep

### 3. T3/Thyroid
**Role:** Master gland of metabolism

> "Your thyroid is the master gland of your metabolism. If this drops, your body fights the fat loss process."

**Optimization:**
- Don't go too low on carbs for too long
- Maintain adequate calorie intake
- Support thyroid with proper nutrition

### 4. Leptin
**Role:** Controls fat storage vs. fat burning

> "Leptin is a hormone that basically controls fat loss. It tells your brain and your body to either store fat or burn fat. Some people have called it your hunger hormone."

**Optimization:**
- Don't restrict calories too severely
- Strategic carb intake maintains leptin
- Refeeds can reset leptin levels

## The Low-Carb Trap

> "The problem with going too low on carbs is that you put your body in starvation mode and throw your T3 and leptin levels off... So you think you're losing weight for the first 2 weeks (mostly water weight), but then your weight loss stops. And instead you start gaining fat because your leptin and T3 is off."

**Key insight:** Every gram of carbohydrate holds up to 3 grams of water. Dropping carbs causes water weight loss that's mistaken for fat loss.

## Recommended Carb Intake

> "5-7 grams of carbs per pound of body weight is recommended by top nutrition experts for people who are lifting heavy weights. For a 165 pound man, that's about 350 grams of carbs per day."

**Warning:** Going too low causes:
- Ketogenic state
- Impaired brain function
- Muscle catabolism
- Skinny-fat physique

## Hormonal Response to Training

| Training Type | Insulin | GH | Cortisol |
|---------------|---------|-----|----------|
| Circuit training | Low during, spike after | High | Elevated |
| Heavy weights | Low | Moderate | Low |
| HIIT | Low during | Very high | Elevated |
| Steady cardio | Low | Low | Elevated if long |

## How Circuit Training Triggers GH

> "High volume, short rest period workouts have been found to increase human growth hormone levels, when compared to training with just longer rest periods."

> "Ball State University researchers discovered that fat-burning hormones increased with completing even just one set of a circuit."
