# Ageless Athlete Base Building
**Type:** program
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell Presents: Ageless Athlete (2017) by Jim Madden
**Aliases:** AA Base Building, Ageless Base

## Summary
A modified version of standard TB II Base Building specifically designed for older athletes. It caps LSS at 30 minutes 3x/week to minimize joint stress while using Tango Circuits for strength-endurance work. Provides a recovery phase from heavy barbell training while building aerobic capacity.

## Why Take a Break from Heavy Barbells?
> "One of the distinct marks of the Tactical Barbell system is the emphasis on aerobic endurance as part of an overall, multi-domain approach to fitness."
— Source: Chapter 13

### Benefits for Ageless Athletes
- Cardiovascular health
- Mood improvement
- Weight control
- CNS rejuvenation
- Joint recovery

## Structure

### Weeks 1-5: Pure Endurance + Strength-Endurance
- No heavy barbell work
- LSS sessions 3x/week (30 min each)
- Tango Circuits for strength-endurance

### Weeks 6-8: Reintroduction
- Limited max strength work returns
- HIC reintroduced
- LSS backed off slightly

## Key Modification: LSS Cap
> "Your default base building approach should be the Ageless Athlete Base Building, which limits LSS to 3 x 30 per week."
— Source: Chapter 13 Central Take-Aways

This differs from standard TB II which allows longer LSS sessions.

## Addressing the Fear of Strength Loss
> "Many of you reading this probably panic at the thought of not touching a heavily loaded barbell for five weeks... Does that mean I will lose all my gains on my maxes?"
— Source: Chapter 13

### The Reality
- Strength loss is minimal and temporary
- Many trainees actually gain strength during base building
- Rebound effect often occurs after base building
- Takes only a few weeks to regain any lost strength

## Avoid "Strength First" Option
Though K. Black offers a Strength First variation, Madden encourages ageless athletes to take the full break:
> "I encourage ageless athletes to avoid the Strength First option."
— Source: Chapter 13
