# Lowest Effective Dose Principle
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell Presents: Ageless Athlete (2017) by Jim Madden
**Aliases:** LED, Minimum Effective Dose, Efficiency Principle

## Summary
The Lowest Effective Dose (LED) principle states that for ageless athletes, training should use the minimum stimulus required to produce the desired adaptation. This preserves recovery capacity for other life demands and reduces injury risk while still driving progress.

## Philosophical Foundation
Madden draws from Aristotle:
> "It is the nature of such things to be destroyed by defect and excess... both excessive and defective exercise destroys the strength, and similarly drink or food which is above or below a certain amount destroys the health, while that which is proportionate both produces and increases and preserves it."
— Source: Part 3 Introduction (Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics)

## Core Principle
Do enough to drive adaptation, but no more than necessary.

## Application to Strength Training

### Template Selection
- Prefer lower frequency when results are equal
- Operator I/A recommended as "close to the ideal for the ageless athlete"
— Source: Chapter 6

### Programming Priorities
> "Whatever program you select, you should prioritize high frequency, high intensity, lower rep schemes."
— Source: Chapter 6 Central Take-Aways

### Exercise Selection: High Yield/Low Risk
Think of exercise selection like financial investment:
> "Putting your money into something that is low risk and high yield is the ideal. We want to strive for the same sort of ideal investment when selecting lifts for a cluster."
— Source: Chapter 7

## Efficiency as Cornerstone
> "Efficiency is the cornerstone for a lifetime of productive training."
— Source: Chapter 7

## Practical Implementation
1. Minimize accessory work
2. Choose exercises that carry over to multiple goals
3. Use training maxes (85-90% of true max) rather than actual 1RMs
4. Scale conditioning to actual goals, not arbitrary standards
