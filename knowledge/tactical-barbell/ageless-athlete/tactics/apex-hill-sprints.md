# Apex Hill Sprints
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell Presents: Ageless Athlete (2017) by Jim Madden
**Aliases:** Apex, Hill Sprint + KB Protocol

## Summary
Apex Hill Sprints is a high-intensity conditioning protocol that combines hill sprints with kettlebell swings. It addresses multiple fitness domains simultaneously: strength, speed, anaerobic capacity, and mental toughness. The book provides detailed progression strategies for ageless athletes.

## The Protocol
1. Sprint up a hill
2. Perform 10 two-handed kettlebell swings at the top
3. Jog down (active recovery)
4. Repeat

## Standards
| Level | Rounds | Kettlebell | Notes |
|-------|--------|------------|-------|
| Beginner | 5+ | 24kg | Breaks allowed, walk down |
| Intermediate | 10+ | 32kg | Minimal breaks |
| Advanced/Operator | 15+ | 48kg (Beast) | No breaks, jog down |

## Common Progression Mistake
> "Smitty wants to progress at Apex... after about five trips, even with some breaks and walking down the slope, Smitty is completely wasted... he is not seeing improvements."
— Source: Chapter 15

## Three Keys to Progress

### 1. Build Your Aerobic Base
> "Sure, Apex is a paradigmatic HIC-style workout. There is, however, a considerable endurance aspect to it."
— Source: Chapter 15

### 2. Master Your Kettlebell
- Swing technique must be automatic
- Build swing volume separately from Apex
- The swing should not be the limiting factor

### 3. Apply Periodization
- Don't go all-out every session
- Include lighter "practice" sessions
- Build volume before intensity
— Source: Chapter 15 Central Take-Aways

## Author's Progression
- Started July 2015 with 70lb bell, quickly reached 20 rounds
- November 2015: 15 rounds with 48kg Beast
- February 2016: 20 rounds with the Beast
— Source: Chapter 15

## For Ageless Athletes
