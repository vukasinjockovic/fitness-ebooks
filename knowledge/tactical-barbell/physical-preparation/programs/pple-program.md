# PPLE (Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement)
**Type:** program
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement (2015) by K. Black
**Aliases:** PPLE, Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement

## Summary
PPLE is a 12-week structured training program designed to prepare candidates for law enforcement physical fitness tests (PFTs) and police academy physical training. It uses a periodized approach with two main blocks: Block I (Base Building) for general strength and conditioning, and Block II (Specificity) for police-specific physical preparation.

## Program Goals
1. Pass entry-level physical fitness testing for law enforcement agencies
2. Prepare physically for the police academy
3. Prepare physically for a career in law enforcement
— Source: Chapter 1, "Goals"

## Program Structure

### Block I - Base Building (4 Weeks)
- Focus: General strength and conditioning
- Weekly structure: 3 strength days, 2 GE days, 1 HIC day, 1 rest day
- Strength template: Tactical Barbell Operator
- Primary exercises: Squat, Bench Press, Pull-ups
— Source: Chapter 1, Chapter 7-8

### Block II - Specificity (8 Weeks)
- Focus: Police-specific physical skills
- Weekly structure: 2 strength days, 2 HIC days, 1 BW day, 1 GE day, 1 rest day
- Strength template: Tactical Barbell Fighter
- Greater emphasis on bodyweight work, timed runs, work capacity
— Source: Chapter 1, Chapter 9-10

### Zero Block (Optional, 1-4 Weeks)
- For beginners who cannot do 5+ pull-ups or run continuously
- Prerequisite to Block I for those starting from zero
— Source: Chapter 1

## Entry Benchmarks (Skip Block I if ALL met)
- 10+ pull-ups
- 1.5 miles in 10:30 or less
- 40 consecutive push-ups
- 3 miles/5km in 25 minutes or less
— Source: Chapter 2, "How to Use PPLE"

## Key Principles

### Consistency Over Intensity
> "It's not about going 110% every single training session... It's about perseverance and CONSISTENCY."
— Source: Chapter 6, "Summary"

### Rest Day Compliance
> "When it's a designated rest day – then REST. Doing extra isn't going to impress anyone, and may impact your results with this program."
— Source: Chapter 6

### Permission to Have Bad Days
> "I give you permission to have a bad workout, take twice as long, or feel like crap while training. Go ahead and have an ugly training session. Your consistency WILL pay off."
— Source: Chapter 6
