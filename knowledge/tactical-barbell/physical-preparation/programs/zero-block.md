# Zero Block
**Type:** program
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement (2015) by K. Black
**Aliases:** Zero Block, Beginner Block, Pre-PPLE

## Summary
Zero Block is an optional 1-4 week preparatory phase in PPLE designed for absolute beginners who cannot perform basic movements like pull-ups or sustained running. It serves as a prerequisite to Block I for those starting from ground zero.

## Target Audience
- Cannot do a single pull-up
- Cannot run for more than a few minutes continuously
— Source: Chapter 1

## Goals
1. Achieve 5+ pull-ups
2. Develop basic running capacity sufficient for Block I
— Source: Chapter 1

## Duration
- 1-4 weeks depending on starting ability and progress
- Move to Block I when able to do approximately 5 pull-ups
— Source: Chapter 1

## Components

### Pull-up Progression
> "This mini-program will bring you up to roughly 5 pull-ups or more from zero."
— Source: Chapter 1

### Running Foundation
> "It's a beginner method provided by a former top ranked national runner and police academy instructor. It'll take you from running a couple minutes at a time, to a level that allows you to begin Block I."
— Source: Chapter 1

## Why It Matters
> "Pull-ups are found in most PFTs, and even if they're not, you'll most likely face them at the academy. They're a great exercise and develop strength to a high degree. They're also harder to perform initially."
— Source: Chapter 1

> "You need a base starting point for optimal results."
— Source: Chapter 1
