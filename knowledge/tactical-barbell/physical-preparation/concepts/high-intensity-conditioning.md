# High Intensity Conditioning (HIC)
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement (2015) by K. Black
**Aliases:** HIC

## Summary
High Intensity Conditioning (HIC) is a category of training in Tactical Barbell that develops anaerobic capacity and work capacity through short, intense bouts of activity. HIC sessions typically combine running, bodyweight exercises, and work capacity challenges in circuit or interval formats.

## Role in PPLE
- **Block I:** 1 HIC session per week (Day 6, combined with BW)
- **Block II:** 2 HIC sessions per week (Days 2 and 5)
— Source: Chapters 7-10

## Example HIC Workouts

### Workout 1: Burpee-Run Complex (20 min)
- 10 Burpees
- Run 100M
- 10 Mountain Climbers
- Repeat continuously for 20 minutes
- Performed at steady, fluid pace with no rest intervals
— Source: Chapter 10, Block II Week 1 Session 2

### Workout 2: Hill Repeats (7 rounds)
- Sprint up hill
- 10 push-ups at top
- Walk down (active recovery)
- Repeat for 7 rounds
— Source: Chapter 10, Block II Week 1 Session 5

## Key Principles

### Work Capacity Focus
> "The idea is work/run continuously for the 20 minutes. You'll be developing work capacity here."
— Source: Chapter 10

### Completion Over Perfection
> "Your PRIMARY GOAL is to complete the session no matter how ugly it looks or you perform. The skill and improvement WILL come over time with consistent effort."
— Source: Chapter 10
