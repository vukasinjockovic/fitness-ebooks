# Rest Interval Rule
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement (2015) by K. Black
**Aliases:** 2-Minute Rule, TB Rest Protocol

## Summary
The Rest Interval Rule is a core principle of Tactical Barbell strength training that mandates a minimum 2-minute rest between sets. This rule supports strength development by ensuring adequate recovery between sets and avoiding muscle failure.

## The Rule
> "You must rest a minimum of 2 minutes in between sets."
— Source: Chapter 4, "Strength"

## Rationale
> "The key to increasing your strength with this program is frequent heavy lifting while avoiding muscle failure."
— Source: Chapter 4

### Why Minimum 2 Minutes?
- Allows partial ATP regeneration
- Ensures quality reps on subsequent sets
- Prevents accumulated fatigue that leads to failure
- Supports strength development over hypertrophy

## Application Guidelines
- **Minimum:** 2 minutes between sets
- **Maximum:** Up to 5 minutes if needed
- **Optimal:** 2-3 minutes for heavier sets (author's preference)
- **If still fatigued after 2 min:** Rest longer

> "If you are still feeling fatigued after a two minute rest, rest longer. Take up to 5 minutes if necessary. Be relatively fresh for all your sets."
— Source: Chapter 4

## Important Distinction
This rule applies ONLY to strength training portions:
> "The above rules ONLY apply to the strength training portions of this program. During conditioning, bodyweight and endurance sessions, muscle failure and fatigue are part of the equation and unavoidable."
— Source: Chapter 4
