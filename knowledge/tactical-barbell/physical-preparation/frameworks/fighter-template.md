# Tactical Barbell Fighter Template
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement (2015) by K. Black
**Aliases:** Fighter, TB Fighter, Fighter Program

## Summary
The Fighter template is a reduced-frequency strength training program from Tactical Barbell designed for athletes who need more time for conditioning and skill work. In PPLE, Fighter is used during Block II (Specificity) when strength training drops from 3 to 2 sessions per week.

## Training Frequency
- 2 strength sessions per week (vs Operator's 3)
- Compensates with increased volume per session (3-5 sets vs 3 sets)
— Source: Chapter 9-10

## Volume Adjustment
> "You'll be doing 3-5 sets of each exercise for the barbell work. The reason for this is we've switched from three days of lifting and strength to two – so to compensate, we're going to increase the work load by increasing the volume."
— Source: Chapter 10

## Auto-Regulation
The template includes built-in auto-regulation:

> "Use your judgement when deciding how many sets to do. On days you feel energized and fresh, aim for 5. On days you're struggling, do only 3. If you know you'll be struggling with that 5th set, drop it."
— Source: Chapter 10

## Example Week (Block II, Week 1)
| Day | Session Type | Details |
|-----|--------------|---------|
| 1 | Strength | Squat 3-5x5 @70%, Bench 3-5x5 @70%, Pull-ups 3 sets |
| 2 | HIC | Burpee-Run Complex 20 min |
| 3 | BW | Circuit training 20 min |
| 4 | Strength | Squat 3-5x5 @70%, Bench 3-5x5 @70%, Pull-ups 3 sets |
| 5 | HIC | Hill Repeats x7 |
| 6 | GE | Steady state run 30 min |
| 7 | Rest | - |
— Source: Chapter 10
