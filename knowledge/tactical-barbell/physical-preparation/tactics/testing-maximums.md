# Testing Maximums Protocol
**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Physical Preparation for Law Enforcement (2015) by K. Black
**Aliases:** 1RM Testing, Max Testing, Test Day

## Summary
A structured protocol for determining one-repetition maximums (1RMs) used to calculate training loads in Tactical Barbell programs. Testing occurs before each training block and uses either direct 1RM testing or rep-based calculations.

## When to Test
1. Before starting Block I (after PFT practice test)
2. Before starting Block II (after completing Block I)
3. After extended training cycles
— Source: Chapters 2, 7, 9

## What to Test
1. **Barbell Squat** - 1RM
2. **Barbell Bench Press** - 1RM
3. **Barbell Deadlift** - 1RM
4. **Pull-ups** - Maximum reps
— Source: Chapter 7

## Testing Protocol
1. Be fully rested before testing
2. Complete proper warm-up
3. Take 3-5 minute rest after warm-up sets
4. Perform test set when fully fresh

> "*Take a minimum 3-5 minute rest interval after your warm up sets prior to your actual test set. The idea is to be fully rested for your money set."
— Source: Chapter 7

## Calculation Method
Can use weight x reps formula to estimate 1RM:
```
WEIGHT x REPS = ONE REPETITION MAXIMUM
```
— Source: Chapter 7 (test form)

## Documentation
Record all maximums on test day:
- Date
- Squat: Weight x Reps = 1RM
- Bench: Weight x Reps = 1RM
- Deadlift: Weight x Reps = 1RM
- Pull-ups: Max reps
— Source: Chapter 7

## Using Your Numbers
All training sessions use percentages of YOUR tested maximums:
> "Every session you'll be called upon to lift a certain percentage of your maximums for a prescribed amount of sets and reps."
— Source: Chapter 4
