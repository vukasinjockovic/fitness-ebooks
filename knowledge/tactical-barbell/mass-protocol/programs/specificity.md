# Specificity Phase
**Type:** program
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Mass Protocol (2018) by K. Black
**Aliases:** Specificity, Alpha, Bravo, Fine-Tuning Phase

## Summary
Specificity is the fine-tuning phase of Mass Protocol where focus shifts to isolation work and targeted hypertrophy. It's used after General Mass to sculpt specific areas and address weaknesses. Comes in two versions: Alpha (mixed strength/hypertrophy) and Bravo (pure hypertrophy).

## Philosophy
> "During General Mass you swapped out your Miata for a Humvee. Now it's time to work on the bells and whistles."
— Source: "Specificity" chapter

## Block Length
3 weeks per block (can run multiple blocks)

## Two Versions

### Alpha - Mixed Model
50/50 split between maximal-strength and hypertrophy:
- 2 MS (Maximal Strength) sessions/week
- 2 H (Hypertrophy) sessions/week

> "Alpha keeps an element of heavy maximal-strength style training in the mix when you're spending months and months on lighter/higher volume hypertrophy work."
— Source: "Specificity" chapter

### Bravo - Pure Hypertrophy
4 hypertrophy sessions per week, no dedicated strength work

## Why Include Strength (Alpha)?
> "Maximal-strength training will give your muscles that hard dense look along with the ability to generate force. That greater strength translates into heavier loads when it comes to hypertrophy training. Heavier loads result in bigger muscles."
— Source: "Specificity Alpha" chapter

## Alpha Clusters

### Maximal-Strength (MS) Cluster
Standard: Bench, Squat, Deadlift
- 75-85% 1RM
- 3-5 reps
- 1-3 sets
- Avoid failure, stay fresh

### Hypertrophy (H) Cluster
6-12 exercises divided into H1 and H2:
- 60-75% 1RM
- 8-12 reps
- 3-4 sets
- Failure acceptable on final reps

## Execution Differences

### MS Days
> "The mindset I want you to have: the last rep of your workout should be as crisp as the first."
— Source: "Specificity Alpha" chapter

### H Days
> "On H days, you can get a little dirtier... Muscle failure/struggle near the end of your set is acceptable and expected."
— Source: "Specificity Alpha" chapter

## When to Use
- After completing General Mass phase
- When specific areas need development
- To break plateaus
- For targeted aesthetic work
