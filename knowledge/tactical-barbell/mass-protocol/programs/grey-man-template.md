# Grey Man Template
**Type:** program
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Mass Protocol (2018) by K. Black
**Aliases:** GM, Grey Man, Gray Man

## Summary
Grey Man is a versatile 3-day mass building template using an alternating A/B/A schedule. It combines main compound lifts with customizable supplementary work, making it popular among operational clients who need to balance lifting with conditioning and recovery.

## Why "Grey Man"?
The name reflects its versatility - it can blend into any training context while still delivering results.

## Structure

### Main Cluster (Standard)
- Bench Press
- Squat
- Overhead Press
- Deadlift

### Supplementary Clusters (Customizable)
Create two S-clusters of 2-3 exercises each:
- **S1:** e.g., Dips, Incline DB Press, Front Squats
- **S2:** e.g., DB Shrugs, Rows

> "You create and customize the S cluster... Don't get too crazy and overload yourself with supplementary exercises. Use no more than 4 to 6."
— Source: "Grey Man Template" chapter

## Weekly Schedule
A/B/A one week, B/A/B the next:
| Day | Week 1 | Week 2 |
|-----|--------|--------|
| 1 | Bench, Squat, S1 | OHP, DL, S2 |
| 3 | OHP, DL, S2 | Bench, Squat, S1 |
| 5 | Bench, Squat, S1 | OHP, DL, S2 |

## Execution

### Main Lifts
- 4-5 sets x 8 reps @ 70% 1RM (typical)
- Rest 2-5 minutes
- Avoid muscle failure

### Supplementary Lifts
- 4 sets x 12 @ 55% 1RM
- Rest 1-2 minutes
- Failure on final reps acceptable
- Super-setting is an option

## Operational Flexibility
> "If you're an operational type and want to use your supplementary time for non-conventional exercises or training – go for it. That's the idea behind GM. If you're feeling kettlebells, bodyweight work, grip, core, plyometrics or even extra conditioning – S cluster is where you do it."
— Source: "Grey Man Template" chapter

## Progression
Every 3-6 weeks:
- Add 5-10lbs to 1RMs
- Recalculate percentages
- Don't force progression on exercises you struggled with
