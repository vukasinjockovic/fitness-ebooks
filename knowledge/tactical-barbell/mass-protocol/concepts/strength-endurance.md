# Strength-Endurance (SE) Training
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** Tactical Barbell: Mass Protocol (2018) by K. Black
**Aliases:** SE, Muscular Endurance, SE Training

## Summary
Strength-Endurance is the ability of a muscle group to exert force against resistance for extended periods. In Mass Protocol's Base Building phase, SE training uses high repetitions with light loads (15-30% 1RM) to prepare the body for heavy hypertrophy work.

## Definition
> "Strength-Endurance (aka muscular-endurance) is the ability of a muscle/muscle-group to exert force against resistance for extended periods of time. Doing 100 push-ups non-stop is an example of strength-endurance."
— Source: "Programming" chapter

## SE in Base Building

### Frequency
3 times per week on alternate days

### Load
10-25% of 1RM (very light)

### Structure
4 exercises performed circuit-style

## Key Principle: Reps Over Load
> "Strength-Endurance training is about reps first, NOT LOAD. This isn't maximal-strength training. No need to get ultra-precise with your calculations. Get within the ballpark. If in doubt, always err on the side of lighter."
— Source: "Strength-Endurance Training" chapter

## Expect Messiness
> "SE TRAINING IS MESSY. You might frequently fail at various reps and sets throughout the session. That's normal. Don't expect to go in there and knock off all the reps of all sets perfectly."
— Source: "SE Training Is Messy" section

## Purpose
1. Build work capacity for hypertrophy phases
2. Strengthen connective tissue
3. Prepare joints for heavy loading
4. Create mind-muscle connection with lighter weights
