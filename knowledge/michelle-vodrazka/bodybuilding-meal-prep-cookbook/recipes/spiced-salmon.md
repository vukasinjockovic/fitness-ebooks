# SPICED SALMON

**Category:** Lunch and Dinner
**Source:** The Bodybuilding Meal Prep Cookbook by Michelle Vodrazka (2019)
**Tags:** Gluten-Free, Nut-Free

> Sheet pan dinners are the one-dish meal that our hectic schedules need more of. Herbs and spices add flavor and fiber to this veggie-salmon meal without adding excess calories. This low-carb meal is a great choice for non-workout or cardio-only days.

## Quick Info

- **Servings:** 6
- **Macros:** 42% Protein / ?% Carbs / 45% Fat

## Ingredients

- 1 broccoli head (318g), cut into florets
- 1 cauliflower head (575g), cut into florets
- Nonstick olive oil spray
- Sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Juice of ½ lemon (30ml)
- 1½ to 2 pounds (0.78 to 1.04kg) salmon fillet
- Nonstick olive oil spray
- ¼ teaspoon (0.7g) garlic powder
- ¼ teaspoon (0.6g) onion powder
- ½ teaspoon (1.1g) paprika
- ½ teaspoon (0.25g) dried parsley
- ½ teaspoon (0.35g) dried basil
- Pinch ground cayenne pepper
- Salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- ½ lemon (42g), sliced

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 425ºF.

2. In a large bowl, combine the broccoli and cauliflower. Spray liberally with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper.

3. Spread the vegetables out onto a sheet pan and bake for 20 minutes.

4. Remove from the oven, let cool, then toss with the lemon juice.

5. Reduce the oven to 400ºF.

6. Place the salmon on a roasting pan, skin-side down.

7. Spray liberally with olive oil, then season evenly with garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, parsley, basil, and cayenne pepper. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and top with the lemon slices.

8. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, or until the fish flakes when pierced with a fork and is no longer translucent inside. Remove from the oven and let cool.

9. Divide the salmon and vegetables evenly among 6 airtight storage containers and seal.
