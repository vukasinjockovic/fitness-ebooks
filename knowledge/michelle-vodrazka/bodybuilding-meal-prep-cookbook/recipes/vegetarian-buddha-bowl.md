# VEGETARIAN BUDDHA BOWL

**Category:** Lunch and Dinner
**Source:** The Bodybuilding Meal Prep Cookbook by Michelle Vodrazka (2019)
**Tags:** Gluten-Free, Nut-Free, Vegetarian

> Buddha bowls are a favorite among bodybuilders because they are a great way to use leftover food. Potatoes, beets, hard-boiled eggs, leftover chicken breast, salmon, beans, rice, quinoa, and any type of veggie can be used to create an incredible meal. Top with a dressing of your choice or drizzle with lemon juice.

## Quick Info

- **Servings:** 4
- **Macros:** 12% Protein / 42% Carbs / 46% Fat

## Ingredients

- 2 large sweet potatoes (550g), peeled and cubed
- 1 large sweet onion (275g), sliced into large wedges
- 1 garlic clove (5g), minced
- Nonstick cooking spray
- Sea salt and black pepper
- 2 tablespoons (30ml) extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tablespoons (30ml) freshly squeezed lemon juice
- 2 to 2½ teaspoons (10 to 12ml) honey
- 2 cups (312g) steamed broccoli florets, divided
- 1 (15.5-ounce) (425g) can chickpeas, rinsed and drained, divided
- 2 cups (140g) shredded kale, divided
- 1 medium avocado (150g), sliced, divided
- 1 cup (110g) shredded carrots, divided
- 2 tablespoons (20g) hemp hearts, divided

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 400ºF.

2. In a large bowl, combine the sweet potatoes, onion, and garlic. Spray well with cooking spray, and stir to combine, then season with salt and pepper. Spread out onto a baking sheet and bake for 25 to 30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

3. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, and honey, and set aside.

4. Into each of 4 airtight storage containers, place half of the roasted sweet potatoes, then top each with ½ cup of steamed broccoli, ½ cup of chickpeas, ½ cup of kale, ¼ of the avocado, and ¼ cup of shredded carrots. Drizzle each Buddha bowl with 1 tablespoon of dressing, and season with salt and pepper. Sprinkle each bowl with ½ tablespoon of hemp hearts and seal.
