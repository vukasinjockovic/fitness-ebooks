# John Meadows

**Type:** researcher
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Mountain Dog, @MOUNTAINDOG1

## Summary

John Meadows was a professional bodybuilder, coach, and creator of the Mountain Dog Training system. He combined decades of competitive bodybuilding experience with an analytical approach to training methodology. Before pursuing fitness full-time, he worked as a VP at a bank. He was known for his brutal yet systematically organized training approach and his emphasis on longevity, injury prevention, and instinctive periodization.

## Background

## Key Contributions

### Mountain Dog Training System
A comprehensive training methodology featuring:
- 4-Phase workout structure (Pre-Pump, Explosive, Supra-Maximal Pump, Loaded Stretching)
- Instinctive periodization (3-phase macrocycle)
- Systematic application of intensification techniques
- Emphasis on injury prevention through workout sequencing

### Training Philosophy

> "Training intensely is important, but training with a purpose is MORE important."
— Fouad Abiad on Meadows

> "Get the most out of the least."
— Meadows' guiding principle

### Coaching Approach
Known for pushing clients beyond what they thought possible:
> "He asks me how many reps he should do on the dumbell bench press with 150 lb dumbbells. I reply 'Twenty five.' His jaw drops, as he doesn't think it is even possible. Then he does it. Bam."
— On training Antoine Valliant
