# Occlusion Training

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Blood Flow Restriction (BFR), Kaatsu Training, Tourniquet Training

## Summary

Occlusion training uses wraps or bands around upper arms or thighs to partially restrict blood flow during exercise. This creates extreme metabolic stress with very light weights, making it valuable for maintaining muscle during periods when heavy training isn't possible. The technique produces "very painful pumps" that may drive growth via cell swelling.

## How to Perform

1. Wrap upper arms or thighs with bands/wraps
2. Tightness: 7 out of 10 (NOT complete restriction)
3. Use light weights
4. Perform high-rep sets
5. Keep wrapped for limited duration only

## Critical Safety Guidelines

> "The goal is NOT to completely restrict blood flow altogether. Most experts say to wrap with a perceived tightness of 7 out of 10."
— Ch. 2

> "If I see a limb turning purple, well that may be overdoing it!"
— Ch. 2

> "I do not believe occluding a muscle for long periods of time is safe (could cause tissue death). We are looking for more fast twitch recruitment."
— Ch. 2

## How It Works

- Blood flow restriction causes metabolic accumulation
- Fatigues muscle, necessitating high threshold motor unit involvement
- Lactate and GH increases documented
- Produces extreme cell swelling/pump

**Primary benefit:** Extreme metabolic stress creates potent hypertrophy stimulus despite minimal mechanical tension.

## Research Highlight

> "One BFR study employing occlusion workouts twice daily for two weeks generated one of the fastest rates of muscle growth recorded in the research literature."
— Ch. 2

## Best Applications

1. **Injury/recovery periods:** When heavy training isn't possible
2. **Crushed/bad days:** When feeling overtrained
3. **Arm training:** Particularly effective for biceps/triceps
4. **Leg training:** Quad/hamstring work
