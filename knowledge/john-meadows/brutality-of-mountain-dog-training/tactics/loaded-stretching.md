# Loaded Stretching

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Stretch Overload, Weighted Stretches, Dynamic Loaded Stretching

## Summary

Loaded stretching is the cornerstone of Phase 4 in Mountain Dog Training - exercises that put the target muscle into a stretched position while overcoming resistance. It's done after muscles are fully pumped and warm for safety. The technique combines dynamic exercises emphasizing the elongated position with static stretches performed with added load.

## Two Forms

### 1. Dynamic Loaded Stretching
Exercises that emphasize tension in stretched position:
- Romanian/Stiff-legged deadlifts (hamstrings)
- Machine flyes (chest)
- Stretch push-ups between steps/boxes (chest)
- Weighted hangs (lats)

### 2. Static Loaded Stretching
Hold stretched position with weight after set:
- Dumbbell press → failure → hold in stretch position while partner pushes down (10-30 sec)
- Squats → lighten weight → sit in deep squat with erect posture (10-30 sec)
- Hang from chin-up bar with weight attached

## Why Phase 4 Placement

> "I believe it's safest to stretch your muscles under load after they're primed to their limits with blood (and the tendons are warm)."
— Ch. 1

**Safety rationale:**
- Warm connective tissue is more pliable
- Pumped muscles better handle stretch stress
- Reduced injury risk compared to stretching cold

## Top 3 Loaded Stretch Exercises

1. **Dumbbell Presses**
   - Go to failure
   - Hold in stretch position
   - Partner gently pushes down for 10-30 seconds

2. **Squats**
   - Complete set, lighten weight
   - Sit in deep squat
   - Maintain erect posture for 10-30 seconds

3. **Hanging with Weight**
   - Attach weight to body
   - Hang from chin-up bar
   - Allow lats to stretch as long as possible

## Scientific Mechanisms

- **Occludes blood flow:** Contractions >50-60% of max occlude blood flow, creating growth stimulus
- **Fascial stretching:** May "make room" for muscle growth
- **Connective tissue remodeling:** May help withstand stretch overload
- **Coordination entrainment:** Better contractions in stretched position
- **Hyperplasia potential:** Theorized if done consistently over time

## Hypertrophy Impact

Loaded stretching creates growth stimulus through:
- Blood flow occlusion during contraction
- Fascial tissue stretching
- Connective tissue adaptation
- Full ROM muscle activation

## Caution

> "Don't try to force the stretch beyond your normal active range of motion as this can be dangerous to the joints and possibly even cause muscle tearing."
— Ch. 2 (Occlusion Stretches)
