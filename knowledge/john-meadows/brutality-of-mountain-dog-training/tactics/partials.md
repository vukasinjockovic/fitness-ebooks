# Partials

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Partial Reps, Quarter Reps, Half Reps

## Summary

Partials are another of Meadows' favorite techniques - after completing full-range reps to failure, you continue with reduced range of motion (half or quarter reps) while maintaining strict body posture. This extends the set, creates additional metabolic stress, and "jams the blood" into the target muscle.

## How to Perform

1. Complete all reps possible with perfect form
2. Maintain body posture (don't lose chest arch, don't bounce)
3. Continue with smaller half-reps or quarter-reps
4. Keep form strict throughout
5. Rep to complete failure in reduced range

## Key Implementation Detail

> "Keep your body posture in correct alignment on this. I see way too many people that are doing presses for example, and then they will lose the arch in their chest, bounce the weight etc. These should be done strictly!"
— Ch. 2

## Applications

### Phase 1 (Pre-Pump)
At end of leg curls:
> "When my legs are too tired to do another full-range rep, I'll bend my knees just a few inches, completing quarter-range reps to failure. Jam the blood in there!"
— Ch. 1

### Phase 3 (Supra-Maximal Pump)
Machine chest press:
- Complete full reps to failure
- Continue with 5-20 partials
- Quarter-range reps from mid to lockout

**Primary benefit:** Extended time under tension with high motor unit recruitment despite reduced ROM.

## When to Use

- **Phase 1:** End of pre-pump sets (jam blood in)
- **Phase 3:** Supra-maximal pump exercises
- After drop sets (end with partials on lightest weight)
- Any exercise where you can safely reduce ROM

## Common Mistakes

- Losing body posture/arch
- Bouncing the weight
- Using momentum instead of muscle
- Going too heavy (partials should be strict)
