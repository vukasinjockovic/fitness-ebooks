# Forced Reps

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Partner-Assisted Reps, Assisted Reps

## Summary

Forced reps require a training partner who provides just enough assistance during the concentric (lifting) portion to allow you to complete additional reps after reaching failure. The key is maintaining perfect form while fighting through excruciating pain - the partner helps only minimally.

## How to Perform

1. Rep to failure with perfect form
2. Partner provides MINIMAL assistance on concentric only
3. Just enough help to keep good form intact
4. Continue fighting through pain with peak concentration
5. Complete additional 2-4 reps typically

## Key Implementation Details

**Partner assistance:**
> "A partner will give you just enough help during the concentric part of the movement to allow you to keep your good form intact and complete a rep."
— Ch. 2

**Mental requirement:**
> "Your concentration will need to be at peak levels as you will be fighting through excruciating pain."
— Ch. 2

## Requirements

- **Training partner:** Absolutely essential
- **Like-minded partner:** Must understand "minimal" help
- **Peak concentration:** Fighting through extreme discomfort
- **Good form:** Non-negotiable throughout

## Why Training Partner Is Essential

> "The first thing I would advise is getting a like-minded training partner that will push you and who also wants to be pushed. A great training partner is worth his/her weight in gold."
— Ch. 2

## When to Use

- **Phase 3:** Supra-Maximal Pump exercises
- Final set of an exercise
- When partner is available and understands technique
