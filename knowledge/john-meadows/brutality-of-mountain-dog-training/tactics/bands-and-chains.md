# Bands and Chains

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Accommodating Resistance, Variable Resistance, EliteFTS Bands

## Summary

Bands and chains provide accommodating resistance that matches the human strength curve - lighter at weak points, heavier at strong points. In Mountain Dog Training, they're used in Phase 2 (Explosive Exercises) to bust plateaus, overload eccentrics, assist weak points, and modify activation patterns. Bands require careful cycling (2-3 weeks max), while chains can be used longer.

## How They Work

### Strength Curve Concept
- You're stronger at lockout than at the bottom
- Standard weights: same resistance throughout
- Bands/chains: resistance increases as you lift
- Forces you to explode through the entire ROM

### Bands vs Chains

| Aspect | Bands | Chains |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Elastic component | Yes (pulls bar down fast) | No |
| Eccentric overload | Extreme | Moderate |
| CNS/body toll | Higher | Lower |
| Max duration | 2-3 weeks | Can use longer |
| Cycling required | Yes, careful | Less critical |

## Applications in Bodybuilding

1. **Overloading the Eccentric**
   - Bands intensify negative portion
   - Must lower under control or band snaps weight down
   - Highly effective but requires care

2. **Assistance (Reverse Bands)**
   - Bands attached to top of rack
   - Lightens load at bottom (weak point)
   - Allows heavier weights with less injury risk

3. **Stronger Muscle Contraction**
   - Bands looped on handles change angle of pull
   - Affects activation pattern and feel

4. **Facilitating Higher Reps**
   - Face pulls, lateral raises easier to rep out with bands
   - Creates bigger pump

5. **Rehabilitation**
   - Over-and-back shoulder stretches
   - Improves mobility in shoulder girdle

## Research Support

- Adding bands to bench press increases bar acceleration vs equivalent free weight
- Chains increase maximal effort bar speed via postactivation potentiation
- Bands/chains on bench and squat: 2-3x greater voluntary strength gains vs traditional training
- Works even in athletes with ~4 years resistance training experience

## When to Introduce

> "If you haven't been training for at least a few years and don't have a well-developed mind-muscle connection or a solid strength base... I usually have people who are brand new to my system go two 12-week cycles before using bands and chains."
— FAQ

**Readiness indicators:**
- Bench ~bodyweight (within 30 lbs for females)
- Squat bodyweight + ~100 lbs for reps
- Can handle heavier side of dumbbell rack
- "Regular people are impressed with your muscularity"
