# Iso-Holds (Maximal Isometric)

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Isometric Holds, Static Holds Against Resistance, Tom Platz Technique

## Summary

Iso-Holds are a particularly brutal intensification technique learned from Tom Platz. Unlike standard isometric contractions, you go to failure, then hold the weight at midpoint while a training partner adds additional pressure. This creates an extreme mechanical tension stimulus by exploiting the muscle's greater strength during isometric contractions.

## How to Perform

1. Perform set to failure (no more reps with perfect form)
2. Hold weight at midpoint (slightly closer to shortened position)
3. Partner adds enough pressure for isometric "hold"
4. Struggle against weight AND partner's pressure
5. Hold for 7-10 seconds

## Why It's So Effective

> "This is brutal, this is really tough, which means I love it."
— Ch. 2

**Scientific basis:**
- Muscle is inherently stronger during isometric contractions
- Amplifies tensile (mechanical tension) stimulus
- Maximizes motor unit recruitment at failure point

## Requirements

- **Training partner:** Essential for adding resistance
- **Mental discipline:** Must fight through intense discomfort
- **Controlled weight:** Don't let partner add too much pressure

## When to Use

- **Phase 3:** Supra-Maximal Pump exercises
- After full failure on final set
- Sparingly - very taxing

## Example Application

Machine chest press:
- Rep to failure
- Hold at mid-chest position
- Partner pushes down on weight stack/handles
- Fight to maintain position for 7-10 seconds
- Extreme pump and tension achieved
