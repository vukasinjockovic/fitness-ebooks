# Pump Workouts

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Pump Days, Optional Workouts, Frequency Workouts

## Summary

Pump Workouts are secondary training sessions in Mountain Dog Training designed to increase training frequency without the stress of Base workouts. They skip the 4-phase structure entirely, focusing solely on achieving maximum pump through supersets, trisets, and giant sets while avoiding heavy compound movements that tax joints and connective tissue.

## Purpose

1. **Add frequency** to your training plan
2. **Increase anabolic opportunities** (fix weaknesses)
3. **Reduce need for excessive cardio**

## Basic Rules

- **No barbells** on pump chest or shoulder days
- **No heavy squats or leg press** on pump leg days
- **No deadlifts or extremely heavy rowing** on pump back days
- **Often use supersets, trisets, or giant sets**
- **Goal:** Just get an insane pump!

## When to Add Pump Workouts

**Prerequisites:**
1. Peri-workout nutrition dialed in
2. Adapted to Mountain Dog training style
3. Recovering well from Base workouts
4. Minimal/no soreness lasting beyond 1-2 days

**Progression:**
- Start with 4 Base days/week
- Add Pump workouts as recovery allows
- Eventually train up to 6-7 days/week if desired

## Key Quotes

> "I love to train. I cannot stand taking more than 1 maybe 2 days off a week. Many people I coach are the same way. You have to be careful though. These pump days need to be done intelligently."
— Ch. 3

> "Pump workouts are how we increase training frequency. These are workouts that do not use the four stages."

## Caution

Without proper peri-workout nutrition, pump days lead to excessive soreness and inadequate recovery, defeating their purpose.
