# Activation Pattern

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Motor Unit Recruitment, Regional Activation, Exercise Selection

## Summary

Activation Pattern refers to how different exercises recruit different motor units and muscle regions, contributing to hypertrophy through varied stimulation. Mountain Dog Training leverages exercise variety and training to failure to maximize motor unit recruitment, recognizing that activation strategies vary substantially depending on exercise selection.

## Key Findings

1. **Activation varies by exercise:** MRI and EMG studies show activation strategies vary substantially depending on exercise choice for a given muscle group.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

2. **Regional hypertrophy possible:** Studies demonstrate region-specific increases in muscle size, meaning you can change the relative shape of a growing muscle.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

3. **Training to failure increases recruitment:** The closer to muscular failure, the greater the number of motor units (and muscle fibers) called into action.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

4. **Supersets enhance activation:** Coupling fatigue with two or more different movements creates a "one-two punch" for the same muscle group.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

5. **Rotation prevents adaptation:** Frequently rotating exercises may prevent the "repeated bout effect" where subsequent sessions produce less muscle damage.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

## Role in Mountain Dog Training

- **Exercise Variety:** Core principle - constantly varied exercises
- **Training to Failure:** Ensures all motor units recruited
- **Supersets:** Different movements hit different motor units in fatigued state
- **Bands on Handles:** Can affect activation pattern and feel of movement

## Practical Implications

**Shape changes are subtle:**
> "John's not promising you'll develop biceps peaks rivaling Ronnie Coleman's using his methods."
— Ch. 2

**But real:**
- Different exercises emphasize different regions
- Full development requires exercise variety
- Supersets combine exercises for complete stimulation
