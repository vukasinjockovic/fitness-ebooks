# Muscle Damage

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** DOMS, Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness, Eccentric Damage

## Summary

Muscle damage refers to the micro-tears and structural disruption in muscle fibers caused by training, particularly eccentric contractions. While not the primary goal of Mountain Dog Training, muscle damage is recognized as an inevitable outcome of heavy loading and a contributor to muscle remodeling. The approach aims to find the "sweet spot" - enough damage to stimulate adaptation without excessive inroads into recovery.

## Key Findings

1. **Eccentric focus:** Eccentric (lowering) contractions cause the majority of muscle damage and soreness.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

2. **Eccentrics more effective:** Compared to concentric contractions, eccentrics may be more effective at increasing acute muscle protein synthesis, as well as long-term strength and muscle growth.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

3. **Less metabolically stressful:** Eccentric contractions are much less metabolically demanding than concentrics, but load contractile elements more intensely.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

4. **Recovery nutrition critical:** Peri-workout supplementation can substantially reduce muscle damage, allowing higher training frequency.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 1-2

5. **Sweet spot exists:** NSAID research suggests there's an optimal level of muscle damage and inflammation for gains - neither too little nor excessive.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 2

## Role in Mountain Dog Training

- **Bands:** Intensify eccentric overload (band pulls weight down faster)
- **Peri-Workout Nutrition:** Reduces muscle damage to allow higher frequency
- **Exercise Rotation:** Prevents repeated bout effect (less damage on familiar exercises)
- **Not the goal per se:** Heavy loading produces damage as side effect, not primary target

## Managing Muscle Damage

**Goal:** Generate tremendous training stimulus while mediating excessive inroads into recovery.

**Strategies:**
1. Peri-workout carb-protein supplementation reduces damage
2. Exercise variety prevents repeated bout adaptation
3. Warm-up may reduce damage
4. Fatigue-induced weight reduction limits excessive damage during intensification

## NSAID Paradox

NSAIDs (aspirin, ibuprofen) present contradictory findings:
- **Reduce:** muscle damage, post-exercise protein synthesis, satellite cell proliferation
- **Yet:** Some studies show chronic NSAID use may promote muscle growth
- **Implication:** "Sweet spot" for damage exists; excessive damage may be counterproductive
