# Peri-Workout Nutrition

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Intra-Workout Nutrition, Workout Nutrition Timing

## Summary

Peri-workout nutrition in Mountain Dog Training refers to the strategic timing and composition of nutrition around training sessions. It's considered essential for recovery, allowing the implementation of pump workouts and higher training frequencies. The approach emphasizes easily digestible proteins (hydrolysates, EAAs) and carbohydrates consumed before and during training.

## Key Findings

1. **Resistance training turns on protein turnover:** Training is both anabolic and inherently catabolic - peri-workout nutrition optimizes the MPS/MPB balance.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 1

2. **Essential amino acids are key:** EAAs, especially leucine and BCAAs, are anabolic and anti-catabolic, rapidly spiking blood levels when consumed in free form.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 1

3. **Carb + protein synergy:** Adding carbohydrate to protein synergistically increases insulin, refuels glycogen, and inhibits protein breakdown.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 1

4. **Hydrolyzed proteins superior:** Di- and tripeptides are rapidly absorbed via specific intestinal transporter, more effectively driving post-exercise protein synthesis than constituent amino acids.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 1

5. **Reduces muscle damage:** Carbohydrate-protein supplements can reduce muscle soreness and damage, allowing higher training frequency.
   — Source: Brutality of Mountain Dog Training, Ch. 1

## Protocol

### Pre-Workout Meal (30-60 min before)
- Small to moderate amount of carbs for energy
- Add fat to slow glucose entry, prevent hypoglycemia
- Moderate amount of easily digestible protein

### Intra-Workout Supplementation
- Begin shortly before training starts
- EAAs or hydrolyzed protein (di/tripeptides)
- Carbohydrates for insulin spike
- Goal: nutrients in bloodstream when muscles are pumped

### Post-Workout Meal (45-60 min after)
- No rush - you had nutrition during training
- Balanced whole food meal (e.g., steak and rice)

## Why It's Essential

> "It is absolutely essential to get peri-workout nutrition nailed in order to recover well enough to implement these pump days."
— Ch. 3

**Benefits:**
1. Controls cortisol levels
2. Enhances glycogen replenishment
3. Improves long-term muscle gains
4. Reduces muscle damage and soreness
5. Enables higher training frequency
6. Drives nutrients into pumped muscles

## Soreness Guidelines

> "You should not be sore longer than 1 or 2 days, and honestly, I don't even want you sore at all really, maybe just a little pain when you stretch your pecs out the day after training them."
— Ch. 3
