# Mountain Dog Periodization Model

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** MD Macrocycle, Instinctive Periodization, 3-Phase Macrocycle

## Summary

The Mountain Dog Periodization Model is a ~3 month training macrocycle comprising three microcycles: Preparation, Destroyer, and Taper. Unlike rigid periodization schemes, it emphasizes instinctive adjustments based on individual recovery and performance, while providing general guidelines for duration.

## Core Principle: Instinctive Periodization

> "I am a strong believer in a much more instinctive approach to periodization. We are not mirror images of each other in terms of recuperative ability."
— Ch. 3

Key insight: Most people don't train hard enough to need periodization. It's only necessary for those who "train like an uncaged animal."

## The Three Microcycles

### 1. Preparation Microcycle (2-3 weeks)

**Purpose:** Build foundation, prepare for brutality ahead.

**Characteristics:**
- Lower volume
- High intensity
- Establishes baseline
- Progressive volume increase
- Eliminates muscle soreness from novel stimulus

**Principle:** "Get the most out of the least" - give yourself somewhere to go.

**Scientific Basis:**
- Novel exercise is more damaging than repeated bouts
- Less stressful training is adequate after breaks
- Starting with lower volume eliminates soreness while producing equivalent gains

### 2. Destroyer Microcycle (5-6 weeks)

**Purpose:** Maximum training stimulus, test limits.

**Characteristics:**
- "Balls out" intensity
- Higher volume
- Challenge sets included
- Tests desire and will
- Separates "champions from also-rans"

**Signs of Overreaching (Expected):**
- Loss of "pop" when training
- Difficulty elevating heart rate
- Tightness and stiffness
- Tendon discomfort on first eccentric motions
- Delayed soreness even after low volume
- Changes in appetite, body weight decrease
- Mental fuzziness during training

**Warning:** You cannot train like this indefinitely.

### 3. Taper Microcycle (2-3 weeks)

**Purpose:** Allow adaptations to manifest, realize gains.

**Characteristics:**
- Volume tapers greatly
- Intensity remains HIGH
- Quick recovery from overreaching
- Often see PRs during this phase
- Can be as short as 1 week if responding well

**Scientific Basis:**
- Maintaining intensity while reducing volume by 2/3 maintains muscle size
- Reduced caloric expenditure + heavy loads = powerful growth stimulus
- Tapering is most effective for strength when intensity is maintained

## Deloading Guidelines

After completing a macrocycle or when overreaching hits hard:

| Parameter | Deload Change |
|-----------|---------------|
| Weekly Frequency | Reduce to 3-4 days/week |
| Intensity/Effort | Cut sets short of failure by 2-3 reps |
| Session Volume | Reduce by 20% from Taper end |
| Intensification Techniques | Eliminate completely |

## Overtraining vs Overreaching

| Aspect | Overreaching | Overtraining |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Timeline | Weeks to months | Longer |
| Recovery | 1-2 weeks | Extended |
| Severity | Expected outcome | Problematic |
| Additional Signs | - | Loss of motivation, sleep disturbances, irritability, persistent fatigue, loss of libido |

## Volume Guidelines by Muscle Group

| Muscle Group | Low | Medium | High |
|--------------|-----|--------|------|
| Legs | 8-10 | 11-15 | 16-20 |
| Chest | 8-10 | 11-14 | 15-18 |
| Shoulders | 6-8 | 9-10 | 11-12 |
| Back | 10-11 | 12-15 | 16-20 |
| Biceps | 6-8 | 9-11 | 12-16 |
| Triceps | 6-8 | 9-11 | 12-16 |
