# Quotable Quotes from The Brutality of Mountain Dog Training

**Author:** John Meadows with Scott Stevenson, PhD
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03

## On Training Philosophy

> "Get the most out of the least."
— Ch. 3 (Preparation Microcycle)

> "Passion trumps everything."
— Dave Tate, quoted in Ch. 3

> "Training intensely is important, but training with a purpose is MORE important."
— Fouad Abiad testimonial

> "It's not about slaughtering your body to the point of no return, it's about stimulating new growth and if you puke in the process that's ok too."
— Fouad Abiad testimonial

## On Intensity and Pain

> "You have to destroy what you think your pain threshold is and learn to do this on a regular basis."
— Ch. 1 (Phase 3)

> "He asks me how many reps he should do on the dumbell bench press with 150 lb dumbbells. I reply 'Twenty five.' His jaw drops, as he doesn't think it is even possible. Then he does it. Bam."
— Ch. 2 (Challenge Sets)

> "Don't just push the weight from point A to point B. We want to feel everything work."
— Ch. 2 (Intensification Techniques)

## On Longevity and Injury Prevention

> "Training for me, and I assume for you as well, is about success over the long haul and being able to look and feel good and enjoy the process until you die."
— Ch. 1 (Phase 1)

> "It's kind of hard to make gains when you are on the sideline. That's just not worth it."
— Ch. 1 (Pre-Pump Injury Prevention)

## On Periodization and Instinct

> "I have never bought into periodization in the way it has been presented as a simple numbers game."
— Ch. 3 (Periodization)

> "Why would you train 3 weeks hard, then back off for a week if you feel awesome after three weeks and are still smashing records and growing at a fast rate?"
— Ch. 3 (Follow Your Instincts)

> "We are not mirror images of each other in terms of recuperative ability."
— Ch. 3 (Instinctive Training)

## On Overtraining

> "I don't believe that most people train hard enough to even need periodization."
— Ch. 3 (Periodization: For Animals Only)

> "Go to your local gym tonight and look around: What you'll see is the majority of people 'training' are talking on their phones, texting, flirting, etc."
— Ch. 3

## On The Pump

> "I like the pump, and I think it can be an excellent barometer for muscle growth in that the pumped up muscle uniquely reflects progress in that area."
— Ch. 1 (Phase 3)
