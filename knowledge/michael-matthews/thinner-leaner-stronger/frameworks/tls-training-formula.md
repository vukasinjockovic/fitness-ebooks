# TLS Training Formula

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Thinner Leaner Stronger Formula, Women's Training Formula

## Summary

The TLS Training Formula is the women's adaptation of the BLS formula. The key difference is the rep range: 8-10 reps instead of 4-6. This modification accounts for women's generally lower testosterone levels while still emphasizing progressive overload with heavy compound movements.

## The Formula

> "The Thinner Leaner Stronger weight training method follows a formula that looks like this: 1 – 2 | 8 – 10 | 12 | 1 – 2 | 45 – 60 | 5 – 7 | 8 – 10"
— Ch. 18

### Decoded

| Element | Value | Meaning |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1-2 | Major muscle groups | Train 1-2 major muscle groups per day |
| 8-10 | Reps per set | Working rep range for nearly all exercises |
| 12 | Working sets | Total working sets per muscle group |
| 1-2 | Rest minutes | Rest 1-2 minutes between sets |
| 45-60 | Workout minutes | Keep workouts under 60 minutes |
| 5-7 | Recovery days | Train each muscle group once every 5-7 days |
| 8-10 | Deload weeks | Take a week off every 8-10 weeks |

## Key Difference from BLS

| Aspect | TLS (Women) | BLS (Men) |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Rep range | 8-10 | 4-6 |
| Working sets | 12 | 9-12 |
| Rest period | 1-2 min | 2-3 min |

## Rep Range Rationale

> "You will be doing 8 – 10 repetitions per set on virtually all exercises (the only exception to this is when training abs, for which I recommend using weight that allows you to do 15 – 20 reps)."
— Ch. 18

> "What is meant by doing 8 – 10 reps? It means that you should use weights that are light enough to allow you to get at least 8 reps, but also heavy enough to prevent you from doing more than 10 reps."
— Ch. 18

## Progressive Overload

> "When you can do a set of 10 reps with perfect form, you should add weight to your next set—5 pounds for dumbbell exercises, and 10 pounds for barbell exercises. This should allow you to do 8 reps on your next set, and you build your strength from there over the next few weeks."
— Ch. 18

## Why Not Lighter?

Matthews explicitly rejects the common advice to use very light weights:

> "This style of training will probably be new to you, as many weightlifting programs for women call for using extremely light weights. As you now know, however, that this style of training does very little to improve your strength and muscle size and definition."
— Ch. 18
