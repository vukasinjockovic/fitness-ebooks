# 8 Muscle Building Myths & Mistakes (Women)

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Women's Training Mistakes, Female Fitness Myths

## Summary

TLS presents 8 common myths and mistakes that prevent women from building the lean, toned physique they desire. These myths perpetuate ineffective training methods and keep women trapped in cycles of minimal results.

## The 8 Myths

### Myth #1: More Sets = More Muscle

> "While we need to push ourselves in our workouts and do the right exercises, putting in more than about an hour or so will cause more harm than good."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Excessive volume leads to overtraining, not more growth. Quality over quantity.

### Myth #2: Pump = Growth

> "The pump is caused by blood being trapped in the muscles, and it's not an indicator of future muscle growth."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** The pump feels good but doesn't drive hypertrophy. Progressive overload does.

### Myth #3: Soreness = Growth

**Reality:** Muscle soreness (DOMS) indicates the muscle was stressed, but its absence doesn't mean the workout was ineffective.

### Myth #4: Training to Failure is Essential

**Reality:** Going to absolute failure every set increases injury risk and impairs recovery. Get close to failure, but don't chase it obsessively.

### Myth #5: Wasting Time with the Wrong Exercises

> "In case you didn't know, most of what your gym has to offer in terms of workout machines and contraptions is worthless."
— Ch. 5

> "More specifically, the most effective muscle-building exercises are known as compound exercises. They're called compound exercises because they involve multiple muscle groups."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Compound movements (Squat, Deadlift, Bench Press) beat isolation exercises and machines.

### Myth #6: Training Like an Idiot

> "Most people don't have a clue as to the proper form of exercises. And, this lack of knowledge stunts their gains; causes unnecessary wear and tear on ligaments, tendons, and joints; and opens the door to debilitating injuries."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Proper form is non-negotiable. Ego lifting leads to injury and poor results.

### Myth #7: Training Like a Wussy

> "Quite frankly, most people just train like wussies. They don't want to push themselves."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Results require intensity. Comfort zones don't build muscle.

### Myth #8: Eating to Stay Weak or Get Fat

> "What most people don't know, however, is what constitutes sufficient nutrition. Most people are way, way off."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Nutrition makes or breaks your results. You can't out-train a bad diet.

## The Compound Exercise Study

> "Two groups of men trained with weights for ten weeks. Group one did four compound upper-body exercises, while group two did the same plus bicep curls and tricep extensions (isolation exercises). After the training period, both groups increased strength and size, but which do you think had bigger arms? The answer is neither. The additional isolation training performed by group two produced no additional effect on arm strength or circumference."
— Ch. 5 (Ball State University, 2000)

## Charles Poliquin Insight

> "Charles Poliquin, trainer to world-class athletes like Olympians and professional sports players, is fond of saying that in order to gain an inch on your arm, you have to gain ten pounds of muscle. His point is the most effective way to build a muscular, strong body is with systemic overload, not localized training."
— Ch. 5
