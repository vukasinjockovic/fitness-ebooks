# 5 Fat Loss Myths & Mistakes (Women)

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Women's Fat Loss Mistakes, Dieting Myths

## Summary

TLS presents 5 common fat loss myths that keep women from achieving their physique goals. These myths lead to frustration, wasted effort, and the yo-yo dieting cycle that plagues so many.

## The 5 Myths

### Myth #1: Counting Calories is Unnecessary

> "I don't know how many people I've consulted who wanted to lose weight but didn't want to have to count calories. This statement is about as logical as saying that you want to drive across the country but don't want to have to pay attention to your gas tank."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** You must create a caloric deficit to lose fat. Whether you call it "counting calories" or not, you need to regulate intake.

> "In order to lose fat, you must keep your body burning more energy than you're feeding it, and the energy potential of food is measured in calories."
— Ch. 7

### Myth #2: Do Cardio = Lose Weight

> "Every day I see overweight people grinding away on the cardio machines. And, week after week goes by with them looking the same."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** Cardio burns calories but doesn't guarantee fat loss if you're eating too much.

> "If you don't eat correctly, that nightly run or bike ride won't necessarily save you."
— Ch. 7

The math example:
> "Let's say you're trying to lose weight and are unwittingly eating six hundred calories more than your body burns during the day. You go jogging for thirty minutes at night, which burns about three hundred calories. That leaves you with a daily excess of three hundred calories."
— Ch. 7

### Myth #3: Chasing the Fads

> "The Atkins Diet. The South Beach Diet. The Paleo Diet. The HCG Diet (this one really makes me cringe). The Hollywood Diet. The Body Type Diet. It seems like a new one pops up every month or two."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** Fad diets work (when they work) because they create caloric deficits, not because of magic mechanisms.

> "The rules are the rules, and no fancy names or snake oil supplements will help you get around them."
— Ch. 7

### Myth #4: Low Weight/High Reps Builds "Lean Muscle"

> "This myth goes like this: If you want that lean, toned look, you want to do a BUNCH of reps with low weight. This is just plain wrong."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** There's no such thing as "lean muscle" vs regular muscle. Being lean is about low body fat, which requires diet. Building muscle requires overload, which requires heavier weights.

> "Research has shown that doing more than fifteen reps causes little to no improvement in muscle power or size due to insufficient overload. It only improves muscle endurance."
— Ch. 7

### Myth #5: Spot Reduction

> "How many people have you seen doing crunches 'to get a six pack'? How many women try to target their butts and thighs to 'burn away the fat'? Well, that's not how it works."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** You cannot target fat loss from specific body parts through exercise.

> "You can't reduce fat in any particular area of your body by targeting it with exercises. You can reduce fat by proper dieting, and your body will decide how it comes off."
— Ch. 7

## The Twinkie Diet Proof

Professor Mark Haub proved calories are king:

> "Two-thirds of his daily calories came from Twinkies, Little Debbies, Doritos, sugary cereals, and Oreos—a 'convenience store diet,' as he called it. And he not only lost the weight, but his 'bad' cholesterol, or LDL, dropped 20 percent."
— Ch. 8
