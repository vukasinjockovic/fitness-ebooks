# HIIT Cardio Protocol (TLS)

**Type:** program
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** High-Intensity Interval Training, TLS Cardio

## Summary

Matthews recommends HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) over traditional steady-state cardio for TLS. HIIT is more effective for fat loss, burns more calories in less time, and causes less muscle breakdown than long, low-intensity sessions.

## Why HIIT Over Steady-State

> "Long, low-intensity cardio sessions tend to negatively impact muscle growth and burn relatively few calories, thus rendering them ineffective in helping with fat loss."
— Ch. 18

> "Studies such as those conducted by Laval University, East Tennessee State University, Baylor College of Medicine, and the University of New South Wales have shown that shorter, high-intensity sessions, however, not only cause less muscle breakdown than low-intensity, steady-state cardio, but they burn more calories and stimulate more fat loss."
— Ch. 18

## The 3 Commandments of Cardio

### Commandment #1: Do Cardio 3-5 Times Per Week

> "Studies have shown that 3 cardio sessions per week is enough to improve cardiovascular function and muscle growth."
— Ch. 18

For getting super lean (under 15% body fat), most women need cardio because:
> "You can only cut calories so much before you lose strength and muscle."
— Ch. 18

### Commandment #2: Do HIIT for 20-30 Minutes

The protocol:

1. **Warm-up:** 2-3 minutes low intensity
2. **High-intensity interval:** 30-60 seconds all-out effort
3. **Recovery interval:** Same duration as high-intensity (or 1.5-2x if new to HIIT)
4. **Repeat:** Cycle for 20-30 minutes
5. **Cool-down:** 2-3 minutes low intensity

> "If you're new to HIIT, 30-second intervals will be plenty, but you want to try to work toward being able to do 60-second intervals."
— Ch. 18

### Commandment #3: Separate Weights and Cardio by Several Hours

> "Combining resistance exercise and cardio in the same session may disrupt genes for anabolism."
— Ch. 18 (RMIT University, 2009)

Effects of combining:
- Cardio before weights: Suppresses anabolic hormones (IGF-1, MGF)
- Cardio after weights: Increases muscle tissue breakdown

> "I currently put about 14 hours in between my weights and cardio because I lift early in the morning and do cardio around 9:30 pm."
— Ch. 18

**Minimum separation:** 2-3 hours

## If You Must Combine

> "If there is no way that you can split up your cardio and weightlifting, I recommend doing your cardio after lifting (never before) and doing no more than 20 – 30 minutes of HIIT."
— Ch. 18

## HIIT Options

You can apply HIIT to any cardio modality:
- Walking/sprinting outdoors
- Elliptical trainer
- Recumbent bike
- Stairmaster
- Rowing machine
