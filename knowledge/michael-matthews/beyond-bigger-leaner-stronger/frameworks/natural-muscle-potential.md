# Natural Muscle Potential

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Genetic Limits, Maximum Natural Gains

## Summary

Evidence-based formulas for estimating how much muscle a natural lifter can build over their lifetime. Matthews presents several models and validates them against his own experience and coaching hundreds of clients.

## The McDonald Model (Lyle McDonald)

Expected muscle gains per year of proper training:

| Year | Expected Muscle Gain |
|------|---------------------|
| Year 1 | 20-25 lbs |
| Year 2 | 10-12 lbs |
| Year 3 | 5-6 lbs |
| Year 4+ | 2-3 lbs |

**Implication:** Majority of natural muscle (35-45 lbs) gained in first 3 years.

## The Berkhan Model (Martin Berkhan)

Maximum muscular potential at 5% body fat:

> "Height in centimeters – 100 = upper limit of weight in kilograms at 5% body fat"
— Ch. 4

| Height | Max Weight @ 5% BF |
|--------|-------------------|
| 5'6" (168 cm) | 150 lbs (68 kg) |
| 5'8" (173 cm) | 161 lbs (73 kg) |
| 5'10" (178 cm) | 172 lbs (78 kg) |
| 6'0" (183 cm) | 183 lbs (83 kg) |
| 6'2" (188 cm) | 194 lbs (88 kg) |

## Realistic Expectations

> "The reality is 190 pounds at 5 to 7% is huge by normal standards. You're fitness cover model material. Girls will love you, and guys will want to be you."
— Ch. 4

> "Unless you're trying to reach freak status, you can achieve the look you want naturally."
— Ch. 4

## Matthews' Personal Experience

Starting point: 155 lbs

After 1.5 years of **improper** training:
- 175 lbs (gained ~20 lbs total, ~15 lbs muscle)
- Riding newbie gains poorly

After 6-7 years of **improper** training:
- 200 lbs at 17% body fat
- Only gained additional 10-15 lbs muscle in 5+ years
- "Pretty bad considering how much time I had put in"

After switching to proper training:
- 184 lbs at 6% body fat
- Dramatic transformation in a few years

> "If we compare the gains I made in just a few years against the above formulas, we see that I've done VERY well for being an advanced lifter. But that's only because I made about half the gains I should've made in my first 6 to 7 years of training."
— Ch. 4

## Key Insight

> "You can gain a LOT of muscle in your first 3 years of training if you do it right. It starts to slow down at that point. And if you've been training improperly for several years, you can make startling gains by doing it right."
— Ch. 4

## Implications for BBLS

Why prerequisites matter:

> "If you're new to weightlifting, 1 to 2 years of BLS will give you 25 to 35 pounds of muscle growth, with an emphasis on myofibrillar growth. It builds a solid foundation of size and strength."
— Ch. 6

BBLS is for those who have already captured most of their "easy" gains and need periodization to continue progress.

## Don't Compare to Fake Naturals

> "Before you start comparing your measurements to other very unnatural 'natural' bodybuilders online and feel completely inadequate or worry that your numbers will never reach the ideal proportions laid out in this chapter, I want you to understand how much muscle you can build naturally."
— Ch. 4
