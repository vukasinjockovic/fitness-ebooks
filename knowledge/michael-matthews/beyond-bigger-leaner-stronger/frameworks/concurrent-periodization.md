# Concurrent Periodization

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Power Bodybuilding, Russian Periodization

## Summary

The periodization model used in Beyond Bigger Leaner Stronger. Unlike linear periodization (which focuses on one rep range for weeks) or nonlinear (which rotates focus), concurrent periodization trains all rep ranges in each workout, with emphasis on one range. Developed in Russia and popularized by Louie Simmons of Westside Barbell.

## The Three Periodization Models

### 1. Linear Periodization

> "Linear periodization is probably the most common, and it's very simple: it starts with a period of high volume of low-intensity exercise and works gradually toward a low volume of high-intensity exercise, or vice versa."
— Ch. 5

**Problem:**
> "When you train in just one rep range for too long, you're building up one biomechanical capacity (muscle endurance with higher reps, pure strength with lower reps, etc.) but detraining (losing performance) in others."
— Ch. 6

### 2. Nonlinear Periodization

> "Nonlinear periodization uses shorter periods, and good programs entail 2 to 3 weeks of focusing on training in a certain rep range while training the others at a maintenance level."
— Ch. 6

Example cycle:
- 2-3 weeks: Focus on 10-12 reps (maintenance for 1-3 and 4-6)
- 2-3 weeks: Focus on 4-6 reps (maintenance for 1-3 and 10-12)
- 2-3 weeks: Focus on 1-3 reps (maintenance for 4-6 and 10-12)

### 3. Concurrent Periodization (BBLS Choice)

> "Concurrent periodization has you train each biomechanical capacity (rep range) in each workout. This method was developed in Russia and is touted by many strength and bodybuilding experts (such as Louie Simmons of Westside Barbell fame) as the optimal way to periodize your training."
— Ch. 6

Example workout structure:
- Sets 1-3: 1-3 rep range (Power)
- Sets 4-7: 4-6 rep range (Myofibrillar)
- Sets 8-10: 8-10 rep range (Sarcoplasmic)

## Why Concurrent Works for Advanced Lifters

1. **Maintains all capacities** - No detraining in any rep range
2. **Addresses all growth pathways** - Tension, damage, and fatigue
3. **Targets all fiber types** - Type IIx and IIa
4. **Keeps training fresh** - Variety in each session

## Critical Caveat for Natural Lifters

> "A common mistake trainers make with periodization is they simply try to do too much in each workout. You can't put equal emphasis on each major rep range without creating a workout that has you in the gym for 2+ hours, beating yourself into a pulp. This works very well with chemically enhanced weightlifters, but does not work well for us natural folk."
— Ch. 7

**Solution:** Emphasize one rep range (4-6 in BBLS) while maintaining others at lower volume.

## BBLS Implementation

| Set Type | Rep Range | % 1RM | Primary Adaptation |
|----------|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| Power Sets | 2-3 | 90%+ | Neurological/Strength |
| Myofibrillar Sets | 4-6 | 80-85% | Muscle fiber growth |
| Sarcoplasmic Sets | 8-10 | 70-75% | Fluid volume/Endurance |
