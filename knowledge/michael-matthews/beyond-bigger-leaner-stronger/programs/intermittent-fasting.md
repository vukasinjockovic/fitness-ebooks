# Intermittent Fasting Protocol

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** IF, Time-Restricted Eating

## Summary

Matthews provides a balanced, evidence-based review of intermittent fasting (IF). While acknowledging fasting's health benefits, he concludes that for healthy, exercising adults, IF doesn't offer clear advantages over traditional meal timing—it simply comes down to personal preference and lifestyle fit.

## What Is Intermittent Fasting?

> "Intermittent fasting (or IF as many people call it) is a style of dieting that revolves around restricting your eating for extended periods of time and then eating your day's worth of food during predetermined feeding windows."
— Ch. 20

Common protocols:
- 16:8 - Fast 16 hours, eat in 8-hour window
- 20:4 - Fast 20 hours, eat in 4-hour window
- Alternate day fasting - Eat one day, fast the next

## Debunking IF Myths

### Myth: Fasting Causes Muscle Loss

> "Don't worry—incorporating some fasting into your meal scheduling won't wreck your metabolism."
— Ch. 20

Research shows amino acids from muscle contribute to glucose only after extended fasting (16+ hours), and even prolonged fasts show minimal muscle loss:
> "8% [reduction]" after extended fasting studies
— Ch. 20

### Myth: Fasting Crashes Metabolism

> "Research has demonstrated that the metabolism speeds up after 36 to 48 hours of fasting."
— Ch. 20

## Proven Benefits of Fasting

> "Research conducted by scientists at the University of California demonstrated that alternate-day fasting decreased blood pressure, reduced oxidative damage to various tissues and DNA, improved insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake, and decreased fat mass."
— Ch. 20

Additional benefits:
- Elevated growth hormone (up to 5× at 40 hours)
- Reduced inflammation biomarkers
- Improved insulin sensitivity

> "The bottom line is fasting is good for the body. It's like a systemic reset button, allowing for the disposal of waste and the reparation of cells and the optimization of the body's many processes."
— Ch. 20

## The Key Question

> "Do they [IF protocols] work better than traditional, continuous energy diets?"
— Ch. 20

Matthews' conclusion: **Not really, for healthy exercising adults.**

> "Exercising alone provides many of the health benefits associated with intermittent fasting."
— Ch. 20

## Matthews' Personal Experience

> "I've both tried intermittent fasting routines and worked with many people who have, and I've yet to see anything notably different in terms of results. Yes, it works—you can use it to build muscle or lose fat. But no, I don't believe it's the Holy Grail of dieting."
— Ch. 20

Why he doesn't use IF:
> "I hate the excessive fullness and lethargy that follows a large meal, and my schedule doesn't lend itself well to shorter feeding windows."
— Ch. 20

## Who IF Works For

> "If you're a healthy adult who exercises regularly, whether you should follow an intermittent fasting protocol just boils down to how you like to eat and what best fits your lifestyle."
— Ch. 20

Good candidates:
- People who don't mind large meals
- Those who find fewer meals easier to track
- People whose schedules allow for eating windows

Poor candidates:
- Those who get hungry after 10-12 hours
- People who feel lethargic from large meals
- Those with irregular schedules

## Practical Applications

### Occasional Fasting
> "Even if you enjoy a more traditional style of dieting like I do, you can work in a planned fast once or twice per week. I occasionally do by simply skipping breakfast on a day that I'm not lifting... and eating my first meal after about 12 to 14 hours of fasting."
— Ch. 20

### Pre-Feast Strategy
> "You save up calories/macros for the upcoming feast by eating nothing but protein leading up to it. Skip post-workout carbs and everything else—just have protein all day."
— Ch. 18

## Popular IF Methods Reviewed

1. **Leangains (16:8)** - Best for weightlifters, feeding window later in day
2. **Eat Stop Eat (24-hour fasts)** - Weekly 24-hour fasts
3. **Warrior Diet (20:4)** - One large meal at night
4. **Alternate Day Fasting** - Better for non-exercisers focused on weight loss
