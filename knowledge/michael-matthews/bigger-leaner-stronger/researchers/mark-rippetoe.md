# Mark Rippetoe

**Type:** researcher
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Rip, Starting Strength Author

## Summary

Mark Rippetoe is a renowned strength coach with nearly four decades of experience in the industry. Author of the iconic "Starting Strength," his methods for the squat, deadlift, bench press, and military press are used by professional athletes and laymen alike. Matthews explicitly defers to Rippetoe as the authority on barbell lift technique.

## Key Role in BLS

### Form Authority

> "I'm going to pass the buck to the man whose work taught me—and hundreds of thousands of others—how to squat, bench press, deadlift, and military press heavy and pain-free: Mark Rippetoe."
— Ch. 17

> "'Rip,' as he's known, has been in this game for nearly four decades and is a renowned and highly respected strength coach. He's the author of several books, including the iconic Starting Strength, and his weightlifting methods are used by professional athletes of all kinds and laymen alike."
— Ch. 17

### The Barbell Philosophy

> "As the well-known strength coach and author Mark Rippetoe says, if you want to be strong, ditch the machines and pick up a barbell. As you'll see, that's what Bigger Leaner Stronger is all about: pushing, pulling, raising, and squatting barbells and dumbbells."
— Ch. 5

### Recommended Reading

> "I'm going to give you everything you need to perform the exercises properly and safely, but I definitely recommend that you read Starting Strength if you want to dive into the biomechanics of each movement."
— Ch. 17

## Programs Referenced

> "There are popular training programs out there that have you do nothing but these four exercises, such as Starting Strength and 5 x 5."
— Ch. 17

## Matthews' Endorsement

Matthews lists Rippetoe among the most respected people in the fitness industry who advocate heavy compound training:

> "Many of the most respected people in this industry, such as Brad Schoenfeld, Charles Poliquin, Mark Rippetoe, Layne Norton, Alan Aragon, Lyle McDonald, and Pavel Tsatsouline, also advocate the same style of training for maximal results in the gym."
— Ch. 16
