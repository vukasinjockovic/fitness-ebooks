## Summary

Charles Poliquin is a world-class strength coach who has trained Olympic athletes and professional sports players. In BLS, he's cited for his insight about the systemic nature of muscle growth—specifically that arm growth requires total body mass increase.

## Key Finding in BLS

### The 10-Pound Rule

> "Charles Poliquin, trainer to world-class athletes like Olympians and professional sports players, is fond of saying that to gain an inch on your arms, you have to gain 10 pounds of muscle."
— Ch. 5

This quote supports Matthews' argument for compound movements over isolation exercises and the concept of systemic overload.

## Matthews' Endorsement

Listed among the most respected people in the fitness industry:

> "Many of the most respected people in this industry, such as Brad Schoenfeld, Charles Poliquin, Mark Rippetoe, Layne Norton, Alan Aragon, Lyle McDonald, and Pavel Tsatsouline, also advocate the same style of training for maximal results in the gym."
