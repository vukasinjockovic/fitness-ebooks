# The BLS Workout Routine

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** BLS Program, 5-Day Split, BLS Phase 1

## Summary

The complete workout routine from Bigger Leaner Stronger, structured as 5-day, 4-day, or 3-day options. Each training phase runs 8-10 weeks before deload/rest, with exercises rotated after each phase while core lifts remain constant.

## 5-Day Template (Recommended)

### Overview

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| Day 1 | Chest & Calves |
| Day 2 | Back & Abs |
| Day 3 | Shoulders & Calves |
| Day 4 | Upper Body & Abs |
| Day 5 | Legs & Shoulders |

### Day 1: Chest & Calves

- **Incline Barbell Bench Press** – Warm-up + 3 working sets (4-6 reps)
- **Incline Dumbbell Bench Press** – 3 working sets
- **Flat Barbell Bench Press** – 3 working sets
- *Optional:* **Dip (Chest Variation)** – 3 working sets (weighted)
- **Calf Workout A**

### Day 2: Back & Abs

- **Barbell Deadlift** – Warm-up + 3 working sets
- **Barbell Row** – 3 working sets
- **Wide-Grip Pull-Up or Chin-Up** – 3 working sets (weighted)
- *Optional:* **Close-Grip Lat Pulldown** – 3 working sets
- *Optional:* **Barbell Shrugs** – 2 working sets
- **3-6 Ab Circuits**

### Day 3: Shoulders & Calves

- **Seated/Standing Barbell Military Press** – Warm-up + 3 working sets
- **Side Lateral Raise** – 3 working sets
- **Bent-Over Rear Delt Raise** – 3 working sets
- **Calf Workout B**

### Day 4: Upper Body & Abs

- **Incline Barbell Bench Press** – Warm-up + 3 sets (8-10 reps)
- **Barbell Curl** – Warm-up + 3 working sets
- **Close-Grip Bench Press** – 3 working sets
- **Alternating Dumbbell Curl** – 3 working sets
- **Seated Triceps Press** – 3 working sets
- **3-6 Ab Circuits**

### Day 5: Legs & Shoulders

- **Barbell Squat** – Warm-up + 3 working sets
- **Leg Press** – 3 working sets
- **Romanian Deadlift** – 3 working sets
- **Side Lateral Raise** – 3 sets (8-10 reps)
- **Bent-Over Rear Delt Raise** – 3 sets (8-10 reps)
- **Calf Workout C**

## Programming Principles

### Frequency Preference

> "Lift weights three to five times per week, with four being better than three, and five being better than four."
— Ch. 18

> "You can certainly make gains lifting 3 or 4 times per week, and I'm going to show you exactly how to do this, but you will do best if you can somehow work in five sessions every week."
— Ch. 18

### Set Structure

- **Working sets:** Heavy, 4-6 rep range
- **Optional sets:** Additional volume when energy permits
- **8-10 rep sets:** Used for secondary chest work and additional shoulder volume

### Exercise Categories

> "An easy, effective way to program a workout is to do 3 to 6 sets of your 'nonnegotiable' exercises followed by 3 to 6 sets of your 'negotiable' exercises and to change the 'negotiables' every eight to ten weeks, after your rest or deload weeks."
— Ch. 18

**Nonnegotiable (never change):**
- Squat
- Deadlift
- Bench Press
- Military Press

**Negotiable (rotate after each phase):**
- Accessory exercises
- Isolation work
- Exercise variations

## Scheduling Flexibility

> "In terms of which days to train on, most people like to lift Monday through Friday and take the weekends off, maybe doing some cardio on one or both of these days. This works well. Feel free to work your rest days however you want, though."
— Ch. 18

## Key Quotes

> "How you train is just as important as the exercises you do. If you do all the right exercises but fail to follow the formula by doing things like lifting too little weight, resting too little, doing too few or too many sets per workout, and so forth, you'll make less-than-optimal gains."
— Ch. 18
