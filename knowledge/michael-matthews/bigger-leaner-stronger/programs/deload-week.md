# Deload Week

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Recovery Week, Rest Week

## Summary

Every 8-10 weeks, the BLS program includes either a deload week (reduced intensity training) or complete rest from weights. This allows for recovery from accumulated fatigue and sets up the next training phase for continued progress.

## The Protocol

### Timing

> "Between each of its eight-week phases, the Bigger Leaner Stronger program includes a choice between what is known as a deload week and several days, or even an entire week, off the weights."
— Ch. 16

### Two Options

#### Option 1: Deload Week

> "It simply involves lower-intensity training for a week."
— Ch. 16

Typical approach:
- Reduce weight by 40-50%
- Reduce volume (fewer sets)
- Keep same exercises
- Focus on form and recovery

#### Option 2: Full Rest

> "If you don't feel reinvigorated by the end of them and physically and mentally ready to hit the heavy weights again, then I recommend that you try no training whatsoever for at least four to five days before getting back to it."
— Ch. 16

### Recommendation

> "I recommend that you start with deload weeks, but if you don't feel reinvigorated by the end of them and physically and mentally ready to hit the heavy weights again, then I recommend that you try no training whatsoever for at least four to five days before getting back to it."
— Ch. 16

## Nutrition During Deload/Rest

> "In terms of how to eat on your deload week or week off the weights, if you're bulking, you can reduce your calories to a maintenance level, and if you're cutting, you don't have to change anything."
— Ch. 16

| Phase | Deload Nutrition |
|-------|------------------|
| Bulking | Drop to maintenance calories |
| Cutting | Keep cutting calories the same |
| Maintaining | No change needed |

## Why It's Necessary

Heavy training accumulates:
- Joint stress
- Central nervous system fatigue
- Hormonal suppression
- Psychological burnout

The deload allows:
- Tissue repair
- CNS recovery
- Hormonal normalization
- Mental freshness

## Programming Context

> "An easy, effective way to program a workout is to do 3 to 6 sets of your 'nonnegotiable' exercises followed by 3 to 6 sets of your 'negotiable' exercises and to change the 'negotiables' every eight to ten weeks, after your rest or deload weeks."
— Ch. 18

The deload marks the transition between training phases:
1. Complete 8-week phase
2. Deload or rest
3. Begin new phase with rotated "negotiable" exercises

## Signs You Need a Deload

- Stalled progress for multiple weeks
- Chronic fatigue
- Decreased motivation
- Minor aches becoming persistent
- Sleep disruption
- Mood changes

## Common Mistakes

1. **Skipping deloads** - Leads to burnout and injury
2. **Going too light** - Some stimulus still beneficial
3. **Adding cardio to "make up for it"** - Defeats the purpose
4. **Changing diet drastically** - Minor adjustments only
