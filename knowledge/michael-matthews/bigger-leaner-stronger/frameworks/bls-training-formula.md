# The BLS Training Formula

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Bigger Leaner Stronger Formula, 1-2-4-6 Formula

## Summary

The core training prescription of Bigger Leaner Stronger, encoded as: **1–2 | 4–6 | 9–12 | 3–4 | 45–60 | 5–7 | 8–10**. This formula represents the key training parameters for natural weightlifters to maximize muscle growth while avoiding overtraining.

## Formula Breakdown

| Number | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **1-2** | Train 1-2 muscle groups per workout |
| **4-6** | Work in the 4-6 rep range (80-85% 1RM) |
| **9-12** | Perform 9-12 heavy working sets per workout |
| **3-4** | Rest 3-4 minutes between sets |
| **45-60** | Complete workouts in 45-60 minutes |
| **5-7** | Train each muscle group every 5-7 days |
| **8-10** | Deload or rest every 8-10 weeks |

## Key Principles

### 1-2 Muscle Groups Per Day

> "By training only one or two muscle groups per day, you'll be able to give your workouts 100 percent focus and intensity and train hard without struggling through the systemic and muscular fatigue that comes with trying to do too much in a workout."
— Ch. 16

### 4-6 Rep Range

Working at 80-85% of 1RM is based on research showing this range optimizes both strength and hypertrophy for trained individuals. Matthews cites:

- Goteborg University review (2007): 70-85% 1RM produces maximum hypertrophy
- American College of Sports Medicine (2002): 5-6 rep max most effective for strength
- Arizona State University meta-analysis: 80% 1RM produces maximal strength gains

### 9-12 Working Sets

> "Research and anecdotal evidence has shown me that 50 to 70 heavy reps performed with each major muscle group every five to seven days is a 'sweet spot' for getting the most out of natural weightlifting."
— Ch. 16

### 3-4 Minute Rest

Based on State University of Rio de Janeiro review:
> "3-5 minutes between sets produced greater increases in absolute strength, due to higher intensities and volumes of training."

## Practical Application

1. Select primary compound movement for target muscle
2. Work up to working weight
3. Perform 3 sets of 4-6 reps, adding weight when 6 reps achieved
4. Rest 3-4 minutes between sets
5. Add accessory work (3 additional sets optional)
6. Complete within 60 minutes
