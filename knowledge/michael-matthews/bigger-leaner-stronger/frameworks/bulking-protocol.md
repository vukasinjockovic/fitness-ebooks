# BLS Bulking Protocol

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Lean Bulk, Clean Bulk Protocol

## Summary

A controlled caloric surplus approach to building muscle that avoids excessive fat gain. Based on the principle that a modest surplus (not "dreamer bulk" levels) optimizes muscle growth while keeping insulin sensitivity high and fat accumulation manageable.

## The Formula

### Standard Bulking Macros

| Macro | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| **Protein** | 1.0g per pound of body weight |
| **Carbs** | 2.0g per pound of body weight |
| **Fat** | 0.4g per pound of body weight |

**Example (175 lb male):**
- Protein: 175g (700 calories)
- Carbs: 350g (1,400 calories)
- Fat: 70g (630 calories)
- **Total: ~2,730 calories**

### Alternative Calculation

1. Calculate TDEE
2. Add 10-15% surplus (300-500 calories)
3. Prioritize protein at 1g/lb
4. Fill remaining calories with carbs and fats

## Key Principles

### Why Bulking Works

> "When you raise your calorie intake and take your body out of a deficit, your anabolic hormone levels rise, and your body's ability to synthesize proteins is restored to normal levels."
— Ch. 14

### Why "Dirty Bulks" Fail

> "As body fat levels rise, insulin sensitivity drops, which means that cells become less responsive to insulin's signals."
— Ch. 14

Consequences of excessive fat gain:
- Impaired ability to burn fat
- Increased likelihood of storing carbs as fat
- Suppressed protein synthesis
- Decreased testosterone
- Increased estrogen

### Body Fat Thresholds

| Guideline | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Don't start bulk if | Over 15% body fat |
| End bulk when | Reaching 15-17% body fat |
| Resume bulk after cut to | 10% body fat |

## Progress Expectations

### Rate of Gain
- New lifters: 0.5-1 lb per week possible
- Intermediate: 0.25-0.5 lb per week
- Advanced: 1-2 lbs per month

> "Some guys need to eat a lot to make steady progress on the scale. I've worked with 170-pound guys who had to eat more than 4,000 calories per day just to gain 0.5 to 1 pound per week!"
— Ch. 14

### Warning Signs

If gaining strength but not size: **Not eating enough**

> "If you're gaining strength but not size, you're not eating enough."
— Ch. 14

## Why Body Recomposition Doesn't Work (For Most)

> "The only people who can effectively (and naturally) build muscle and lose fat simultaneously are newbies who have a fair amount of fat to lose and people who used to be in great shape."
— Ch. 14

For experienced lifters:
- Calorie deficit impairs protein synthesis
- Anabolic hormone levels drop
- Body cannot fully repair exercise-induced muscle damage

## Cardio During Bulk

- 2 HIIT sessions per week
- 25 minutes each
- Maintains metabolic conditioning
- Preserves insulin sensitivity

## Common Mistakes

1. **Eating too little** - Spinning wheels without size gains
2. **Eating too much** - Excessive fat gain, hormonal issues
3. **Getting too lean first** - Looking scrawny at low body fat
4. **Ignoring body fat percentage** - Bulking past 17%

## Practical Tips

- Track calories consistently
- Weigh yourself at same time daily
- Use weekly averages for progress assessment
- Adjust by 200-300 calories based on results
- Don't fear carbohydrates
