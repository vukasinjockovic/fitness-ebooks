# The Big Four Lifts

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Core Lifts, Foundational Movements, Compound Big 4

## Summary

The four fundamental barbell exercises that form the foundation of the Bigger Leaner Stronger program: Squat, Deadlift, Bench Press, and Military Press. Matthews argues these lifts have "proven power" validated by "over a century of bodybuilders, strongmen, and athletes."

## The Four Lifts

### 1. Squat (Barbell Back Squat)

**Primary muscles:** Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings
**Secondary:** Core, lower back, calves

Key points:
- Full depth: butt below parallel
- Strengthens every muscle in legs
- Improves flexibility, mobility, agility
- Effective core workout
- NOT bad for knees when done correctly

> "Many guys will do anything for leg training before putting the barbell on their backs, they're missing out on what many of the top strength coaches in the world consider the absolute toughest and most rewarding exercise we can do."
— Ch. 17

### 2. Deadlift

**Primary muscles:** Posterior chain, back, hamstrings, glutes
**Secondary:** Grip, core, traps

Key points:
- Keep spine neutral (not rounded)
- Pull through legs, not back
- Don't hyperextend at lockout
- Start light, perfect form first

### 3. Bench Press

**Primary muscles:** Chest, front deltoids, triceps
**Secondary:** Core (stabilization)

Key points:
- Touch bar to chest (full ROM)
- Not "six inches above chest"
- Proper form protects shoulders
- Foundation for all pressing strength

### 4. Military Press (Overhead Press)

**Primary muscles:** Shoulders, triceps
**Secondary:** Core, upper chest

Key points:
- Press to full lockout overhead
- Not stopping above chin
- Standing version preferred
- Builds shoulder strength and stability

## Why These Four?

> "Out of the hundreds and hundreds of exercises you could possibly do, four reign supreme. If you neglect them like I did when I started lifting, you're guaranteed to never reach your genetic potential."
— Ch. 17

### Full-Body Strength

> "Popular training programs have you do nothing but these four exercises, such as Starting Strength and 5 x 5."
— Ch. 17

### Compound Movement Benefits

From Ch. 5:
- Train multiple muscle groups simultaneously
- Allow for progressive overload
- Build functional strength
- Transfer to athletic performance

### Ball State University Study (2000)

Two groups trained for 10 weeks:
- Group 1: Four compound upper body exercises
- Group 2: Same + bicep curls and triceps extensions

Result: Both groups gained identical arm size - proving compound movements develop the entire body without isolation work.

## Form Principles

Matthews defers to Mark Rippetoe's Starting Strength for detailed form:

> "I'm going to teach you Rip's methods of pushing, pulling, and squatting because they've withstood the tests of time and large numbers. They're safe and effective."
— Ch. 17

### Common Form Errors

| Exercise | Common Error | Correction |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Bench | Stopping above chest | Touch bar to chest |
| Squat | Quarter reps | Butt below parallel |
| Deadlift | Rounded back | Neutral spine |
| Military | Stopping at chin | Full lockout overhead |

> "Not only does improper form dramatically reduce the effectiveness of exercises, but it also opens the door to injury."
— Ch. 17

## Programming in BLS

Each lift trained once per week with:
- 3 heavy working sets (4-6 reps)
- 3 optional additional sets
- Focus on adding weight when hitting 6 reps
