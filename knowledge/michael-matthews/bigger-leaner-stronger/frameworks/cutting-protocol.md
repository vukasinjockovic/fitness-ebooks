# BLS Cutting Protocol

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Bigger Leaner Stronger Diet, Fat Loss Protocol

## Summary

A macro-based approach to fat loss that prioritizes protein to preserve muscle mass, maintains moderate carbohydrates for training performance, and keeps fats at a functional minimum. Designed to produce 0.5-1 lb fat loss per week while maintaining strength.

## The Formula

### Standard (Under 25% Body Fat)

| Macro | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| **Protein** | 1.2g per pound of body weight |
| **Carbs** | 1.0g per pound of body weight |
| **Fat** | 0.2g per pound of body weight |

**Example (200 lb male):**
- Protein: 240g (960 calories)
- Carbs: 200g (800 calories)
- Fat: 40g (360 calories)
- **Total: ~2,120 calories**

### Modified (Over 25% Body Fat)

| Macro | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| **Protein** | 0.8g per pound of body weight |
| **Carbs** | 0.6g per pound of body weight |
| **Fat** | 0.3g per pound of body weight |

**Example (250 lb male at 30% BF):**
- Protein: 200g (800 calories)
- Carbs: 150g (600 calories)
- Fat: 80g (720 calories)
- **Total: ~2,120 calories**

### Extremely Obese (Over 30% Body Fat)

Calculate BMR, multiply by 1.2:
- 40% calories from protein
- 30% calories from carbs
- 30% calories from fat

## Key Principles

### High Protein Priority

> "If you don't eat enough protein while restricting calories for weight loss, you'll lose more muscle than you would if you had eaten an adequate amount."
— Ch. 8

### Moderate Carbohydrates

> "If you eat too few carbohydrates while in a calorie deficit, your training will suffer, your muscle repair will be impaired, and your hormone profile will become more catabolic."
— Ch. 8

### Minimum Essential Fats

> "If you eat too little dietary fat, you can experience a significant drop-off in testosterone levels and other undesirable effects."
— Ch. 8

## Progress Markers

After 7-10 days, assess:

| Marker | Positive Sign | Warning Sign |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Weight | Decreasing 0.5-1 lb/week | Unchanged or increasing |
| Clothes | Looser at waist | Tighter |
| Mirror | Looking leaner | Looking the same |
| Energy | Comfortable | Constantly drained |
| Strength | Stable or slight decrease | Dramatic decrease |
| Sleep | Normal | Anxious, restless |

## Common Pitfalls

### Hidden Calories
- 2 tbsp olive oil = 240 cal
- 2 tbsp mayo = 200 cal
- 3 cubes feta = 140 cal
- 3 tbsp cream in coffee = 80 cal

> "These 'little' additions add up every day and are by far the number-one reason why people fail to get results."
— Ch. 14

### What-the-Hell Effect

> "For many, the vicious cycle of indulge, regret, and seriously indulge feels inevitable and inescapable."
— Ch. 10

Solution: Self-compassion, not self-criticism after slips.

## Training Modifications During Cut

- **Keep lifting heavy** - Don't switch to high reps
- **Maintain progressive overload** focus
- **Add 3-5 HIIT cardio sessions** per week
- **Expect initial strength dip** that usually rebounds

> "Training heavy is especially important when you're cutting because the name of the game is muscle preservation."
— Ch. 16

## Cardio Recommendations

When cutting:
- 3-5 HIIT sessions per week
- 25 minutes per session
- Separate from weightlifting by several hours
- Never more than 5 sessions total
