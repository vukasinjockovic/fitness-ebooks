# Insulin Sensitivity

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Insulin Resistance, Cellular Insulin Response

## Summary

Insulin sensitivity refers to how responsive your cells are to insulin's signals. Higher sensitivity means better nutrient partitioning (more nutrients to muscle, less to fat). Body fat levels, exercise, and diet all influence insulin sensitivity, making it a key factor in body composition.

## Key Findings

### The Problem with Fat Gain

> "As body fat levels rise, insulin sensitivity drops, which means that cells become less responsive to insulin's signals. As the body becomes more insulin resistant, its ability to burn fat decreases, and the likelihood of storing carbohydrates as fat increases."
— Ch. 14

### Impact on Muscle Building

> "Furthermore, insulin resistance suppresses intracellular signaling responsible for protein synthesis, which means less total muscle growth."
— Ch. 14

### The Virtuous Cycle

> "Retaining insulin sensitivity is highly beneficial when you're eating a surplus of calories to build muscle, whereas insulin resistance inhibits muscle growth and promotes fat storage."
— Ch. 19

## How to Improve Insulin Sensitivity

### 1. Cardio

> "This is where cardio comes in because it improves insulin sensitivity and does so in a dose-dependent manner (meaning the more you do, the more benefits you get)."
— Ch. 19

### 2. Stay Lean

Starting a bulk at lower body fat means better nutrient partitioning throughout the bulk.

### 3. Resistance Training

Heavy weightlifting improves insulin sensitivity in muscle tissue.

## Insulin's Actual Role

Matthews debunks the "insulin makes you fat" myth:

> "Insulin tells the body to stop burning its fat stores and instead absorb some of the fatty acids and glucose in the blood and turn them into more body fat…but that's not what causes you to get fatter over time—overeating does."
— Ch. 12

### Anti-Catabolic Benefits

> "When insulin levels are elevated, the rate at which muscle proteins are broken down decreases. This, in turn, creates a more anabolic environment in which muscles can grow larger more quickly."
— Ch. 12

## Metabolic Syndrome Connection

> "Metabolic syndrome is a combination of disorders including high blood pressure, high insulin levels, obesity (with excessive weight in the abdomen area), high levels of triglycerides (particles in the body that carry fats), and low HDL (good) cholesterol levels."
— Ch. 12

## Practical Implications

| Body Fat Level | Insulin Sensitivity | Recommendation |
|----------------|--------------------|-----------------|
| <15% (men) | Good | Can bulk effectively |
| 15-20% (men) | Moderate | Cut to 10-12% first |
| >20% (men) | Poor | Definitely cut first |

## Key Quotes

> "All is not lost if you're not a part of the genetic elite, however, because an important factor in what your body does with the food you eat is insulin sensitivity, and this is something we can positively affect."
— Ch. 19
