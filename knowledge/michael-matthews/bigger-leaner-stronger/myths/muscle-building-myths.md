# 7 Muscle Building Myths

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Muscle Myths, Training Fallacies

## Summary

Chapter 5 of BLS systematically debunks seven common beliefs that prevent natural lifters from making optimal progress. These myths lead to wasted effort in the gym and explain why many people fail despite "working hard."

## The 7 Myths

### Myth 1: More Reps = More Definition

> "Getting that shredded look is just a matter of having a fair amount of muscle and a low amount of body fat—and nothing else. There aren't any special exercises that 'bring out the striations,' and burning your muscles out with tons of reps does nothing to improve your overall look."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Definition comes from low body fat, not high reps. Heavy training with proper diet creates the "toned" look.

### Myth 2: Pump and Burn Drive Growth

> "Muscle 'burn' and pump are not paramount in achieving muscle growth."
— Ch. 5

> "When your muscles are burning, what you're feeling is a buildup of lactic acid, which continues to accumulate as you contract your muscles again and again."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Progressive tension overload drives growth, not pump or burn.

> "A big 'pump' is also not a good predictor of future muscle growth. The pump you feel when training is the result of blood being 'trapped' in the muscles."
— Ch. 6

### Myth 3: Muscle Confusion Works

> "Guys who make the mistake of doing lots of ineffective exercises often believe the 'muscle confusion' myth, which is the belief that you have to constantly change up your routine to 'keep your body guessing' and make gains."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Muscles don't have cognitive abilities—they respond to mechanical tension, not novelty.

### Myth 4: Soreness = Effective Workout

**Reality:** Soreness (DOMS) indicates novel stimulus, not necessarily growth. You can grow without being sore and be sore without optimal growth.

### Myth 5: Time Under Tension Is Key

**Reality:** While time under tension matters somewhat, load (weight) is far more important. 10 seconds at 80% 1RM beats 60 seconds at 30% 1RM for muscle growth.

### Myth 6: Machines Are As Good As Free Weights

University of Saskatchewan study:
> "The free weight squat resulted in 43 percent more leg muscle activation than the Smith machine squat."
— Ch. 5

University of California study:
> "The free weight bench press resulted in greater activation of the upper-body muscles than the Smith machine bench press."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Free weights force greater muscle activation through stabilization demands.

### Myth 7: Isolation Builds Better Muscles

Ball State University Study (2000):
> "Both groups increased strength and size, but which do you think had bigger arms? The answer is neither. The additional isolation training performed by group two produced no additional effect on arm strength or circumference."
— Ch. 5

**Reality:** Compound movements provide systemic overload that isolation can't match.

## The Path to Misery

> "You've just learned the path to muscle-building misery: grind away for hours in the gym, do tons of 'burnout' sets, do the wrong exercises with bad form, avoid pushing yourself too hard, and eat incorrectly."
— Ch. 5

> "These mistakes are responsible for untold amounts of frustration, discouragement, and confusion. They're the prime reasons why guys make little or no gains and quit."
— Ch. 5

## What Actually Works

1. Heavy compound lifts (4-6 reps)
2. Progressive overload (add weight over time)
3. Adequate volume (9-12 sets per muscle per week)
4. Sufficient recovery (5-7 days between training same muscle)
5. Proper nutrition (calorie surplus, high protein)
