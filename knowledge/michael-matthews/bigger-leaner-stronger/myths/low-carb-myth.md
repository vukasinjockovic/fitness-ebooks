# The Low-Carb Superiority Myth

**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Keto Is Better, Carbs Are Bad

## Summary

Matthews addresses the belief that low-carb diets are inherently superior for fat loss or body composition. He presents research showing that when protein is matched and calories are equal, carbohydrate intake doesn't significantly impact fat loss results.

## The Claim

Many people believe:
- Low-carb diets cause more fat loss than high-carb diets
- Ketogenic diets have a "metabolic advantage"
- Carbohydrates prevent fat loss regardless of calories

## The Research

### When Protein Is Matched

> "There is no significant difference in weight loss."
— Ch. 12, citing 4 studies

### Initial "Advantage" Explained

When studies show early low-carb advantages:

> "Severely obese subjects with a high prevalence of diabetes or the metabolic syndrome lost more weight during six months on a carbohydrate-restricted diet than on a calorie- and fat-restricted diet, with a relative improvement in insulin sensitivity and triglyceride levels, even after adjustment for the amount of weight lost."
— Ch. 12

This often reflects:
1. **Water loss** - Glycogen depletion causes rapid scale drop
2. **Appetite suppression** - Ketosis can reduce hunger
3. **Protein differences** - Low-carb often means higher protein
4. **Compliance** - Simpler rules may improve adherence

### Ketogenic Diet Problems

> "KLC [ketogenic low-carbohydrate] and NLC [non-ketogenic low-carbohydrate] diets were equally effective in reducing body weight and insulin resistance, but the KLC diet was associated with several adverse metabolic and emotional effects. The use of ketogenic diets for weight loss is not warranted."
— Ch. 12

## Sugar Science

### HFCS vs Sucrose

> "Sucrose, HFCS, invert sugar, honey, and many fruits and juices deliver the same sugars in the same ratios to the same tissues within the same time frame to the same metabolic pathways. Thus…it makes essentially no metabolic difference which one is used."
— Ch. 12

> "The data presented indicated that HFCS is very similar to sucrose, being about 55 percent fructose and 45 percent glucose, and thus, not surprisingly, few metabolic differences were found comparing HFCS and sucrose."
— Ch. 12

## Why Carbs Are Beneficial

### Training Performance

Low carbs impair high-intensity exercise performance. Heavy lifting requires glycogen.

### Hormonal Benefits

Adequate carbs support testosterone and thyroid function during calorie restriction.

### Muscle Preservation

Post-workout carbs enhance protein synthesis environment through insulin's anti-catabolic effects.

### Psychological Sustainability

> "When you're training and dieting correctly, you'll look forward to the gym every day. You'll never feel starved, you'll get to eat foods you love, and you won't suffer from overpowering cravings."
— Ch. 2

## When Low-Carb Makes Sense

Matthews acknowledges low-carb can work for:
- People who prefer it
- Those with severe insulin resistance
- Those who find it easier to comply with

But it's not *required* or *superior* for fat loss.

## The Fundamental Principle

> "It doesn't matter what you eat. If your metabolism is healthy and you set your calorie intake correctly—if you maintain a moderate calorie deficit by eating a bit less energy than you burn every day—you will lose weight."
— Ch. 8

## Key Quotes

> "That's why research has shown that so long as they're eating less energy than they're burning, people lose fat equally well on high-carbohydrate or low-carbohydrate diets."
— Ch. 7
