# 5 Fat Loss Myths

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-04
**Aliases:** Diet Myths, Weight Loss Fallacies

## Summary

Chapter 7 of BLS debunks five common fat loss myths that prevent people from getting lean. These misconceptions lead to ineffective dieting strategies, wasted effort, and yo-yo weight cycling.

## The 5 Myths

### Myth 1: Counting Calories Doesn't Work

> "The real problem isn't counting calories but failing to make and follow a meal plan that allows you to eat foods you like while ensuring that you burn more fat than you store over time."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** Energy balance is physics. If you're not losing fat, you're eating too much—period.

### Myth 2: Cardio Is the Key to Fat Loss

> "Cardio can enhance fat loss in two ways—burning calories and speeding up your metabolic rate—but that's it."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** You can't out-cardio a bad diet. A 30-minute jog burns ~300-400 calories—easily negated by one snack.

### Myth 3: Chasing the "Fat-Burning Zone"

**Reality:** While lower-intensity exercise burns a higher percentage of calories from fat, higher intensity burns more total calories and more total fat.

### Myth 4: Spot Reduction Works

> "The reality is that training the muscles of a certain area of your body burns calories and can result in muscle growth, both of which certainly can aid in fat loss, but it doesn't directly burn the fat covering them to any significant degree."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** You can't do crunches to lose belly fat. Fat loss is systemic based on overall calorie deficit.

### Myth 5: High Reps = More Definition

> "Getting that shredded look is just a matter of having a fair amount of muscle and a low amount of body fat—and nothing else."
— Ch. 7

**Reality:** "Toned" is just muscle with low body fat. Heavy weights build muscle; calorie deficit reveals it.

## The Fed/Fasted Cycle

> "Your body flips between 'fed' and 'fasted' states every day, storing fat from the food you eat and then burning it once there's nothing left to use from the meals."
— Ch. 7

> "If you store just as much fat as you burn every day, your weight stays the same. If you store more fat than you burn (by overeating), you get fatter. And if you burn more fat than you store, you get leaner."
— Ch. 7

## Hidden Calorie Traps

Common mistakes people make:
- Underestimating portion sizes
- Forgetting cooking oils and condiments
- Drinking calories (juice, coffee additions, alcohol)
- "Healthy" foods that are calorie-dense
- Weekend overeating erasing weekday deficits

## What Actually Works

1. **Calculate TDEE accurately**
2. **Create moderate deficit** (20-25%)
3. **Track intake** (at least initially)
4. **Prioritize protein** (preserve muscle)
5. **Use cardio as a tool** (not the solution)
6. **Be patient** (sustainable rate: 0.5-1 lb/week)

## Heavy Weights During Cuts

> "A study published by Greek sports scientists found that men who trained with heavy weights (80 to 85 percent of 1RM) increased their metabolic rates over the following three days, burning hundreds more calories than the men who trained with lighter weights (45 to 65 percent of 1RM)."
— Ch. 7

## Key Quotes

> "Simply put, you can't get fatter unless you feed your body more energy than it burns, and you can't get leaner unless you feed it less energy than it burns."
— Ch. 7

> "That's why research has shown that so long as they're eating less energy than they're burning, people lose fat equally well on high-carbohydrate or low-carbohydrate diets."
— Ch. 7
