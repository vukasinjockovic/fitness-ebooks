# Healthy Carb Portions Guide

**Type:** Framework
**Status:** Solid
**Source:** The Renaissance Feast, Quick Guides (p. 124)
**Author:** Lori Shaw

## Summary

A quick reference guide for measuring healthy carbohydrate portions. Lists common carb sources with portion sizes for 10g through 50g net carbs.

## Key Distinction

> "*Grams of carbs FROM healthy sources, not total weight of food - this is the number that matches your template.*"

Net carbs = Total carbs - Fiber

## Practical Applications

### Post-Workout Meals
Higher GI options (rice, banana) for faster glycogen replenishment.

### General Meals
Lower GI options (oats, berries) for sustained energy.

### Meal Prep Portioning
Pre-measure carb sources for the week.

### Fruit as Carb Source
Understand how much fruit equals your carb allotment.

## Notable Observations

### Low-Carb Fruits (More Volume)
- Raspberries: 2 Cups = 20g carbs
- Strawberries: 1 pint = 20g carbs
- Blackberries: 2 Cups = 30g carbs

### Higher-Carb Fruits (Less Volume)
- Banana: 1 medium = 25g carbs
- Mango: 1 fruit = 25g carbs
- Grapes: 2 Cups = 30g carbs
