## The 6 Principles of Diet and Health

### Hierarchy with Health Impact Percentages

| Rank | Principle | Health Impact | Definition |
|------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| 1 | **Calorie Balance** | 60% | How much food you are eating per average day in relationship to how much energy you are burning |
| 2 | **Food Composition** | 20% | The sources of food you are consuming; animal vs plant proteins, fibrous vs simple carbs, types of fats, processed vs unaltered |
| 3 | **Macronutrient Amounts** | 10% | How much of each of proteins, carbs, and fats you are eating on average per day |
| 4 | **Nutrient Timing** | 5% | How often you eat and how you space out your meals and snacks |
| 5 | **Hydration** | 2.5% | How much fluid you take in from food and drink |
| 6 | **Supplements** | 2.5% | What kinds of extra pills, powders or extracts you are taking to enhance your diet |

## Key Concepts

### Unequal Effects Principle
> "It turns out that some of the principles of healthy eating are much more impactful on health than others. Some of the principles are hugely important; getting them right is a great start to a healthy diet, and getting them wrong can mean poor health even if the rest of the principles are applied spot-on."

### Wrong Conclusions from Equal Weighting
If all principles had equal weight, you'd conclude (incorrectly):
- "You can eat all the food you want, so long as it's timed correctly"
- "If you just take certain supplements, you can eat only junk food and be quite healthy"
- "If you eat only healthy foods, you can be 200lbs overweight and not suffer any ill effects"

### Focus Strategy
1. **First:** Get the biggest principles right (Calorie Balance, Food Composition)
2. **Then:** Address intermediate principles (Macros, Timing)
3. **Finally:** Optimize small principles (Hydration, Supplements)

> "Only getting these small ones right will do so little to improve your health, you'll barely notice."

## Health vs Performance Focus

This book differs from other RP materials by focusing on general health rather than physique/performance:

| Aspect | Understanding Healthy Eating | Renaissance Woman |
|--------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| Primary Goal | Health outcomes | Body composition |
| Target Audience | General adult population | Women athletes/dieters |
| Calorie Focus | Health maintenance | Fat loss/muscle gain |
| Supplements | Health benefits | Performance benefits |

## Disclaimers
