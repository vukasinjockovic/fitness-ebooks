# Renaissance Woman: Fat Loss, Muscle Growth & Performance Through Scientific Eating

**Authors:** Dr. Jen Case, Dr. Melissa Davis, Dr. Mike Israetel
**Publisher:** Renaissance Periodization (2019)
**Book:** RP 7

## Overview

A comprehensive nutrition guide specifically designed for female athletes and fitness enthusiasts. Written by PhDs in Sports Nutrition, Neurobiology, and Sport Physiology, this book provides evidence-based approaches to fat loss, muscle gain, and athletic performance with attention to female-specific physiological and psychological considerations.

## Core Premise

Scientific dieting based on controlled research produces more reliable and predictable results than anecdote-based approaches. The book identifies five fundamental principles that govern how the body responds to nutrition manipulation.

## Key Differentiators

- **Female-focused:** Addresses women-specific challenges including menstrual cycle effects, Female Athlete Triad, pregnancy nutrition
- **PhD-authored:** All authors hold doctorates in relevant fields
- **Practical frameworks:** Step-by-step procedures for fat loss, muscle gain, and performance
- **Psychology integration:** Full chapter on dieting psychology including the 4-step psychological reset protocol

## Quotable Insights

> "The more weight you lose in one continuous streak, the more hunger is upregulated. Not only is hunger upregulated, but hunger for super high calorie and tasty foods (pizza, ice cream, burgers, and so on) is upregulated the most."

> "If you're training twice a day, the first session is likely going to utilize quite a bit of glycogen... the intra and immediately post-workout carbs for the first workout are a great start to this process."

> "Eating more protein than needed (above what is required for maintenance) does NOT have any special effect on muscle growth beyond what any extra calories will."
