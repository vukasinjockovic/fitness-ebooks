# 4-Step Psychological Reset Protocol

**Type:** Framework
**Status:** Solid
**Source:** Renaissance Woman, Chapter 5
**Authors:** Dr. Jen Case, Dr. Melissa Davis, Dr. Mike Israetel

## Summary

A structured 4-phase protocol for recovering from diet burnout and unhealthy relationships with food. Designed for athletes who have over-dieted and developed aversion, anxiety, or unhealthy patterns around eating. Not a treatment for clinical eating disorders.

## When to Use This Protocol

**Symptoms indicating need for reset:**
- Dread of further dieting
- Aversion to traditional diet foods (chicken, rice)
- Intense cravings that don't subside even when indulged
- Growing hatred for counting/measuring/restriction
- Recurring fantasies of eating everything and never dieting again
- Above symptoms appearing at the START of a diet (not just end)

**Critical distinction:** End-of-diet fatigue is normal. Beginning-of-diet symptoms indicate deeper burnout.

**NOT for clinical eating disorders:** Anorexia, bulimia, binge eating disorder require professional help.

## Minimum Duration

**Total:** 6-12 months (minimum 6 months)

> "If you rush through them, you have wasted several months. By rushing, you get neither the impressive changes of a hard diet nor the psychological recovery you need."

## Phase 1: Unattached Eating

**Goal:** Eat freely with zero concern over food choices

**Purpose:** Ease psychological burden and reduce negative relationships with food

**Duration:** 2-4 months

**Completion marker:** Can honestly look forward to cleaning up diet again

### Protocol
- Eat whatever you want, whenever you want
- No counting anything (calories, macros, meals)
- No guilt, deals with yourself, or plans
- No weighing yourself
- Continue training normally
- Don't intentionally stuff yourself, but deny nothing

### What to Expect
- Initial difficulty enjoying food (psychological state)
- Eventually, genuine enjoyment (progress marker)
- Weight gain (temporary and reversible)
- Gradual satiation of cravings

> "Your choice is about what kind of woman you want to be. You can be the kind of woman that always criticizes her own body and hates the way she looks, but doesn't have the follow-through to commit to a diet and stick to making the changes she wants to see."

### Exit Criteria
- Junk food has lost its appeal
- Craving healthier, lighter foods
- Thinking "I want a crisp apple" instead of cookies
- Ready for Phase 2

## Phase 2: Nutritious Choices

**Goal:** Focus diet on healthy foods without counting

**Purpose:** Re-establish healthy habits without tracking pressure

**Duration:** 2-3 months

**Completion marker:** No fears or doubts about gently controlling food intake

### Protocol
Focus on choosing (when convenient):
- Minimally processed, fresh foods
- High-protein sources (lean meats, dairy)
- High-fiber, high-vitamin foods (fruits, vegetables)
- Healthy fats (nuts, nut butters, avocado, olive oil)

### Examples
- Burrito with steak, brown rice, veggies instead of burger
- Fresh fruit instead of potato chips
- Carrots + almond butter instead of chicken fingers + ranch

### What to Expect
- Natural transition from Phase 1
- Improved energy and mood
- Stress-free relationship with "healthy" vs "unhealthy"

### Exit Criteria
- Nutritious choices feel automatic
- No intense fears about food control
- Naturally gravitating toward healthier options

## Phase 3: Rough Macros

**Goal:** Establish meal structure without counting

**Purpose:** Build habits of balanced meals

**Duration:** 1-3 months

**Completion marker:** Balanced meals happen by habit without stress

### Protocol
For most meals include:
- **Protein:** Palm-sized portion of lean meat OR coffee cup of protein drink/skim milk
- **Vegetables:** Large handful of mostly green veggies
- **Carbs (if training soon or recently):** Small handful whole grains OR 1-2 pieces fruit
- **Fats:** Based on hunger—finish protein/veggies/carbs first, then healthy fats to fullness

### Timing Guidelines
- Training within 3 hours: Full carb portion
- Trained within 6 hours: Full carb portion
- Neither: Half portion or skip carbs

### Exit Criteria
- Balanced meals are second nature
- No stress in meal assembly
- Ready for counting without anxiety

## Phase 4: Counting & Measuring

**Goal:** Resume tracking macros without emotional attachment

**Purpose:** Normalize counting as neutral activity

**Duration:** 1-3 months

**Completion marker:** Counting is painless habit, not source of stress

### Protocol
- Count protein, carbs, and fat
- Spread across 4-6 balanced meals
- No goal numbers (just observe)
- Eyeballing is fine
- Use apps if helpful

**If you need guidance:**
- Step on scale once (record, step off)
- Calculate maintenance macros
- Target those numbers loosely (±30g each macro is fine)

### What to Expect
- Counting becomes mechanical, not emotional
- Food becomes fuel, not enemy or reward
- Ready for goal-directed dieting

### Exit Criteria
- Counting and measuring feel simple
- Nutritious choices happen automatically
- Can envision starting a real diet without dread

## Post-Reset: Moving Forward

**After completing all phases:**
1. Set a specific goal (fat loss, muscle gain)
2. Design diet using earlier chapters
3. Execute with commitment but without anxiety
4. Use 2-3 month diet-to-maintenance structure
5. Prevent future burnout through proper phases

## No Weigh-Ins During Reset

**Critical rule:** No scale use during entire reset

> "When you're psychologically well, losing gained weight is super easy and straightforward. If you care about your weight during the reset, not only will you just be disappointed, you won't accomplish the goal of the reset and it will have been largely in vain."
