# Five Principles of Scientific Dieting

**Type:** Framework
**Status:** Solid
**Source:** Renaissance Woman, Chapter 1
**Authors:** Dr. Jen Case, Dr. Melissa Davis, Dr. Mike Israetel

## Summary

The foundational framework for all RP diet recommendations. These five principles, ordered by importance, dictate how diets should be designed and modified to produce optimal results for fat loss, muscle gain, and performance.

## The Five Principles (Hierarchy)

### 1. Calorie Balance (Most Important)
The fundamental driver of weight change.

**For fat loss:**
- Caloric deficit = weight loss
- Target: 0.5-1.0% bodyweight per week
- Each lb fat = ~3500 calories

**For muscle gain:**
- Caloric surplus = weight gain
- Target: 0.3-0.7% bodyweight per week
- Faster gains = mostly fat, not more muscle

**Key insight:** "The most important ingredient of a fat loss diet is the act of eating less than you burn."

### 2. Macronutrient Amounts
The three macros have different effects on body composition:

| Macro | Calories/g | Role | Cut Priority |
|-------|-----------|------|--------------|
| Protein | 4 | Muscle building blocks | Cut LAST |
| Carbs | 4 | Performance fuel | Cut after fats |
| Fats | 9 | Hormonal function | Cut FIRST |

**Baseline recommendations (per lb LBM):**
- Protein: 1.0g
- Carbs: 1.5g
- Fats: Fill remaining calories

**Minimum fat thresholds:**
- Long-term (>2 months): 0.3g per lb LBM
- Short-term (<2 months): 0.1g per lb LBM

### 3. Nutrient Timing
When you eat matters, but less than what and how much.

**Guidelines:**
- Eat every 3-5 hours (no longer than 5 hours between meals)
- Post-workout shake after weight training
- Carbs: Around training (before/during/after)
- Fats: Away from training
- Protein: Even distribution across meals
- Casein/milk protein before bed (sustained amino acid release)

### 4. Food Composition
The quality of food sources affects outcomes.

**Prioritize:**
- Whole, minimally processed foods
- Lean animal proteins
- Whole grains
- Fresh fruits and vegetables
- Healthy monounsaturated fats (olive oil, nuts, avocado)

**Limit:**
- Processed foods
- High-sugar foods
- Saturated fats (moderate, not eliminate)

### 5. Supplements (Least Important)
Supplements cannot replace the fundamentals.

**Only useful if principles 1-4 are in place:**
- Caffeine (ramp up slowly during diet)
- Creatine (for muscle gain phases)
- Whey protein (convenience)
- Casein protein (pre-sleep)

**Key insight:** "If you're going to take supplements, do so consistently and intelligently. Slowly ramp up caffeine intake as the diet progresses... don't just start with the kitchen sink!"

## Application Framework

```
Priority Order for Diet Design:
1. Set calorie target based on goal (deficit/surplus)
2. Calculate macro splits based on LBM
3. Plan meal timing around training
4. Select food sources (whole foods base)
5. Add supplements if needed/desired
```

## Modification Framework

When progress stalls, adjust in this order:
1. First: Verify calorie tracking accuracy
2. Then: Adjust calories (250-500 at a time)
3. Then: Adjust macros (fats first, then carbs)
4. Consider: Meal timing refinements
5. Rarely: Supplement additions
