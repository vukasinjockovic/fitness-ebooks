# Water Cut Protocol for Competition

**Type:** Framework
**Status:** Solid
**Source:** Renaissance Woman, Chapter 4
**Authors:** Dr. Jen Case, Dr. Melissa Davis, Dr. Mike Israetel

## Summary

A comprehensive guide to cutting water weight for competition weigh-ins, with protocols scaled to available rehydration time. Critical safety considerations included.

## WARNING

> "Cutting water weight can be dangerous if done incorrectly, or when there are pre-existing conditions involved. Please consult your physician before attempting any of these dehydration techniques."

## Weigh-In Types & Limits

| Weigh-In | Max Cut | Setup Time | Rehydration Time |
|----------|---------|------------|------------------|
| 24 hour | 5% BW | ~1 week | Full recovery possible |
| 12 hour | 3% BW | 3-4 days | Near-full recovery |
| 2 hour | 2% BW | 1 day | Partial recovery |
| Matside | ~1% | Night before | No recovery |

**Safety limits:**
- Never exceed 5% bodyweight water loss
- Over 7% is dangerous
- Don't stay dehydrated longer than necessary

## 24-Hour Weigh-In Protocol

### Depletion Phase (1 Week Out)

#### 1. Carbohydrate Restriction (7 days out → weigh-in)
- Reduce carbs to near-zero
- Maintain total calories via increased fats
- Purpose: Deplete glycogen (each gram stores 3g water)
- Expected: ~2% BW loss from glycogen + water

**Why it works:**
- 150 lb female stores ~500g glycogen
- 500g glycogen + 1500g water = 2kg (4.4 lbs)
- 75% depletion = ~3.3 lbs

#### 2. Water Loading (7 days → 24 hours out)
- 1 gallon per 100 lbs bodyweight per day
- Example: 130 lb woman = 1.3 gallons/day
- Spread evenly throughout day (don't chug)
- Success marker: Frequent urination of clear urine

**Purpose:** Establish diuretic hormone state for easy water loss later

**WARNING - Hyponatremia:**
- Don't exceed 1 gal per 100 lbs significantly
- Watch for: nausea, headache, confusion, muscle spasms
- Spread intake across the day

#### 3. Salt Restriction (48 hours out → weigh-in)
- Begin low-salt eating 2 days before weigh-in
- NOT earlier (triggers antidiuretic response)
- Continue normal salt until this point
- Powerade Zero can help with electrolytes during water loading

#### 4. Water Restriction (24 hours out → weigh-in)
- Minimal water intake (only to swallow food)
- Continued urination from built-up diuretic drive
- Expected: Additional ~1% BW loss

#### 5. Food Volume Restriction (24 hours out → weigh-in)
- Low volume, low fiber foods
- Best option: Casein pudding (2 scoops + 2-4 oz water)
- Purpose: Minimize GI tract contents

#### 6. Hyperthermia (12-4 hours out → weigh-in)
- Final ~1% loss through sweating
- Use in 20-minute bouts with 10-minute cool-downs

**Methods:**
- Warm clothing throughout final 12 hours
- Hot shower
- Sauna
- Hot bath (WITH supervision)
- Car heater with warm clothes

**Safety rules:**
- Never exceed 20 minutes of extreme heat
- Always have supervision
- Stop if: vision blurs, coordination drops, muscle weakness

### Timeline Summary (24-Hour)

| Strategy | Start | End |
|----------|-------|-----|
| Carb Depletion | 7 days out | At weigh-in |
| Water Loading | 7 days out | 24 hours out |
| Salt Depletion | 48 hours out | At weigh-in |
| Water Restriction | 24 hours out | At weigh-in |
| Food Volume Reduction | 24 hours out | At weigh-in |
| Hyperthermia | 12-4 hours out | At weigh-in |

### Repletion Phase (Post Weigh-In)

#### Water Repletion
- Minimum: 1 gallon per 100 lbs BW in first 12 hours
- Success marker: Clear urine by morning of competition
- Mix water with sports drinks, juices
- Sip consistently (don't chug)

#### Carbohydrate Repletion
- Target: 5g per lb bodyweight in 12 hours
- 150 lb woman = 750g carbs
- Rate limit: 1g per 2 hours per lb BW
- High glycemic, low fiber, moderate-to-high GI foods

**Best carb sources:**
- Low-fat cookies/cakes
- Kids cereal
- White bread/rice
- Baked chips
- Skim chocolate milk
- Non-diet soda
- Fruit juices
- Sushi, burritos, sub sandwiches

#### Protein & Fat
- Protein: ~0.6g per lb BW (don't overeat - too filling)
- Fat: ~0.85g per lb BW
- Keep fiber low

#### Calories
- ~30 calories per lb bodyweight in first 12 hours
- 100 lb woman = 3000 cal
- 180 lb woman = 5400 cal

### Sample Repletion Schedule (24-Hour)

```
Right after weigh-in:
- 48oz water + 90g Gatorade powder + 20g whey

30 min later:
- Subway sandwich + Baked Lays + water

2 hours later:
- Burrito + 2 glasses Sprite

Between meals:
- Low-fat cookies + chocolate skim milk

3 hours later:
- Panera sandwich + low-fat chips + water

Between meals:
- Devil's food cake + chocolate milk

3 hours later:
- Sushi (lots) + water

3 hours later:
- Chinese food until done for the night

Morning of competition:
- Similar meal to previous day + plenty of fluids
```

## 12-Hour Weigh-In Protocol

**Max cut:** 3% BW

**Modification:** Remove EITHER water loading OR hyperthermia
- Hyperthermia removal preferred (more fatiguing)

**Repletion adjustments (multiply 24-hour values by 3/5):**
- Water: 0.6 gal per 100 lbs
- Carbs: 3g per lb BW
- Protein: 0.35g per lb BW
- Fat: 0.5g per lb BW
- Calories: 20 per lb BW

**Timing:** Most consumed in first 4-6 hours
**Pro tip:** Set aside midnight snacks (cookies + milk + water)

## 2-Hour Weigh-In Protocol

**Max cut:** 2% BW

**Day before:**
- Low food volume
- Near-zero carbs
- No salt
- No water in final half of day

**Morning of:**
- No water until weigh-in
- Minimal low-volume food

**Post weigh-in (2 hours):**
- Water: 0.25 gal per 100 lbs (closer to 40 oz)
- Carbs: 1-1.4g per lb BW
- Protein: 0.2g per lb BW
- Fat: 0.25g per lb BW
- Eat immediately after weigh-in

**Best meals:** Subs, sandwiches, sushi, rice, pasta + sports drinks

## Matside Weigh-In

**Restrictions only (no aggressive cutting):**
- Avoid salty foods day before and morning of
- 1% hydration impairment doesn't affect strength/power (but does affect endurance)

**For strength sports (if 1% over):**
- Low food volume morning-of
- Minimal water to avoid thirst

**Best approach:** Use fat loss diet in preceding weeks to hit weight naturally
