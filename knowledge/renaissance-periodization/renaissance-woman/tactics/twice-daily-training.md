# Twice-Daily Training Nutrition

**Type:** Tactic
**Status:** Solid
**Source:** Renaissance Woman, Chapter 4
**Authors:** Dr. Jen Case, Dr. Melissa Davis, Dr. Mike Israetel

## Summary

Specialized nutrition protocols for athletes training hard twice per day. Focuses on maximizing recovery from Session 1 and ensuring adequate energy for Session 2 through strategic carbohydrate and hydration timing.

## The Two Primary Challenges

### 1. Recovery for Session 2
- Session 1 depletes glycogen
- Must replete enough to perform in Session 2
- Limited time window between sessions

### 2. Preserving Adaptations from Session 1
- Training initiates adaptive processes
- These processes take time to unfold
- Session 2 can interfere with Session 1's adaptations

## Session Scheduling

### Optimal Approach
- 6-8 hours between sessions
- First session: After 1-2 morning meals
- Second session: Early evening
- Leave 1-2 meals after second session

### Priority Order (if scheduling is limited)
1. **Most important:** Maximum time between AM and PM sessions
2. **Second:** At least one meal before first workout
3. **Third:** At least one meal (preferably two) after second workout

## Session Difficulty Sequencing

### Best: Intensity Before Volume
- Heavy/low-rep work in first session
- High-rep/volume work in second session

**Why:**
- Fatigue hurts technique on heavy work more
- Can still grind through volume when tired
- Volume fatigues more than intensity

### Acceptable: Harder Session Later
- Easier session first, harder session second
- Not ideal but workable

### Worst: Two Equally Hard Sessions
- Avoid putting two highest-intensity sessions same day
- Both performance and adaptation suffer

## The Glycogen Problem

**Key insight:** First session uses substantial glycogen stores. Without rapid repletion, Session 2 will suffer.

### Glycogen Repletion Strategy

**During and immediately post-Session 1:**
- Intra-workout shake: Simple carbs + protein
- Post-workout shake: High glycemic carbs + protein

**Between sessions:**
- High carb intake
- High glycemic index foods
- Minimize fat and fiber (slow absorption)
- Moderate protein (usual per-meal amount)

## Specific Nutrition Targets

### Carbs Between Sessions (6-hour gap)
- **Minimum:** 1.5g per lb lean body mass
- **Example:** 130 lb LBM = 195g carbs in 6 hours
- Most consumed by 2 hours before Session 2

### Carb Timing
- High GI carbs immediately post-Session 1
- Continue high GI for first meal after
- Can moderate GI in later meals before Session 2

### Total Daily Carbs (2x hard training)
- **Baseline:** 2.5g per lb lean body mass
- May need more in some cases
- Start at 2.5g, adjust based on performance

### Post-Session 1 Meal Guidelines
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| High glycemic carbs | High fat |
| Moderate protein | High fiber |
| Low fat/fiber | Slow-digesting foods |

**Example high-GI sources:**
- Low-fat baked goods
- Kids cereals
- White bread/rice
- Sports drinks

## Hydration Protocol

### Critical Fact
Each gram of stored glycogen requires ~3g of water

**Implication:**
- Normal water intake = potential dehydration
- Must drink MORE than usual during repletion
- Add electrolytes to water

**Success marker:** Clear urine within 1 hour of Session 2

## Sample Day (2x Training)

```
7am - Meal 1 (Pre-Session 1):
  Egg whites, 2 pieces fruit, 2 tbsp nut butter

10am - Meal 2:
  Lean turkey, brown rice, broccoli, 1 tbsp olive oil

12pm - Intra/Post Shake (Session 1):
  25g whey protein, 75g Gatorade powder

2pm - Meal 4 (Glycogen priority):
  Whey protein, skim milk, 2 bowls Lucky Charms

4pm - Meal 5:
  Lean turkey, brown rice, broccoli, 1 tbsp olive oil

7pm - Post-Session 2 Shake:
  25g whey protein, 50g Gatorade

8pm - Meal 7:
  Grilled salmon, whole grain pasta, 2 tbsp nut butter

Bedtime - Meal 8:
  Casein pudding (1 scoop + 2 tbsp nut butter)
```

## Key Principles Summary

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Time between sessions | Maximize (6-8 hours ideal) |
| Session order | Intensity before volume |
| Post-S1 carbs | High glycemic, fast-digesting |
| Between-session carbs | 1.5g/lb LBM minimum |
| Daily carbs | 2.5g/lb LBM for 2x hard training |
| Hydration | More than usual + electrolytes |
| Fat/fiber | Minimize between sessions |

## Why This Works

**Glycogen science:**
> "High GI carbs digest quickly and appear quickly in the blood, raising insulin levels rapidly and leading to faster glycogen loading into the muscles and liver than lower GI carbs."

**Research support:**
> "In the research literature, high GI carbs have shown a more complete repletion of glycogen between repeat training sessions than low GI carbs, even when matched for carb amount."
