# Eric Helms

**Type:** researcher
**Status:** authoritative
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Dr. Eric Helms, Eric R. Helms

## Summary

Eric Helms, PhD, is a researcher, coach, and author known for evidence-based approaches to natural bodybuilding and strength training. His "Muscle and Strength Pyramids" books are cited by Israetel et al. as recommended prerequisite reading for understanding the Scientific Principles of Hypertrophy Training.

## Background

**Education:**

- MSc in Exercise Science

**Positions:**
- Researcher, Auckland University of Technology (New Zealand)
- Coach and consultant for natural bodybuilders

**Accomplishments:**
- Drug-free bodybuilding and powerlifting competitor
- Co-founder, 3D Muscle Journey
- Author of influential training and nutrition guides

## Key Contributions

### The Muscle and Strength Pyramids

Two-book set covering:
- **Training Pyramid**: Training principles organized by importance
- **Nutrition Pyramid**: Diet principles for physique athletes

Referenced as prerequisite reading:
> "The Muscle and Strength Pyramids, by Dr. Eric Helms, Andrea Valdez, and Andy Morgan. This is a two book set... the training book is the one you'll find most valuable in preparing you to read the Scientific Principles of Hypertrophy Training."

### Pyramid Framework

The Muscle and Strength Pyramids organize training variables by priority:
1. Adherence (base)
2. Volume, Intensity, Frequency
3. Progression
4. Exercise Selection
5. Rest Periods (top)

This hierarchical approach influenced evidence-based training discourse.

### Natural Bodybuilding Research

Key research areas:
- Contest preparation for natural bodybuilders
- Protein requirements for muscle retention during dieting
- Training modifications during caloric restriction

## Role in This Book

Helms' work is cited as intermediate-level prerequisite reading that bridges the gap between beginner content (Art and Science of Lifting) and advanced content (Scientific Principles of Hypertrophy Training).

The Pyramids framework complements the Seven Principles framework:
- Both organize training knowledge hierarchically
- Both emphasize evidence-based approaches
- Pyramids is more accessible; Seven Principles is more detailed
