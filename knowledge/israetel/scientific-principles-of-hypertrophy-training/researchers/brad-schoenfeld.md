**Type:** researcher

## Summary

Brad Schoenfeld, PhD, is one of the most prolific and influential researchers in the field of muscle hypertrophy. His textbook "The Science and Development of Muscle Hypertrophy" is cited by Israetel et al. as essential prerequisite reading for understanding the Scientific Principles of Hypertrophy Training. He has authored numerous studies on rep ranges, volume, and training frequency.

## Background

**Research Focus:**
- Mechanisms of muscle hypertrophy

## Key Contributions

### The Science and Development of Muscle Hypertrophy

University-level textbook that:
- Establishes terminology and physiological mechanisms for hypertrophy
- Covers muscle physiology at the cellular level
- Referenced as prerequisite reading in Scientific Principles

> From the Introduction: "This book is a university-level textbook, so it's no breezy read, but it establishes the vast majority of the terms and the anatomical and physiological mechanisms which our book builds upon."

### Volume-Hypertrophy Research

Key findings on training volume:
- Higher volumes generally produce more hypertrophy (dose-response)
- Diminishing returns at very high volumes
- Volume likely more important than load within effective ranges

### Rep Range Research

Studies on loading parameters:
- Growth possible across wide rep ranges (6-30+)
- Volume-equated comparisons show similar hypertrophy
- Load selection affects fatigue, not just stimulus

### Mind-Muscle Connection Research

Research on attentional focus:
- Internal focus (on the muscle) can enhance activation
- Effect more pronounced for isolation exercises
- May contribute to preferential muscle development
