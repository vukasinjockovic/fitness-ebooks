# Mike Israetel

**Type:** researcher
**Status:** authoritative

**Aliases:** Dr. Mike Israetel, Dr. Mike, Mike

## Summary

Mike Israetel, PhD, is the co-founder and Chief Content Officer at Renaissance Periodization (RP). He holds a PhD in Sport Physiology and is a professor in the Masters program in Exercise Science at Lehman College. He is one of the most influential figures in evidence-based bodybuilding and hypertrophy science, known for developing practical frameworks that translate sport science research into actionable training programs.

## Key Contributions

### The Seven Principles Framework
Developed the hierarchical framework for hypertrophy training principles:
1. Specificity
2. Overload
3. Fatigue Management
4. SRA (Stimulus Recovery Adaptation)
5. Variation
6. Phase Potentiation
7. Individualization

### Volume Landmarks System
Conceptualized and popularized:
- **MV** (Maintenance Volume)
- **MEV** (Minimum Effective Volume)
- **MAV** (Maximum Adaptive Volume)
- **MRV** (Maximum Recoverable Volume)

### Stimulus to Fatigue Ratio (SFR)
Developed the SFR concept as central to training optimization:
> "The SFR is a measure of how much stimulus you get for the fatigue cost of any training input."

### Volume Progression Algorithms
Created practical algorithms for:
- MEV Stimulus Estimator
- Set Progression Algorithm
- Loading and rep progression rules
