# Volume Landmarks

**Type:** concept
**Status:** authoritative
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** MV, MEV, MAV, MRV, Volume Thresholds, Training Volume Markers

## Summary

Volume landmarks are threshold values that define the boundaries of effective training volume for hypertrophy. They represent the minimum, optimal, and maximum volumes that produce specific training outcomes, allowing for precise volume prescription and autoregulation. These landmarks are individualized and shift with training status and fatigue levels.

## The Four Volume Landmarks

### MV - Maintenance Volume
**Definition:** The amount of training needed to prevent muscle loss (maintain baseline muscle mass).

**Characteristics:**
- Lowest productive volume level
- No muscle growth expected
- Used during deloads and resensitization phases
- Varies by muscle group and training age

**Typical Values:** ~4-6 sets per muscle per week for intermediates

### MEV - Minimum Effective Volume
**Definition:** The amount of training needed to begin making measurable progress (begin stimulating robust muscle growth).

**Characteristics:**
- Stimulus threshold for growth
- Where accumulation phases should begin
- Increases with training age and adaptation
- Affected by fatigue (rises during mesocycle)

**Typical Values:** ~6-10 sets per muscle per week for intermediates

### MAV - Maximum Adaptive Volume
**Definition:** The amount of training which provides greatest adaptive outcomes at any given time.

**Characteristics:**
- Theoretical "sweet spot" for growth
- Shifts during mesocycle (gets higher as you adapt)
- Not a fixed point—more of a zone
- Hard to pinpoint exactly

**Typical Values:** Somewhere between MEV and MRV

### MRV - Maximum Recoverable Volume
**Definition:** The maximum amount of training from which recovery is possible; beyond this, overreaching occurs.

**Characteristics:**
- Upper limit of productive training
- Where accumulation phases should end
- Performance begins to decline if exceeded
- Triggers need for deload

**Typical Values:** ~15-25+ sets per muscle per week for intermediates

## Key Relationships

```
MV < MEV < MAV < MRV

Volume too low:           [MV]--------[MEV]
                         (maintenance) (no growth)

Effective range:                      [MEV]========[MAV]========[MRV]
                                      (growth zone—where most training occurs)

Volume too high:                                              [MRV]--------
                                                             (overreaching/loss)
```

## Practical Application

### Finding MEV (RP MEV Stimulus Estimator Algorithm)
Rate the following after a session (0-3 scale each):
1. How much did the session challenge your target muscles?
2. How much pump did you experience?
3. How much did the session disrupt your target muscles?

**Total Score Interpretation:**
- 0-3: Too easy, likely below MEV → add sets
- 4-6: At or just above MEV → good starting point
- 7-9: Well above MEV → possible to start here or reduce slightly

### Set Progression Algorithm
After each week, rate:
1. Soreness level (0-3)
2. Performance vs. expectations (0-3)

**Decision Matrix:**
- Low soreness + good performance → Add 2+ sets
- Moderate soreness + stable performance → Add 0-1 sets
- High soreness + declining performance → Consider recovery strategies

## How Landmarks Shift

| Factor | Effect on MEV | Effect on MRV |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Training age increases | ↑ Higher | ↑ Higher (but MEV rises faster) |
| Within mesocycle | ↑ Higher | ↑ Higher (converge over time) |
| After deload | ↓ Lower | ↓ Lower |
| After resensitization | ↓↓ Much lower | ↓ Lower |
| Calorie deficit | → Same or ↑ | ↓ Lower |
| Calorie surplus | → Same | ↑ Higher |

## Key Quotes

> "Training between MEV and MRV is best for growth."

> "At the end of your accumulation phase, your MEV is fairly close to your MRV. If your MEV never went down, you'd soon be starting mesocycles right under your MRV."

> "Because you spend a week at MV level training during deload, you re-sensitize to higher volumes and your MEV drops, potentiating another productive accumulation."
