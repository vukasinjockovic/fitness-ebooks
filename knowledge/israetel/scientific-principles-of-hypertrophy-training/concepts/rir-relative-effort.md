# RIR (Reps in Reserve) and Relative Effort

**Type:** concept
**Status:** authoritative
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** RIR, Reps in Reserve, Relative Effort, Proximity to Failure, Effective Reps

## Summary

Reps in Reserve (RIR) is a measure of relative effort that indicates how many more repetitions could be performed before reaching muscular failure. It is the primary method for prescribing and autoregulating training intensity in hypertrophy programs. RIR sets the bounds for effective tension and volume stimulus ranges and directly affects the stimulus-to-fatigue ratio.

## RIR Scale

| RIR | Meaning | Relative Intensity |
|-----|---------|-------------------|
| 5 | Could do 5 more reps | Moderate effort |
| 4 | Could do 4 more reps | Moderate-high effort |
| 3 | Could do 3 more reps | High effort |
| 2 | Could do 2 more reps | Very high effort |
| 1 | Could do 1 more rep | Near maximal |
| 0 | Next rep would fail | Failure |

## The Effective Stimulus Range: 5-0 RIR

### Why 5 RIR is the Lower Bound
- Last 5 reps before failure see vast majority of tension from largest, most growth-prone motor units
- This occurs at any load within the stimulus range (30-85% 1RM)
- Reps above 5 RIR still contribute to growth, just less so
- "Effective reps" = reps at 5 RIR or lower

### Stimulus Per RIR Level
Growth increases as RIR decreases, but with diminishing returns:
- 5→4 RIR: Notable stimulus jump
- 4→3 RIR: Good stimulus increase
- 3→2 RIR: Moderate increase
- 2→1 RIR: Smaller increase
- 1→0 RIR: Smallest increase

### Why 0 RIR Isn't Always Best
While 0 RIR provides maximum per-set stimulus, it:
- Generates disproportionately more fatigue
- Limits progressive overload across weeks
- Degrades technique and mind-muscle connection
- Increases injury risk

## Optimal RIR for Hypertrophy

### Session Average: 2-3 RIR
This range provides:
- Best stimulus-to-fatigue ratio
- Adequate motor unit recruitment
- Sustainable training across weeks
- Room for progressive overload

### Mesocycle Progression
Typical RIR progression across accumulation:
- Week 1: 4-5 RIR (start conservative)
- Week 2: 3-4 RIR
- Week 3: 2-3 RIR
- Week 4: 1-2 RIR
- Week 5 (pre-deload): 0-1 RIR

## RIR and Fiber Type Recruitment

### At High RIR (>5)
- Primarily slow-twitch fibers working
- Larger motor units not fully activated
- Stimulus suboptimal for growth

### As RIR Decreases (5→0)
- Larger motor units progressively recruited
- Fast-twitch fibers increasingly engaged
- Maximum recruitment near failure

### Special Case: Heavy Loads (>85% 1RM)
- All reps are already "close to failure"
- Large motor units activated from first rep
- RIR matters less for heavy loads

## RIR and Other Variables

### RIR + Volume
- Counting "hard sets" (5-0 RIR) is a valid proxy for growth stimulus
- 2 sets at 2-3 RIR ≈ 2x growth of 1 set at 0 RIR
- More volume at moderate RIR > less volume at 0 RIR

### RIR + Load
- At 30% 1RM: Only last ~5 reps recruit largest motor units
- At 85% 1RM: Nearly all reps recruit largest motor units
- RIR "normalizes" different load effects on stimulus

### RIR + Recovery
| RIR Level | Recovery Time | Sustainable? |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| 5-4 | Fastest | Very sustainable |
| 3-2 | Moderate | Sustainable |
| 1-0 | Slowest | End of accumulation only |

## Key Quotes

> "Performing most, if not all, working sets in a program at 5 RIR or less is probably best practice."

> "Averaging 2-3 RIR likely allows the best stimulus to fatigue ratio for long-term growth."

> "While one session at 0 RIR will cause more growth than one session at 5 RIR, the 0 RIR session will create vastly more fatigue."

> "This does not mean that you should only ever train at 2-3 RIR, but it probably means that your average RIR across weeks should be set at around 2-3 RIR."

## Practical Tips

1. **Calibration**: Practice RIR assessment—it improves with experience
2. **Conservative Start**: When uncertain, aim higher RIR (less risk)
3. **Compound vs Isolation**: RIR often easier to gauge on isolation
4. **Rep Range Effect**: Easier to judge RIR in 8-15 rep range than 3-5 or 25+
