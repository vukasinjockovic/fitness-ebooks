# The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Training

## Book Information

- **Authors**: Eric Helms, PhD, CSCS; Andrea Valdez, MS; Andy Morgan, BS
- **Edition**: Second Edition (2018)
- **Publisher**: Muscle and Strength Pyramids
- **Foreword by**: Dr. Michael C. Zourdos, PhD, CSCS

## Overview

The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Training is a comprehensive guide that prioritizes training variables in order of importance for strength and hypertrophy. The pyramid structure ensures readers focus on fundamentals before details, avoiding "putting the cart before the horse."

## Core Thesis

> "It doesn't matter how good your programming is if you cannot stick to it."

The book emphasizes prioritization over optimization, teaching readers to differentiate between "big rocks and pebbles" in training and to understand that adherence trumps theoretical perfection.

## The Training Pyramid (6 Levels)

1. **Adherence** (Foundation - Most Important)
   - Realistic, Enjoyable, Flexible (REF)
   - Sustainability over optimality
   - Life stress impacts training capacity

2. **Volume, Intensity, Frequency**
   - 10-20 sets per muscle group/movement per week
   - Strength: 2/3-3/4 volume in 1-6 rep range
   - Hypertrophy: 2/3-3/4 volume in 6-12 rep range
   - 2+ times per muscle group/movement per week

3. **Progression**
   - Novice: session to session
   - Intermediate: week to week
   - Advanced: month to month
   - Deloads, periodization models

4. **Exercise Selection**
   - Strength: 50-75% competition lifts, 25-50% accessories
   - Hypertrophy: 1-2 compounds + 1-3 isolations per muscle group
   - Consider overlap between exercises

5. **Rest Periods**
   - Longer rest (2-5 min) for strength and hypertrophy
   - Short rest doesn't enhance hypertrophy via hormones
   - Antagonist paired sets for time efficiency

6. **Lifting Tempo** (Top - Least Important)
   - Control the eccentric, explode on concentric
   - Time under tension overrated
   - Slow tempos generally inferior

## Overarching Theme: Periodization

Periodization encompasses all levels - the intentional manipulation of variables over time. Placed outside the pyramid because it affects everything.
