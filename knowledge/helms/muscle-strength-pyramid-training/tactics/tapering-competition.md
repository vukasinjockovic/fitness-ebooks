# Tapering for Competition

**Type:** tactic
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Peaking, Competition Prep, Meet Prep

## Summary

Tapering reduces training volume 1-4 weeks before competition to allow fatigue dissipation and peak performance expression. Unlike deloads (which enable continued training progress), tapers are specifically designed to maximize performance on competition day. The approach differs for strength vs physique competitors.

## Taper vs Deload

| Aspect | Deload | Taper |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Purpose | Enable continued progress | Peak for competition |
| Timing | Between training blocks | Before competition |
| Duration | ~1 week | 1-4 weeks |
| Intensity | Maintain | Maintain or slight increase |
| Volume | ~50% reduction | 33-66% reduction |

## General Taper Principles

Based on taper research (Pritchard et al., 2015):

1. **Duration**: 1-4 weeks (proportionate to overload period and fatigue)
2. **Volume reduction**: 33-66%
3. **Intensity**: Maintain or slightly decrease (no more than 10%)
4. **Frequency**: Maintain (don't skip sessions)

### Primer Sessions
> "There is data showing that a light 'primer session' may improve performance for 24-48 hours."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 3

- 1-2 days before competition
- Very low volume, low RPE
- Example: Work up to 4-5 RPE single on competition lifts, nothing else

## Powerlifting Taper Protocol

### 2 Weeks Out
- Train all three lifts together, in competition order
- Low reps (1-3), moderate percentages (75-85% 1RM)
- This maintains intensity but keeps RPE low
- Increased frequency compensates for rep reduction
- Total main lift volume reduced 50-70%
- Accessory work: half as many sets

### 1 Week Out (Opener Practice)
- Saturday before meet (7 days out)
- Work up to proposed openers on all three lifts
- Openers should feel like 7.5-8.5 RPE singles

### Meet Week
- Start like week 2 began
- 1-2 days before: Primer session
  - Work up to 4-5 RPE single on each lift
  - Nothing else
- Competition day replaces final training day

## Attempt Selection Strategy

> "A great training plan and taper can be ruined by poor attempt selection on meet day."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 3

**Conservative Strategy:**
| Attempt | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Opener | Current 3RM |
| Second | Current 2RM |
| Third | Next incremental PR (2.5 kg jump) or conservative increase |

**The Math:**
- 2.5 kg per lift × 3 lifts × 2-3 meets/year = 15-22.5 kg to total annually
- Consistent small PRs > occasional big attempts with misses

**Exception:** If you've made massive gains since last competition (common for novices), be more aggressive.

## Bodybuilding Taper Protocol

Different purpose: Peak appearance, not strength.

### Goal
Challenge glycolytic system to encourage glycogen storage for carb load.

### Final Week Training
- Same exercises, split, number of sets
- All reps in 8-20 range
- RPE 6-8.5 (don't push to failure)
- Avoid movements causing soreness (new movements, heavy eccentrics)
- Finish training split 2-3 days out

### Day Before Competition
- Full body pump routine
- Similar to show-day pump up
- RPE no higher than 6
- ~30 minutes only

## Timeline Summary

### Powerlifters
```
Week -2: All lifts together, 1-3 reps, 75-85%, reduce sets 50-70%
Week -1, Sat: Opener practice (7.5-8.5 RPE singles)
Week 0, early: Same as week -2 start
Week 0, 1-2 days out: Primer (4-5 RPE singles)
Competition: Execute
```

### Bodybuilders
```
Final week: 8-20 reps, RPE 6-8.5, avoid damage
2-3 days out: Finish training split
1-2 days out: Light pump routine
Show day: Pump up, compete
```
