## Summary

Eric Helms, PhD, CSCS, is the lead author of The Muscle and Strength Pyramids (Nutrition and Training). He is a researcher, coach, natural bodybuilder and powerlifter, and educator known for evidence-based approaches to physique and strength sports. His work bridges academic research and practical application.

## Key Contributions

### The Muscle and Strength Pyramids

Two-book comprehensive system emphasizing prioritization:

**Training Pyramid (6 Levels):**
1. Adherence (foundation) - REF: Realistic, Enjoyable, Flexible
2. Volume, Intensity, Frequency - The interdependent triad
3. Progression - Training age determines approach
4. Exercise Selection - Goal-specific choices
5. Rest Periods - Less important than believed
6. Lifting Tempo (top) - Least important

**Nutrition Pyramid (5 Levels):**
1. Energy Balance (foundation)
2. Macronutrients and Fiber
3. Micronutrients and Water
4. Nutrient Timing and Frequency
5. Supplementation (top)

### Core Philosophy

> "It doesn't matter how good your programming is if you cannot stick to it."
> — Training Pyramid, Level 1

> "There's no such thing as a good or bad food, but there is such a thing as a good or bad diet."
> — Nutrition Pyramid

> "Do enough to progress, not as much as possible. Increase when plateaued if you are recovering well."
> — Training Pyramid, Level 2
