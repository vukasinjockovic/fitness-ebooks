# Block Periodization

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Block Model, Accumulation-Intensification-Realization, A-I-R Model

## Summary

Block periodization divides training into distinct phases (blocks) with different goals: accumulation (volume/hypertrophy), intensification (strength), and realization (peak/test). Unlike traditional linear periodization with months-long phases, blocks typically last 4-6 weeks, preventing detraining of previously developed qualities.

## The Three Blocks

### 1. Accumulation Block (~6 weeks)
**Goal:** Build muscle mass and work capacity

| Week | Sets/Muscle | Frequency | RPE |
|------|-------------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | 12 | 2x/week | 5-8 |
| 2 | 14 | 2x/week | 5-8 |
| 3 | 16 | 2x/week | 5-8 |
| 4 | 18 | 3x/week | 5-8 |
| 5 | 20 | 3x/week | 5-8 |
| 6 | 22 | 3x/week | 5-8 |

- **Strength focus**: 4-6 reps for 2/3 volume, 8-12 reps for remainder
- **Hypertrophy focus**: 6-12 reps for 2/3 volume, 12-15 reps for remainder
- Load increases only when easy (not forced)
- Stay submaximal to ensure recovery

### 2. Intensification Block (~4 weeks)
**Goal:** Convert accumulated work capacity into specific strength

| Week | Sets/Muscle | Intensity Trend | RPE |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| 1-2 | 18 | Increasing | 8.5-10 |
| 3 | 15 | Higher | 8.5-10 |
| 4 | 12 | Highest | 8.5-10 |

- **Strength focus**: 2-5 reps for 2/3 volume, 6-10 reps for remainder
- **Hypertrophy focus**: 6-10 reps for 2/3 volume, 3-5 reps for remainder
- Volume decreases while load increases
- Approach near-maxes by block end

### 3. Realization Block (~2 weeks)
**Goal:** Dissipate fatigue, express fitness, test strength

**Week 1 (Taper):**
- 10 sets per muscle group
- Maintain rep ranges from intensification
- RPE 7-9 (stay short of failure)
- Maintain frequency

**Week 2 (Test):**
- Hypertrophy: AMRAPs on main lifts, halve accessory sets
- Strength: Work up to singles at 5-7 RPE, then 1RM test
- Or mock meet for powerlifters

## Key Quotes

> "Block periodization uses mesocycles for volume and intensity that last typically no longer than a month and a half."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 3

> "A volume block doesn't necessarily need to be devoid of high-intensity work, it just needs to be higher in volume and lower in intensity relative to the intensity block that follows."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 3

## Comparison: Block vs Linear Periodization

| Aspect | Linear | Block |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Phase length | Months | 4-6 weeks |
| Qualities trained | Sequential | Concentrated |
| Detraining risk | Higher | Lower |
| Flexibility | Lower | Higher |
| Peaks per year | 1-2 | 3-4+ |

## Integration with Other Models

Block periodization integrates well with:
- **Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP)**: Within blocks, daily variation
- **Linear elements**: Overall trend from volume to intensity
- **Autoregulation**: RPE-based adjustments within framework

Example integration:
```
Accumulation (6 wk) → Deload → Intensification (4 wk) → Taper → Test
     ↑                                                        ↓
     └────────────── Repeat cycle ──────────────────────────────┘
```

## Practical Example (Helms' Table)

| Block | Length | Goal | Volume Trend | RPE Range | Rep Ranges |
|-------|--------|------|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Accumulation | 6 weeks | Work capacity/Hypertrophy | 12→22 sets | 5-8 | 4-15 |
| Intensification | 4 weeks | Specific strength | 18→12 sets | 8.5-10 | 2-10 |
| Taper | 1 week | Fatigue dissipation | 10 sets | 7-9 | Maintain |
| Test | 1 week | Performance expression | Minimal | Variable | 1RM or AMRAP |
