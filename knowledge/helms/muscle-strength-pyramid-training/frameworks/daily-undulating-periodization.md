# Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP)

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** DUP, Nonlinear Periodization, Modified DUP, HPS Model

## Summary

Daily Undulating Periodization varies training variables (rep ranges, intensity, volume) across training sessions within a week, rather than across weeks or months. The "modified DUP" model (HPS: Hypertrophy-Power-Strength) was shown superior to traditional HSP ordering in research by Dr. Michael Zourdos, allowing better recovery before the critical strength session.

## Traditional DUP (HSP)

**Day 1: Hypertrophy**
- Moderate intensity, high volume
- 6-12 reps at RPE 5-8

**Day 2: Strength**
- High intensity, moderate volume
- 1-6 reps at RPE 8-10

**Day 3: Power**
- Moderately-high intensity, low volume
- 1-3 reps at RPE 5-7

### Problem with Traditional Order
> "There will be a lot of muscle damage and fatigue from Day 1's high volume hypertrophy session, which could compromise the most important session for the powerlifter — the strength session which occurred on Day 2."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 3

## Modified DUP (HPS) - Recommended

**Day 1: Hypertrophy**
- High volume, moderate intensity
- 6-12 reps at RPE 5-8
- Most weekly sets here (e.g., 5 of 12 bench sets)

**Day 2: Power**
- Low volume, semi-heavy technique work
- Singles at 75-90% 1RM (RPE 5-7)
- 3-5 sets per main lift
- Allows recovery before strength day

**Day 3: Strength**
- Moderate volume, high intensity
- 1-6 reps at RPE 7-9
- AMRAPs or heavy working sets

## Research Support

Dr. Michael Zourdos' PhD dissertation:
- Compared HSP vs HPS in powerlifters
- HPS group gained more strength
- Likely due to greater volume completion on strength day AMRAPs
- Better recovery from hypertrophy → power → strength sequence

## Practical Implementation (4-Day Example)

| Day | Squat | Bench | Deadlift | Accessories |
|-----|-------|-------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Hypertrophy | Hypertrophy | Off | High |
| 2 | Power | Power | Power | Low |
| 3 | Strength | Strength | Off | Moderate |
| 4 | Off | Off | Strength | Moderate |

### Why Deadlift Structure Differs
- No hypertrophy day for deadlift
- Eccentric typically rushed in deadlift (not ideal for hypertrophy)
- Squat and accessories cover hip hinge musculature
- Deadlift strength split from squat/bench strength to manage fatigue

## Key Quotes

> "In his study, on the strength day he had the participants perform AMRAP's with 85% of 1RM. Interestingly, he found with the HPS modified protocol, the lifters gained more strength and this was likely due to their ability to perform more volume on their strength day AMRAPs, presumably due to greater recovery."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 3

> "Even within the same model of periodization, there are variations which can substantially change the outcome."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 3

## Integration with Block Periodization

DUP works within blocks:
- **Volume block**: More sets on hypertrophy day, fewer on strength
- **Intensity block**: More sets on strength day, maintain power day
- **Realization**: Strength day becomes test day

Example weekly set distribution in volume block:
- Bench: 5 sets (Day 1 hypertrophy) + 3 sets (Day 2 power) + 4 sets (Day 3 strength) = 12 sets

## DUP vs WUP vs Linear

| Model | Variation Frequency | Best For |
|-------|---------------------|----------|
| DUP | Daily | Trained lifters, frequent exposure |
| WUP | Weekly | Moderate experience |
| Linear | Monthly | Novices or specific peaking |

## Rep Range Guidelines by Day

| Day Type | Rep Range | % 1RM | RPE | Volume |
|----------|-----------|-------|-----|--------|
| Hypertrophy | 6-12 | 65-80% | 5-8 | High |
| Power | 1-3 | 75-90% | 5-7 | Low |
| Strength | 1-6 | 80-95% | 7-9 | Moderate |
