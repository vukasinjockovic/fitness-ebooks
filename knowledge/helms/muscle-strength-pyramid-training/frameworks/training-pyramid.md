# The Training Pyramid

**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-03
**Aliases:** Muscle and Strength Training Pyramid, 6-Level Training Hierarchy

## Summary

The Training Pyramid is a hierarchical framework that organizes training variables by importance. The six levels prioritize adherence as the foundation, followed by volume/intensity/frequency, progression, exercise selection, rest periods, and tempo. Approximately 80% of results come from the bottom three levels.

## The Six Levels

### Level 1: Adherence (Foundation)
The most important level. Training must be:
- **Realistic**: Sustainable schedule and time frame
- **Enjoyable**: Passion drives harder training
- **Flexible**: Accommodate unexpected life events

> "It doesn't matter how good your programming is if you cannot stick to it."
> — Helms, Training Pyramid, Level 1

### Level 2: Volume, Intensity, Frequency
The actual foundation of programs. These three variables are interdependent:
- **Volume**: 10-20 sets per muscle group/movement per week
- **Intensity**: Goal-specific rep ranges at 5-10 RPE
- **Frequency**: 2+ times per muscle group per week

### Level 3: Progression
How training changes over time to ensure continued adaptation:
- Novices progress session to session
- Intermediates progress week to week
- Advanced lifters progress month to month
- Includes deloads, periodization models

### Level 4: Exercise Selection
Varies by goal:
- Strength: 50-75% competition lifts
- Hypertrophy: Compound + isolation coverage of all muscle groups

### Level 5: Rest Periods
Less important than commonly believed:
- Longer rest (2-5 min) generally better for both goals
- Short rest doesn't enhance hypertrophy via hormones
- Time efficiency tools: antagonist paired sets

### Level 6: Lifting Tempo (Top)
Least important level:
- Control eccentric, explode concentrically
- Time under tension overrated
- Deliberately slow tempos generally inferior

## Key Insight: Periodization Wraps Everything

Periodization is placed outside the pyramid because it encompasses manipulation of all levels over time. It's the overarching theme connecting everything.

## Practical Application

1. Start at Level 1: Can you realistically adhere to this program?
2. Set up Level 2: Determine appropriate VIF for your training age
3. Plan Level 3: Choose periodization model appropriate to experience
4. Select Level 4: Choose exercises based on goals
5. Levels 5-6: Don't overthink—use reasonable rest and natural tempo
