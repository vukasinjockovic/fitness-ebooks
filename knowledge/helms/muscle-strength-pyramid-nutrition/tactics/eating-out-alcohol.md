# Eating Out and Alcohol

## Source
- **Book**: The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Nutrition
- **Author**: Eric Helms, PhD
- **Context**: Behavior and Lifestyle chapter - practical implementation

## Overview

Practical guidance for integrating nutrition goals with real life, including social eating and moderate alcohol consumption while tracking.

## Eating Out

### The Challenge

> "Chefs and cooks are not going to weigh your food out for you. They don't care about your macros, they care only about giving you a tasty meal."

**Problems:**
- No control over portions
- Hidden oils, butter, sauces
- Menu nutrition info often inaccurate
- Easy to underestimate calories

### Frequency Guidelines

| Situation | Recommended Frequency |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Contest prep | Max 1x/month (reserve for important events) |
| Cutting (non-competitive) | 1-2x/week |
| Gaining/Maintenance | As desired (with awareness) |

### Contest Prep Approach

**When to eat out during prep:**
> "The times I suggest that you do eat a meal out during a competition diet is when you have something that is very important in your life."

**Eric's Example (8-month prep):**
- Anniversary dinner
- Mother's Day
- Mother's birthday
- Total: 3 times

### Smart Restaurant Choices

**Low Risk Options:**
- Lean protein (grilled steak, chicken, fish)
- Steamed vegetables
- Plain preparations (no butter, no sauce)

**High Risk Options:**
- Pasta (easy to add 250+ hidden calories in oil)
- Fried foods
- Creamy sauces
- Complex dishes with many ingredients

**Example Order:**
> "I got a very simple meal. I had double steamed broccoli and a lean sirloin steak with no butter."

**Why this works:**
- Limited ingredients = limited error
- Protein easy to estimate
- Vegetables low-calorie regardless of portion
- Margin of error: "maybe a couple grams of carbohydrate and maybe 6 or 7 grams of protein and fat each"

### Estimation Strategy

**Conservative Estimation:**
- Always overestimate calories
- Assume more oil/butter than stated
- Round up on portions

**Use the 3-Tier System:**
When eating out, automatically drop tiers:
- Normal: "Best" (full macros)
- Eating out: "Better" (protein + calories) or "Good" (calories only)

See [[three-tiered-tracking]]

### Beyond Contest Prep

> "If you're not in contest prep, if you're doing a cut for a weight class or other purpose, eating out 1 to 2 times per week is fine, so long as you make conservative estimations."

**Key principles:**
- Choose low-calorie options
- Err on the side of overestimation
- Participate in social life

## Alcohol

### Core Principle

> "The key with alcohol is moderation."

**Definition of Moderation:**
> "Drinking to the point where you don't feel it the next day. Or if you did feel it slightly in the morning, it certainly wouldn't hamper your performance."

### Caloric Content

| Substance | Calories/gram |
|-----------|---------------|
| Alcohol | 7 |
| Carbohydrate | 4 |
| Protein | 4 |
| Fat | 9 |

**Note:** Most alcoholic drinks also contain carbohydrates (beer, wine, mixers).

### Tracking Alcohol

**Problem:** Alcohol isn't a tracked macro.

**Solution:** Automatically shift to lower tracking tier.

**Process:**
1. Calculate alcohol calories
2. Subtract from daily budget
3. Focus on hitting protein + remaining calories
4. Don't track carbs/fat precisely

**Example:**
- Daily target: 2630 kcal (200P, 300C, 70F)
- Two beers: ~400 calories
- Remaining budget: 2230 kcal
- Focus: Hit protein (200g), stay within ±100 of 2230 total
- Result: "Better" tier achieved

### Frequency Guidelines

| Situation | Recommended Frequency |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Contest prep | Minimize (probably too frequent at 1-2x/week) |
| General cutting | 1-2x/week max |
| Maintenance/Gaining | Moderate as desired |

### Amount Limit

> "I would recommend consuming no more than 15% of your total daily allotted calories from alcohol."

**For most people:** 1-3 drinks depending on calorie budget

### Why Moderation Matters

**Performance Impact:**
- Impairs recovery
- Affects sleep quality
- Can impact training next day if excessive

**Body Composition:**
- Extra calories
- May increase hunger/lower inhibitions
- Can lead to poor food choices

## Integrating with Lifestyle

### The Philosophy

> "What good is a nutrition plan if you cannot follow it? And unless you're in a contest prep fat-loss phase, what good is a nutrition plan if it is not maintainable in the long run?"

### Social Isolation Warning

Rigid dieting can lead to:
- Missing important events
- Relationship strain
- All-or-nothing thinking
- Eventual adherence failure

### The Solution

> "This system is used when tracking is needed... If your goal requires a certain level, the levels above it are also options."

**Example:** Contest prep bodybuilder
- Most days: "Best" (full macros)
- Wedding: "Better" (protein + calories)
- Unexpected event: "Good" (calories only)

None of these are "failure."

## Practical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Dinner Out During Cut

1. Choose lean protein + vegetables
2. Ask for preparation without butter/oil
3. Estimate conservatively
4. Focus on hitting protein for the day
5. Stay within calorie target (±100)
6. Resume normal tracking next day

### Scenario 2: Drinks with Friends

1. Count alcohol calories
2. Subtract from daily budget
3. Prioritize protein in remaining meals
4. Stay within adjusted calorie target
5. Don't track macros precisely (use tier system)

### Scenario 3: Weekend Trip (Contest Prep)

1. Plan ahead - bring food if possible
2. Research restaurant menus
3. Make simple, estimable choices
4. Accept temporary "Better" or "Good" tier
5. Don't stress - one weekend won't ruin prep

## Key Mindset Shifts

### From Binary to Flexible

**Old thinking:** "I can't track perfectly, so I'll just binge."

**New thinking:** "I'll drop to a lower tier and stay on track."

### From Isolation to Integration

**Old thinking:** "I can't eat out during my diet."

**New thinking:** "I can eat out with smart choices and estimation."

### From Perfection to Progress

**Old thinking:** "I drank alcohol, diet ruined."

**New thinking:** "I tracked the alcohol, hit protein, stayed within calories."
