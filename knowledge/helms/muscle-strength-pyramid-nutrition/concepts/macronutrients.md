# Macronutrients

## Source
- **Book**: The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Nutrition
- **Author**: Eric Helms, PhD
- **Context**: Level 2 of the Nutrition Pyramid

## Overview

Macronutrients are the calorie-providing nutrients: protein, carbohydrate, and fat. Once energy balance is established (Level 1), macronutrient distribution determines body composition outcomes.

## Caloric Values

| Macronutrient | Calories per gram |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Protein | ~4 |
| Carbohydrate | ~4 |
| Fat | ~9 |
| Alcohol* | ~7 |

*Alcohol is not tracked as a macro; see [[eating-out-alcohol]]

## Calculation Methods

### Protein: Per Body Weight

**Rationale:**
- Primary function is structural (building/repairing tissue)
- Only ~10% used for energy
- Needs scale with body mass, not calorie budget

### Carbs & Fat: Percentage of Calories

**Rationale:**
- Primary function is energy provision
- Should scale with total energy budget
- Flexible ratio based on preference

## Protein Recommendations

### During Fat Loss

**Range:** 0.7-1.2 g/lb (1.6-2.7 g/kg)

**Why Higher When Dieting:**
1. Activity typically increases (more cardio)
2. Less dietary fat/carbs → more protein used for fuel
3. Depleted glycogen/fat stores → more reliance on protein
4. Leaner individuals lose more muscle when dieting

**Research Support:**
- 0.72 g/lb vs 0.36 g/lb (1 week): Higher protein preserved more muscle
- 1.05 g/lb vs 0.45 g/lb (2 weeks): Higher protein preserved more muscle
- 1.09 g/lb vs 0.55 g/lb (3 weeks): Higher protein preserved more muscle

### During Gaining

**Range:** 0.7-1.0 g/lb (1.6-2.2 g/kg)

**Why Potentially Lower:**
- Adequate carbs/fat available for energy
- Less protein diverted to fuel
- Anabolic environment more favorable

### High Body Fat Considerations

If body fat is very high, calculate protein based on:
- Lean body mass estimate, OR
- Goal body weight, OR
- Use lower end of range

> "How to set protein if your body fat is very high" - addressed in Second Edition additions

## Carbohydrate Recommendations

### Functions

- Primary fuel for resistance training
- Glycogen storage in muscle
- Performance maintenance
- Hormone support (thyroid, testosterone)

### Setting Intake

After protein is set, remaining calories split between carbs and fat.

**General Guidance:**
- Athletes typically benefit from higher carbs
- Supports training performance
- Aids glycogen replenishment

**Minimum Intake:** 0.5 g/lb (~1 g/kg)

> "Be careful to not go below the daily minimums of... 0.5 g/lb (~1 g/kg) for dietary carbohydrate."

### Ketogenic Diets

**When They May Work:**
- Strong personal preference
- Improved adherence
- Specific medical conditions

**Considerations:**
- Not inherently superior for fat loss
- May impair high-intensity performance
- Requires adaptation period
- Second Edition expanded discussion

> "But How Do I Know if a Higher-fat, Lower-carb or Ketogenic Diet Is Right for Me?"

## Fat Recommendations

### Functions

- Hormone production (testosterone, estrogen)
- Fat-soluble vitamin absorption
- Cell membrane integrity
- Satiety
- Intramuscular triglyceride replenishment

### Setting Intake

**Minimum Intake:** 0.25 g/lb (~0.5 g/kg)

> "Be careful to not go below the daily minimums of 0.25 g/lb (~0.5 g/kg) for dietary fat."

**General Guidance:**
- Essential for hormonal health
- Don't go too low even when cutting
- Higher fat can aid satiety for some

### Fat Types

- Prioritize unsaturated fats
- Omega-3s particularly important (see [[supplement-evaluation]])
- Limit trans fats
- Saturated fat in moderation

## Example Calculations

### Cutting Example

**Person:** 180 lb, 2000 calorie target

1. **Protein:** 180 × 1.0 = 180g (720 cal)
2. **Remaining:** 2000 - 720 = 1280 cal
3. **Fat:** 25% of total = 500 cal = 55g
4. **Carbs:** Remaining = 780 cal = 195g

**Macros:** 180P / 195C / 55F

### Gaining Example

**Person:** 180 lb, 2800 calorie target

1. **Protein:** 180 × 0.8 = 144g (576 cal)
2. **Remaining:** 2800 - 576 = 2224 cal
3. **Fat:** 25% of total = 700 cal = 78g
4. **Carbs:** Remaining = 1524 cal = 381g

**Macros:** 144P / 381C / 78F

## Making Adjustments

When weight loss stalls, adjust carbs and/or fat:

| Adjustment Size | Carbs | Fat | Combined |
|-----------------|-------|-----|----------|
| ~100 kcal | 25g | 10g | 15g C, 5g F |
| ~150 kcal | 40g | 15g | 25g C, 5g F |
| ~200 kcal | 50g | 20g | 25g C, 10g F |

> "Feel free to reduce carbs or fat, or both, whichever you prefer, but be careful to not go below the daily minimums."

**Important:** Round to nearest 5g. Don't make <5g changes.

> "Don't make less than 5g macro changes, it gives clients the impression they should be that precise and that <5g changes matter, which encourages unnecessary neuroticism."

## Fiber Recommendations

**Daily Target:** ~14g per 1000 calories consumed

**Benefits:**
- Satiety (feeling full)
- Blood sugar regulation
- Digestive health
- Nutrient absorption

**Sources:**
- Vegetables
- Fruits
- Whole grains
- Legumes
