# Micronutrients and Water

## Source
- **Book**: The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Nutrition
- **Author**: Eric Helms, PhD
- **Context**: Level 3 of the Nutrition Pyramid

## Overview

Micronutrients are nutrients needed in small amounts (milligrams or less) that play essential roles in health, performance, and body composition. While needed in tiny amounts, deficiencies can significantly impact results.

## Macro vs. Micro

> "Macro means big and micro means small. Thus, you need macronutrients in much greater amounts compared to micronutrients."

- **Macronutrients:** Measured in grams (100s per day)
- **Micronutrients:** Measured in milligrams or micrograms

## Categories of Micronutrients

### Minerals (Inorganic)

**Macrominerals** (needed in larger amounts):
- Calcium
- Phosphorus
- Potassium
- Sulfur
- Sodium
- Chlorine
- Magnesium

> "Many of those are the electrolytes, which tie into our fluid intake and balance."

**Trace Minerals** (needed in smaller amounts):
- Iron
- Copper
- Cobalt
- Zinc
- Molybdenum
- Iodine
- Selenium

### Vitamins (Organic)

**Fat-Soluble (ADEK):**
- Vitamin A
- Vitamin D
- Vitamin E
- Vitamin K

**Characteristics:**
- Absorbed with dietary fat via small intestine
- Stay in body longer
- Easier to overdose (stay in system)
- Harder to become deficient (stored)

**Water-Soluble (B vitamins + C):**
- 8 forms of Vitamin B
- Vitamin C

**Characteristics:**
- Rapidly excreted through sweat and urine
- Need daily replenishment
- Harder to overdose (excreted)
- Easier to become deficient (high turnover)

## The "Inclusive" vs. "Exclusive" Mindset

### The Problem with Exclusion

> "The traditional approach to dieting of focusing on 'clean' foods and avoiding 'dirty' foods popularized by bodybuilders, which supposedly increases the micronutrient density of a diet, can often, in fact, be so restrictive as to leave out key nutrients and result in micronutrient deficiencies."

Even "clean eating" diets often lack key micronutrients due to limited food variety.

### The Solution: Inclusion

Focus on ADDING micronutrient-rich foods rather than excluding "bad" foods.

> "You want to make sure you include foods high in micronutrients rather than trying to exclude foods that aren't high in them."

## Common Deficiencies in Bodybuilders

### Historical Data (1980s-1990s Surveys)

Most commonly reported deficiencies:
1. **Vitamin D**
2. **Calcium**
3. **Zinc**
4. **Magnesium**
5. **Iron**

### Zinc Deficiency Case Study

> "Zinc deficiencies can result in a downregulation of thyroid to the point where energy expenditure is substantially reduced. In one case study, one subject increased their resting metabolic rate (RMR) by 194 kcals after four months of supplementing with zinc, and the other subject increased RMR by a whopping 527 kcals after supplementing with zinc for two months."

**Lesson:** Micronutrient deficiencies can dramatically impact metabolism and fat loss.

## Deficiency Considerations

### When Dieting

**Increased Risk Because:**
- Less total food = fewer micronutrients
- Restricted food choices
- Higher physiological stress

**Recommendation:**
- Consider basic multivitamin as "safety net"
- Focus on nutrient-dense food choices
- More variety, not less

### When Gaining

**Lower Risk Because:**
- More total food = more micronutrients
- Can include wider variety

**Still Important:**
- Don't fill calories with "empty" foods
- Maintain whole food base

## Practical Recommendations

### Fruit and Vegetable Intake

**General Guideline:**
- Minimum 5 servings combined per day
- Mix of colors for variety of nutrients
- Include both cooked and raw options

**When Cutting:**
- Vegetables help with fullness
- Choose lower-calorie options
- Watch calorie-dense fruits

**When Gaining:**
- Can include more calorie-dense options
- Still important for health

### Supplementation Approach

> "A multivitamin should not be seen as an excuse to eat a micronutrient poor diet."

**Food First:**
> "Foods are much more complex compounds than simply having macro and micronutrients. Plant-based foods contain phytonutrients and animal-based foods contain zoonutrients; nutrients not classified as vitamins or minerals, but have beneficial effects for health."

**When to Supplement:**
- During aggressive diets
- With restricted food choices
- When blood work shows deficiencies
- Not as replacement for food variety

**What to Use:**
- Basic one-a-day multivitamin
- NOT mega-dosed bodybuilding vitamins
- High-quality, third-party tested

> "I don't really like massive bodybuilding vitamins that are either giant horse pills or a large packet of pills that you're supposed to take on a daily basis."

### Blood Work

> "I would highly recommend getting blood work done before consuming in any vitamin or mineral supplement to ensure you aren't potentially providing micronutrients in excess or neglecting an unknown deficiency."

## Hydration (Water Intake)

### Why It Matters

- Performance
- Recovery
- Appetite regulation
- Nutrient transport
- Temperature regulation

### Guidelines

**General Recommendations:**
- Monitor urine color (pale yellow = well hydrated)
- Increase with activity and heat
- Include water from food sources

**Signs of Dehydration:**
- Dark urine
- Thirst (already mildly dehydrated)
- Fatigue
- Decreased performance

### Special Considerations

**Athletes:**
- Higher needs due to sweat loss
- Pre, during, and post-workout hydration
- Electrolyte consideration for extended exercise

**Dieting:**
- May help with satiety
- Can confuse thirst for hunger
- No need to overdo it

## The Bigger Picture

### Hierarchy Reminder

Level 3 is less important than Levels 1 and 2, but still matters:

> "These nutrients that we only need in tiny amounts can make a big difference in general health, gym performance, hunger levels, mental state, and overall ability to put on muscle if we happen to have deficiencies in them."

### Integration with Macros

Good macro tracking naturally leads to decent micronutrient intake if:
- Protein comes from varied sources
- Carbs include whole grains, fruits, vegetables
- Fats include nuts, seeds, fish, olive oil
