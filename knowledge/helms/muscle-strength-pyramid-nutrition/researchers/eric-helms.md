## Summary

Eric Helms, PhD, CSCS, is the lead author of The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Nutrition (and Training). He is a researcher, coach, natural bodybuilder, and educator known for evidence-based approaches to physique and strength sports.

## Key Contributions

### The Muscle and Strength Pyramids

Two-book comprehensive system:

**Nutrition Pyramid (5 Levels):**
1. Energy Balance (foundation)
2. Macronutrients and Fiber
3. Micronutrients and Water
4. Nutrient Timing and Frequency
5. Supplementation (top)

**Training Pyramid (5 Levels):**
1. Adherence (foundation)
2. Volume, Intensity, Frequency
3. Progression
4. Exercise Selection
5. Rest Periods (top)

### Philosophy and Approach

From the Preface:
> "I typically can tell if someone has some deep-rooted misconceptions about nutrition based on the questions they ask me. Very commonly in the fitness field, you will be asked questions like, 'Should I take the yolks out when I eat eggs?' or 'Is bread bad?' Well, that's kind of missing the whole point."

Key philosophical stance:
> "There's no such thing as a good or bad food, but there is such a thing as a good or bad diet."

### The Recovery Diet

Developed (with Jeff Alberts) as alternative to reverse dieting:
- Immediate exit from deficit post-competition
- Intentional weight gain (5-10% above stage weight)
- 4-8 week recovery period
- Then use reverse diet principles from healthy baseline
