# The Nutrition Pyramid Framework

## Source
- **Book**: The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Nutrition
- **Author**: Eric Helms, PhD
- **Context**: Core organizational framework for nutrition priorities

## Overview

The Nutrition Pyramid is a 5-level hierarchy that organizes nutritional concepts in order of decreasing importance. Each level provides foundational knowledge for subsequent levels.

## The Five Levels

```
          ┌─────────────────┐
          │ 05 SUPPLEMENTS  │  ← Least Important
          ├─────────────────┤
          │ 04 NUTRIENT     │
          │    TIMING       │
          ├─────────────────┤
          │ 03 MICRO-       │
          │    NUTRIENTS    │
          ├─────────────────┤
          │ 02 MACRO-       │
          │    NUTRIENTS    │
          ├─────────────────┤
          │ 01 ENERGY       │  ← Most Important
          │    BALANCE      │
          └─────────────────┘
       BEHAVIOR AND LIFESTYLE
```

## Level Details

### Level 1: Energy Balance (Foundation)

The most important factor determining body composition changes.

**Key Components:**
- Determining maintenance calories
- Setting appropriate caloric deficit/surplus
- Rates of weight change:
  - Fat loss: 0.5-1% body weight per week
  - Muscle gain: 0.5-1.5% body weight per month

**Finding Maintenance:**
1. **Preferred Method**: Track weight and food for 2 weeks, calculate relationship
2. **Calculation Method**: Body weight (lbs) × 10 × activity multiplier

> "If you've already screwed up Level 1, you will inevitably have already screwed up your macronutrient distribution as well."

### Level 2: Macronutrients and Fiber

Once calories are set, determine where those calories come from.

**Protein** (calculated per body weight):
- Dieting: 0.7-1.2 g/lb (1.6-2.7 g/kg)
- Gaining: Similar or slightly lower end

**Carbohydrate and Fat** (calculated as % of remaining calories):
- Scaled to energy allotment since primary role is energy provision
- Minimums: Fat ≥0.25 g/lb; Carbs ≥0.5 g/lb

**Fiber**: Health and nutrient absorption

### Level 3: Micronutrients and Water

The small nutrients that make a big difference.

**Minerals:**
- Macrominerals: calcium, phosphorus, potassium, sulfur, sodium, chlorine, magnesium
- Trace minerals: iron, copper, cobalt, zinc, molybdenum, iodine, selenium

**Vitamins:**
- Fat-soluble (ADEK): Stay in body longer, harder to be deficient
- Water-soluble (B vitamins, C): Need daily replenishment

**Hydration**: Fluid intake recommendations

### Level 4: Nutrient Timing and Frequency

How to distribute calories over different time periods.

**Components:**
- Diet breaks (planned increases to maintenance)
- Single-day refeeds
- Multi-day refeeds and carb cycling
- Meal frequency
- Peri-workout nutrition

### Level 5: Supplementation (Top)

Least important level - not necessary for success.

> "The vast majority of supplements on the market don't do anything except burn a hole in your wallet."

**Evaluation Criteria:**
1. Quality (third-party testing)
2. Validity (scientific evidence)
3. Effectiveness (relevant to your goals)

## Key Principles

### Respect the Hierarchy

> "Too often, information in Level 4 or 5 is delivered without context in the fitness world and this creates a lot of confusion."

Each level builds on the previous. Optimizing Level 4 is meaningless if Level 1 is wrong.

### The Cart Before the Horse

Common mistake: Focusing on details before understanding fundamentals.

**Example**: Asking "Is bread bad?" without considering energy balance and macros.

### Context Dependency

Different goals require different precision:
- Contest prep bodybuilder → High precision needed
- Offseason athlete → More flexibility acceptable
- Recreational lifter → Even more flexibility

## Practical Application

### For Dieting

1. Set appropriate caloric deficit (Level 1)
2. Prioritize protein to preserve muscle (Level 2)
3. Ensure micronutrient adequacy with food choices (Level 3)
4. Consider strategic refeeds if needed (Level 4)
5. Optional evidence-based supplements (Level 5)

### For Gaining

1. Set appropriate caloric surplus (Level 1)
2. Adequate protein, fill rest with carbs/fat (Level 2)
3. Eat varied whole foods for micronutrients (Level 3)
4. Distribute protein throughout day (Level 4)
5. Creatine if not getting from food (Level 5)
