# Three-Tiered Tracking System

## Source
- **Book**: The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Nutrition
- **Author**: Eric Helms, PhD
- **Context**: Flexible approach to dietary tracking that avoids binary thinking

## Overview

The Three-Tiered System provides a non-binary approach to tracking nutrition, allowing for varying levels of precision based on circumstances while avoiding the "all-or-nothing" mentality that leads to diet failure.

## The Problem with Binary Approaches

Traditional approaches force a choice:
1. **IIFYM**: Hit exact macros every day forever
2. **Meal Plans**: Follow rigid food prescriptions exactly

Both promote black-and-white thinking:
- "Either I hit my macros or I didn't"
- "Either I'm on my diet or off it"

> "In a culture where 'if it fits your macros' or 'follow a meal plan and eat clean all the time' are often presented as the only two ways of doing things, it seems like you're stuck with two rigid options."

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1: GOOD (Calories Only)

**Track**: Total calories within ±100 kcal

**Use When:**
- Can't effectively track macros
- Eating out frequently
- Social situations
- Travel

**Why It Works:**
- Maintains energy balance (Level 1 - most important)
- Allows maximum flexibility
- Still achieves body composition goals

### Tier 2: BETTER (Protein + Calories)

**Track**:
- Protein within target range
- Calories within ±100 kcal

**Use When:**
- Offseason tracking (if needed)
- Post-contest recovery
- Non-competitive cuts
- Mini cuts
- Diet breaks

**Protein Ranges:**
- ±5g for precision needs
- ±10g for general tracking

**Why It Works:**
- Ensures muscle preservation (protein priority)
- Maintains energy balance
- More flexible than full macro tracking

### Tier 3: BEST (Full Macros)

**Track**: All macros within specified ranges

**Use When:**
- Contest prep
- Cutting to make weight class
- Final stages of competitive dieting

**Ranges:**
- ±5g if deep into final prep stages
- ±10g for early prep or weight class cutting

## Application Logic

### If your goal requires a certain level, higher levels are always acceptable

Example for contest prep (normally "Best"):
- Most days: Aim for "Best" (full macros)
- Social events: "Better" is acceptable
- Emergencies: "Good" is still a win

### Dropping Tiers is NOT Failure

> "With a binary approach, a slight deviation results in 'falling off the diet', but a flexible approach allows for other outcomes."

A controlled tier drop is infinitely better than abandoning the diet entirely.

## Borrowing System

Companion concept that adds weekly flexibility.

### How It Works

1. Take up to 20% of macros/calories from one day
2. Add them to another day(s) within the week

### Example

Daily goal: 2500 kcal

Weekly distribution could be:
- Day 1: 3000 kcal (+500)
- Day 2: 2000 kcal (-500)
- Day 3: 2700 kcal (+200)
- Day 4: 2300 kcal (-200)
- Days 5-7: 2500 kcal each

**Result**: Weekly average of 2500 kcal achieved

### When to Use

- Planned events (dinners, celebrations)
- Variable hunger days
- Higher activity days
- Recovery from unexpected overages

### Tracking with Borrowing

Set up an Excel sheet that calculates:
- Daily intake
- 7-day rolling average
- Comparison to target

> "So long as you averaged that over the 7-day period, you've met your goal for the week."

## Practical Scenarios

### Scenario: Work Dinner (During Contest Prep)

**Without tiers**: "I can't track, so I'll just binge"

**With tiers**:
1. Estimate calories conservatively
2. Prioritize protein (grilled meat, etc.)
3. Drop to "Better" tier for the day
4. Resume "Best" next day

### Scenario: Travel Day

**Without tiers**: "Can't weigh food, diet ruined"

**With tiers**:
1. Use "Good" tier (calorie estimation only)
2. Make conservative choices
3. Resume normal tracking when home

### Scenario: Alcohol Consumption

When alcohol is consumed, you automatically shift:
- Cannot track "alcohol macros"
- Default to "Better" (protein + calories) or "Good" (calories only)

Example:
- Two beers = ~400 kcal
- Daily budget: 2630 kcal
- Remaining: 2230 kcal for protein/carbs/fat
- Hit protein target, stay within calorie range

## Key Principles

### 1. Progress Over Perfection

> "It's not either a zero or a one, black or white, on or off. Rather, you have a continuum."

### 2. Match Precision to Context

| Situation | Recommended Tier |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Final prep (2 weeks out) | Best (±5g) |
| Early prep | Best (±10g) |
| Weight class cut | Best/Better |
| Diet break | Better |
| Offseason | Better/Good |
| Maintenance | Good or habits |
| Social events | Good |

### 3. Sustainability Over Optimality

Short-term "optimal" tracking that causes burnout is worse than slightly less precise tracking maintained long-term.
